Key Topics & Course Structure

This accelerated 6-week course ensures mastery of essential Algebra II topics through focused instruction and extensive practice, including ~500 curated problems.

  • Equations and Inequalities (Linear, Quadratic, Rational, Radical, Absolute Value)
  • Functions and Their Graphs (Types, Properties, Transformations)
  • Polynomial and Rational Functions (Operations, Factoring, Graphing, Solving)
  • Exponential and Logarithmic Functions (Properties, Equations, Applications)
  • Systems of Equations and Inequalities (Linear, Nonlinear, Matrices)
  • Sequences and Series (Arithmetic, Geometric, Sigma Notation, Recursion)
  • Probability and Statistics (Counting Methods, Distributions)
  • Trigonometry (Right Triangle, Unit Circle, Graphs, Identities)
  • Conic Sections (Parabolas, Ellipses, Circles, Hyperbolas)
  • Complex Numbers (Operations, Solving Equations)

Typical Session Structure

  • Concept Review & Clarification: Ensuring foundational understanding.
  • Guided Problem-Solving: Working through examples step-by-step.
  • Interactive Practice: Applying concepts with instructor feedback.
  • Strategy Discussion: Focusing on efficient and effective solving techniques.
  • Q&A and Support: Addressing individual questions.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is this different from the school's Algebra II course?

This course is accelerated, covering material more rapidly and deeply. The small group size allows for highly personalized attention. We focus heavily on problem-solving strategies and conceptual understanding, often using problems relevant to math competitions and standardized tests, which may go beyond a standard curriculum.

What is the expected homework/study time commitment?

Expect approximately 2-4 hours of work outside of the live sessions per week. This includes practice problems, reviewing concepts, and potentially short quizzes. Consistent effort is key in an accelerated format.

How is student progress monitored?

Progress is monitored through active participation in sessions, completion of practice assignments, and periodic assessments. Feedback is provided regularly, and the small class setting allows the instructor to closely track each student's understanding.

What happens if a student misses a session?

While live attendance is highly encouraged for maximum benefit, sessions are recorded (with consent) for review. Students are responsible for catching up on missed content, and support is available via the dedicated WhatsApp group.

Is this course suitable for students who struggled with Algebra I?

The course assumes a solid understanding of Algebra I concepts. While key ideas are briefly reviewed, the pace is accelerated. Students who had significant difficulty might consider a standard-paced course. Please review the prerequisites or contact us to discuss your situation.
