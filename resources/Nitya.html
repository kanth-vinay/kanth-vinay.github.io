Applications of Sines, Cosines, and Triangle Area (Set 2)

1. A hiker leaves base camp (Point C) and walks 5 km at a bearing of N 45° E to reach a checkpoint (Point A). From the checkpoint, the hiker turns and walks 7 km at a bearing of S 15° E to reach a lake (Point B). How far is the lake from the base camp? Round to the nearest tenth of a km.
Diagram Setup: Draw a N-S line at C and at A. The bearing from A back to C is S 45° W. The new bearing is S 15° E. The angle between these two paths inside the triangle at Point A is 45° + 15° = 60°.
Method: Law of Cosines (SAS).
Calculation: CB² = 5² + 7² - 2(5)(7)cos(60°)
CB² = 25 + 49 - 70(0.5) = 74 - 35 = 39
CB = √39 ≈ 6.2
Final Answer: 6.2 km
2. An observer on a flat plain looks up at a hovering hot air balloon. The angle of elevation is 40°. The observer drives 100 meters directly toward the balloon's position. From the new location, the angle of elevation to the balloon is 55°. Find the height of the balloon to the nearest tenth of a meter.
Diagram Setup: This creates an obtuse triangle formed by the two observation points and the balloon. The interior angles are 40°, 125° (supplement of 55°), and 15° (180° - 125° - 40°). The base is 100 m.
Method: Law of Sines to find the hypotenuse, then right triangle trigonometry (Sine).
Calculation: Let hyp be the hypotenuse from the second point.
hyp / sin(40°) = 100 / sin(15°) → hyp ≈ 248.37 m.
Height = 248.37 × sin(55°) ≈ 203.4
Final Answer: 203.4 meters
3. A triangular commercial plot is bordered by two avenues that measure 80 meters and 110 meters. The angle of intersection between the two avenues is 85°. What is the area of the commercial plot? Round to the nearest square meter.
Diagram Setup: A standard triangle with two known sides and an included angle (SAS).
Method: Area = ½ × a × b × sin(C)
Calculation: Area = 0.5 × 80 × 110 × sin(85°)
Area = 4400 × 0.9962 ≈ 4383.2
Final Answer: 4,383 square meters
4. Three small towns (A, B, and C) form a triangle. Town A is 10 miles from Town B. Town B is 12 miles from Town C. Town A is 15 miles from Town C. A regional planner needs to find the measure of the angle formed at Town B. Find this angle to the nearest tenth of a degree.
Diagram Setup: A triangle with side lengths 10, 12, and 15. The angle at B is opposite the side measuring 15 miles.
Method: Law of Cosines (SSS to find an angle).
Calculation: 15² = 10² + 12² - 2(10)(12)cos(B)
225 = 100 + 144 - 240cos(B) → -19 = -240cos(B) → cos(B) = 19/240
B = arccos(19/240) ≈ 85.5°
Final Answer: 85.5°
5. Two coast guard stations, Station A and Station B, are 20 miles apart on a straight coastline. A distress signal from a ship is detected. From Station A, the signal is at an angle of 50° relative to the coastline. From Station B, the signal is at an angle of 75° relative to the coastline. How far is the ship from Station A? Round to the nearest tenth of a mile.
Diagram Setup: A triangle with two known angles at the base (50° and 75°) and a known base length of 20 miles (ASA). The third angle at the ship is 180° - 50° - 75° = 55°.
Method: Law of Sines. The distance to Station A is opposite the 75° angle.
Calculation: d_A / sin(75°) = 20 / sin(55°)
d_A = 20 × sin(75°) / sin(55°) ≈ 20 × 0.9659 / 0.8192 ≈ 23.58
Final Answer: 23.6 miles
6. Two cargo ships leave a port at exactly the same time. Ship 1 sails at a bearing of 030° (N 30° E) at a speed of 20 knots (nautical miles per hour). Ship 2 sails at a bearing of 140° (S 40° E) at a speed of 25 knots. How far apart are the two ships after 3 hours? Round to the nearest tenth of a nautical mile.
Diagram Setup: The distances after 3 hours are 60 nm and 75 nm. The angle between their paths is the difference in bearings: 140° - 30° = 110°.
Method: Law of Cosines (SAS).
Calculation: d² = 60² + 75² - 2(60)(75)cos(110°)
d² = 3600 + 5625 - 9000(-0.3420) ≈ 9225 + 3078 = 12303
d = √12303 ≈ 110.9
Final Answer: 110.9 nautical miles
7. A triangular courtyard is designated to be paved with decorative stone. The three sides of the courtyard measure 30 feet, 40 feet, and 60 feet. Calculate the total area that needs to be paved. Round to the nearest whole square foot.
Diagram Setup: A triangle with all three sides known (SSS).
Method: Heron's Formula.
Calculation: Semi-perimeter (s) = (30 + 40 + 60) / 2 = 65.
Area = √(65(65-30)(65-40)(65-60)) = √(65 × 35 × 25 × 5) = √284375 ≈ 533.27
Final Answer: 533 square feet
8. A metal gusset plate is structured as a parallelogram. The two adjacent sides of the plate measure 15 cm and 22 cm, and one of the interior angles is 125°. To reinforce the structure, a steel rod is welded across the longer diagonal. Find the length of this rod to the nearest tenth of a cm.
Diagram Setup: A parallelogram split into two triangles by a diagonal. The longer diagonal is opposite the obtuse interior angle (125°).
Method: Law of Cosines (SAS).
Calculation: x² = 15² + 22² - 2(15)(22)cos(125°)
x² = 225 + 484 - 660(-0.5736) ≈ 1087.56
x = √1087.56 ≈ 32.97
Final Answer: 33.0 cm
9. A tall pine tree leans away from a park ranger, forming an 85° angle with the level ground. The ranger stands 60 feet from the base of the tree, on the side from which it is leaning. From the ranger's position, the angle of elevation to the top of the tree is 42°. How tall is the tree? Round to the nearest tenth of a foot.
Diagram Setup: A triangle formed by the tree, the ground, and the line of sight. Because the tree leans away from the ranger, the interior angle at the base of the tree is obtuse: 180° - 85° = 95°. The angle of elevation at the ranger's position is 42°. The third angle (at the top of the tree) is 180° - 95° - 42° = 43°.
Method: Law of Sines.
Calculation: Tree / sin(42°) = 60 / sin(43°)
Tree = 60 × sin(42°) / sin(43°) ≈ 60 × 0.6691 / 0.6820 ≈ 58.86
Final Answer: 58.9 feet
10. An architect is attempting to measure the height of a historic monument from a distance. The initial angle of elevation to the top of the monument is 32°. The architect then walks 150 feet directly toward the monument and measures the new angle of elevation to be 48°. Calculate the height of the monument. Round to the nearest foot.
Diagram Setup: An obtuse triangle is formed by the two observation points and the top of the monument. The interior angles are 32°, 132° (180° - 48°), and 16° (180° - 132° - 32°). The base between the two points is 150 ft.
Method: Law of Sines to find the hypotenuse from the second point, then Sine to find the vertical height.
Calculation: Let hyp be the hypotenuse extending from the second point.
hyp / sin(32°) = 150 / sin(16°) → hyp ≈ 288.4 ft.
Height = 288.4 × sin(48°) ≈ 214.3
Final Answer: 214 feet
