Unit 1: Exponents

An exponent represents repeated multiplication. In the expression \( b^n \), \( b \) is the base and \( n \) is the exponent (or power).

1.1 The Laws of Exponents

Fundamental Rules
Product Rule\( x^a \cdot x^b = x^{a+b} \)
Quotient Rule\( \frac{x^a}{x^b} = x^{a-b} \)
Power Rule\( (x^a)^b = x^{a \cdot b} \)
Zero Exponent\( x^0 = 1 \) (if \(x \neq 0\))
Negative Exponent\( x^{-n} = \frac{1}{x^n} \)

Concept Check

  • 1. Product Rule: Simplify \( (2x^3)(4x^5) \).
    Multiply coefficients, add exponents: \( 2 \cdot 4 = 8 \) and \( 3+5=8 \).
    Result: \( 8x^8 \)

1.2 Scientific Notation

Used to express very large or very small numbers. Format: \( a \times 10^n \), where \( 1 \le |a| < 10 \).

Large Number
\( 4,500,000 = 4.5 \times 10^6 \)
Small Number
\( 0.00032 = 3.2 \times 10^{-4} \)

Unit 2: Radicals

A radical expression, denoted by \( \sqrt[n]{x} \), asks "what number multiplied by itself \( n \) times equals \( x \)?".

2.1 Simplifying Radicals

Properties
Product Property\( \sqrt{ab} = \sqrt{a} \cdot \sqrt{b} \)
Quotient Property\( \sqrt{\frac{a}{b}} = \frac{\sqrt{a}}{\sqrt{b}} \)

Concept Check

  • 1. Simplify \( \sqrt{50} \)
    Factor 50 into a perfect square: \( 50 = 25 \cdot 2 \).
    \( \sqrt{25 \cdot 2} = \sqrt{25}\sqrt{2} = 5\sqrt{2} \).

B. Written Response (FRQ Style)

Conceptual
Q9. Explain why \( x^0 = 1 \) using the Quotient Rule of Exponents.
Using the Quotient Rule, \( \frac{x^n}{x^n} = x^{n-n} = x^0 \).
However, any number divided by itself is 1 (e.g., \( \frac{5}{5}=1 \)).
Therefore, \( x^0 \) must equal 1.
Error Analysis
Q10. A student simplifies \( \sqrt{x^2 + 16} \) as \( x + 4 \). Explain the error.
The student incorrectly distributed the square root over addition. Roots only distribute over multiplication and division.
Counter-example: \( \sqrt{9+16} = \sqrt{25} = 5 \), but \( \sqrt{9}+\sqrt{16} = 3+4 = 7 \).

Q11. Simplify \( \left( \frac{x^{-2}y^3}{x^3y^{-4}} \right)^{-2} \)
Inside first: \( \frac{y^7}{x^5} \).
Then power -2: \( \frac{y^{-14}}{x^{-10}} \).

Equation
Q12. If \( 2^{20} + 2^{20} = 2^x \), find \( x \).
\( 2(2^{20}) = 2^{1} \cdot 2^{20} = 2^{21} \).
So \( x = 21 \).
Comparison
Q13. Which is larger: \( 3^{75} \) or \( 5^{50} \)?
Reduce powers by GCD (25).
\( 3^{75} = (3^3)^{25} = 27^{25} \).
\( 5^{50} = (5^2)^{25} = 25^{25} \).
Since \( 27 > 25 \), \( 3^{75} \) is larger.
Nested Roots
Q14. Solve \( \sqrt{x\sqrt{x}} = 8 \)
Rewrite as exponents: \( (x \cdot x^{1/2})^{1/2} = (x^{3/2})^{1/2} = x^{3/4} \).
\( x^{3/4} = 8 \Rightarrow x = 8^{4/3} = (\sqrt[3]{8})^4 = 2^4 = 16 \).

Unit 3: Rational Exponents

This is the bridge between Exponents and Radicals. We can express roots as fractional powers.

The Definition
\( x^{\frac{m}{n}} = \sqrt[n]{x^m} \)
  • The Numerator (m) is the Power.
  • The Denominator (n) is the Root.

Unit 4: Logarithms

A logarithm is simply an exponent in disguise.

4.1 Definition & Properties

\( b^y = x \iff \log_b(x) = y \)
Product\( \log_b(mn) = \log_b m + \log_b n \)
Quotient\( \log_b(\frac{m}{n}) = \log_b m - \log_b n \)
Power\( \log_b(m^p) = p \log_b m \)

4.2 The Inverse Relationship

Exponential and Logarithmic functions are inverses. They "undo" each other. This is the key to solving equations.

The "Undo" Properties:

Log "Undoes" Exponent
\( \log_b(b^x) = x \)
(Take log base b of both sides)
Exponent "Undoes" Log
\( b^{\log_b(x)} = x \)
(Raise base b to both sides)

Unit 5: Solving Equations

5.1 Exponential Equations

Use logs to solve for exponents when bases don't match. Specifically, we apply the inverse property.

Example: Solve \( 5^x = 12 \)
  1. Isolate the exponential term (already done).
  2. Take the log of both sides (usually \( \ln \) or \( \log \)).
    \( \ln(5^x) = \ln(12) \)
  3. Use the Power Property to move \( x \) down.
    \( x \cdot \ln(5) = \ln(12) \)
  4. Divide to isolate \( x \).
    \( x = \frac{\ln(12)}{\ln(5)} \approx 1.54 \)

5.2 Logarithmic Equations

Condense logs into a single term, then "exponentiate" both sides to remove the log.

Example: Solve \( \log_2(x) = 3 \)
  • Exponentiate base 2:
    \( 2^{\log_2(x)} = 2^3 \)
  • Inverse property applies:
    \( x = 8 \)

Continuous Calc

\( P=1000, r=0.05, t=10 \).
\( A = 1000e^{(0.05)(10)} = 1000e^{0.5} \).

Solving for Time
Q9. How long for \$500 to double at 6% continuous interest?
\( 1000 = 500e^{0.06t} \Rightarrow 2 = e^{0.06t} \).
Take ln: \( \ln(2) = 0.06t \).
\( t = \frac{\ln(2)}{0.06} \approx 11.55 \) years.
Solving for Rate
Q10. You want \$100 to become \$150 in 5 years (compounded annually). Find r.
\( 150 = 100(1+r)^5 \Rightarrow 1.5 = (1+r)^5 \).
Root both sides: \( (1.5)^{1/5} = 1+r \).
\( r \approx 1.084 - 1 = 0.084 \) or 8.4%.

C. The Gauntlet (Rigorous / AoPS Style)

Log Eqn
Q11. Solve \( \log_2(x) + \log_2(x-6) = 4 \)
Condense: \( \log_2(x^2-6x) = 4 \).
Exponentiate: \( x^2 - 6x = 16 \).
Quadratic: \( x^2 - 6x - 16 = 0 \Rightarrow (x-8)(x+2)=0 \).
Reject -2 (domain). Answer: \( x=8 \).
Substitution
Q12. Solve \( e^{2x} - 4e^x - 5 = 0 \)
Let \( u = e^x \). Then \( u^2 - 4u - 5 = 0 \).
\( (u-5)(u+1) = 0 \). So \( e^x = 5 \) or \( e^x = -1 \).
\( e^x \) cannot be negative. \( x = \ln(5) \).
System
Q13. Solve the system:
\( 2^x \cdot 4^y = 64 \)
\( \log_x(y) = 2 \)
Eq 1: \( 2^x \cdot 2^{2y} = 2^6 \Rightarrow x+2y=6 \).
Eq 2: \( x^2 = y \).
Sub \( y \) into Eq 1: \( x + 2x^2 = 6 \Rightarrow 2x^2 + x - 6 = 0 \).
Factors: \( (2x-3)(x+2)=0 \).
Base \( x \) must be positive, so \( x = 1.5 \). \( y = 2.25 \).
Change of Base
Q14. Simplify \( \log_3(4) \cdot \log_4(5) \cdot \dots \cdot \log_{80}(81) \)
Change all to ln: \( \frac{\ln 4}{\ln 3} \cdot \frac{\ln 5}{\ln 4} \dots \frac{\ln 81}{\ln 80} \).
Telescoping product. Only \( \frac{\ln 81}{\ln 3} \) remains.
\( \log_3(81) = 4 \).
