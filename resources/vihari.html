Unit 1: Exponents

An exponent represents repeated multiplication. In the expression \( b^n \), \( b \) is the base and \( n \) is the exponent (or power).

1.1 The Laws of Exponents

Fundamental Rules
Product Rule\( x^a \cdot x^b = x^{a+b} \)
Quotient Rule\( \frac{x^a}{x^b} = x^{a-b} \)
Power Rule\( (x^a)^b = x^{a \cdot b} \)
Zero Exponent\( x^0 = 1 \) (if \(x \neq 0\))
Negative Exponent\( x^{-n} = \frac{1}{x^n} \)

1.2 Scientific Notation & Scale

Format: \( a \times 10^n \), where \( 1 \le |a| < 10 \). This is crucial for physics and chemistry.

Large Number
\( 4,500,000 = 4.5 \times 10^6 \)
Small Number
\( 0.00032 = 3.2 \times 10^{-4} \)

Level 2: Analysis & Application

  • conceptual. Explain why \( -5^2 \) is different from \( (-5)^2 \).
    Order of Operations (PEMDAS).
    In \( -5^2 \), the exponent applies only to the 5. Result: \( -(25) = -25 \).
    In \( (-5)^2 \), the negative is inside the group being squared. Result: \( (-5)(-5) = 25 \).
  • Application. A colony of bacteria doubles every hour. If you start with 100 bacteria, write an expression for the population after \( t \) hours. How many are there after 5 hours?
    Formula: \( P(t) = P_0 \cdot 2^t \)
    \( P(5) = 100 \cdot 2^5 = 100 \cdot 32 = 3,200 \) bacteria.

Level 3: The Gauntlet

Variable Equivalence
Find \( n \) if \( \frac{8^{n-1}}{4^{2n}} = 16 \).
Base 2: \( \frac{(2^3)^{n-1}}{(2^2)^{2n}} = 2^4 \)
\( \frac{2^{3n-3}}{2^{4n}} = 2^4 \Rightarrow 2^{(3n-3) - 4n} = 2^4 \)
\( -n - 3 = 4 \Rightarrow n = -7 \).
System
If \( 3^x = 9^y \) and \( x - y = 4 \), find \( x \).
\( 3^x = (3^2)^y \Rightarrow 3^x = 3^{2y} \Rightarrow x = 2y \).
Sub into Eq 2: \( (2y) - y = 4 \Rightarrow y = 4 \).
Then \( x = 2(4) = 8 \).

Unit 2: Radicals

A radical expression, denoted by \( \sqrt[n]{x} \), asks "what number multiplied by itself \( n \) times equals \( x \)?".

2.1 Simplifying Radicals

Properties
Product Property\( \sqrt{ab} = \sqrt{a} \cdot \sqrt{b} \)
Quotient Property\( \sqrt{\frac{a}{b}} = \frac{\sqrt{a}}{\sqrt{b}} \)

2.2 Rationalizing Denominators

The Golden Rule: Standard mathematical form does not allow radicals in the denominator.

  • Monomial: Multiply top and bottom by the radical.
    \( \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \cdot \frac{\sqrt{2}}{\sqrt{2}} = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \)
  • Binomial: Multiply by the conjugate (\( a + \sqrt{b} \to a - \sqrt{b} \)).
    Denominator becomes rational difference of squares: \( (a+\sqrt{b})(a-\sqrt{b}) = a^2 - b \).

Error Analysis

\( 5 \neq 7 \), so the statement is false.

Unit 3: Rational Exponents

This is the bridge between Exponents and Radicals. We can express roots as fractional powers.

The Definition
\( x^{\frac{m}{n}} = \sqrt[n]{x^m} = (\sqrt[n]{x})^m \)
  • The Numerator (m) is the Power.
  • The Denominator (n) is the Root (Think: roots grow "down" under).

Unit 4: Logarithms

A logarithm is simply an exponent in disguise.

4.1 Definition & Properties

\( b^y = x \iff \log_b(x) = y \)
Product\( \log_b(mn) = \log_b m + \log_b n \)
Quotient\( \log_b(\frac{m}{n}) = \log_b m - \log_b n \)
Power\( \log_b(m^p) = p \log_b m \)

4.2 Deep Dive: Orders of Magnitude

Why do we need logs?

Human perception is often logarithmic. We perceive "scales" of difference rather than linear difference.

  • Richter Scale: A 7.0 earthquake is \( 10 \times \) stronger than a 6.0.
  • Decibels: Every 10dB increase represents a \( 10 \times \) increase in sound intensity.
  • PH Scale: Measures \( H^+ \) concentration in powers of 10.

Domain

Argument must be positive: \( x-5 > 0 \).

True or False: \( \log(x+y) = \log x + \log y \).
False. Logs do not distribute over addition.
Counter-example: \( \log(10+100) = \log(110) \approx 2.04 \).
\( \log(10)+\log(100) = 1+2 = 3 \).
Magnitude

\( \log_3 27 = 3 \).
\( \log_2 10 \): \( 2^3=8, 2^4=16 \). So it is \( 3.something \).

Inverse Functions
Find inverse of \( f(x) = 1 + \log_3 x \).
\( y = 1 + \log_3 x \Rightarrow x = 1 + \log_3 y \).

Unit 5: Solving Equations

The Strategy: Isolate & Invert

  1. Isolate the exponential or logarithmic part. (Get it alone on one side).
  2. Inverse it.
    • If it's \( 5^x = \dots \), take \( \log_5 \) of both sides.
    • If it's \( \log_2(x) = \dots \), exponentiate (make them powers of 2).
  3. Solve for x.
