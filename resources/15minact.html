  • \( \sqrt[n]{x^m} = x^{m/n} \)
  • \( (x^a)^b = x^{ab} \)
  • \( \sqrt[n]{ab} = \sqrt[n]{a}\sqrt[n]{b} \)
  • \( \frac{x^a}{x^b} = x^{a-b} \)
  • \( x^{-n} = \frac{1}{x^n} \)

Directions: Select the best answer for each question. You have 25 minutes. No calculators allowed.

  1. Rationalize the denominator and simplify: \( \frac{3}{\sqrt[4]{27}} \)
    Solution: Convert denom to \( \sqrt[4]{3^3} \). Multiply top/bottom by \( \sqrt[4]{3^1} \). Result: \( \frac{3\sqrt[4]{3}}{\sqrt[4]{3^4}} = \frac{3\sqrt[4]{3}}{3} = \sqrt[4]{3} \).
  2. Simplify completely: \( \left( 8^{2/3} \cdot 4^{-1/2} \right)^{3} \)
    Solution: \( 8^{2/3}=4 \), \( 4^{-1/2}=1/2 \). Product is \( 4 \cdot 0.5 = 2 \). Then \( 2^3 = 8 \).
  3. Simplify into a single radical expression: \( \sqrt[3]{\sqrt{64x^6}} \)
    Solution: Multiply indices: \( \sqrt[6]{64x^6} \). Since \( 2^6=64 \), this simplifies to \( 2x \).
  4. Perform the operation and simplify: \( \frac{\sqrt{x^3}}{\sqrt[3]{x^2}} \)
    Solution: \( x^{3/2} \div x^{2/3} = x^{3/2 - 2/3} = x^{9/6 - 4/6} = x^{5/6} = \sqrt[6]{x^5} \).
  5. Simplify using positive exponents only: \( \left( \frac{x^{-2/5}}{y^{1/2}} \right)^{-10} \)
    Solution: Distribute -10 power: \( x^{(-2/5)(-10)} = x^4 \), \( y^{(1/2)(-10)} = y^{-5} \). Move \( y^{-5} \) to top: \( x^4 y^5 \).
  6. Subtract and simplify: \( \sqrt[3]{54x^4} - x\sqrt[3]{16x} \)
    Solution: \( \sqrt[3]{27\cdot2\cdot x^3\cdot x} = 3x\sqrt[3]{2x} \).
    \( x\sqrt[3]{8\cdot2\cdot x} = 2x\sqrt[3]{2x} \).
    Subtracting gives \( 1x\sqrt[3]{2x} \).
  7. Multiply and simplify: \( \sqrt[4]{x} \cdot \sqrt[3]{x^2} \)
    Solution: \( x^{1/4} \cdot x^{2/3} = x^{3/12 + 8/12} = x^{11/12} = \sqrt[12]{x^{11}} \).
  8. Simplify the nested expression: \( \sqrt{ 9\sqrt[3]{5} + 16\sqrt[3]{5} } \)
    Solution: Inside sum is \( 25\sqrt[3]{5} \). Square rooting yields \( \sqrt{25}\sqrt{\sqrt[3]{5}} = 5 \cdot \sqrt[6]{5} \).
  9. Evaluate without a calculator: \( 27^{-2/3} + 9^{1/2} \)
    Solution: \( 27^{-2/3} = 1/9 \). \( 9^{1/2} = 3 \). Sum \( = 3 + 1/9 = 28/9 \).
  10. Rationalize the denominator: \( \frac{x}{\sqrt[5]{x^2}} \)
    Solution: Multiply top/bottom by \( \sqrt[5]{x^3} \). Denom becomes \( x \). Top is \( x\sqrt[5]{x^3} \). The \( x \)'s cancel, leaving \( \sqrt[5]{x^3} \).
