1. The Limit

The Problem

Imagine this function:

$$ f(x) = \frac{x^2 - 1}{x - 1} $$

What happens if we plug in $x = 1$?

$$ \frac{1^2 - 1}{1 - 1} = \frac{0}{0} \quad \text{(ERROR!)} $$

The Solution

We don't plug it in. We sneak up on it.

x f(x)
0.9 1.9
0.99 1.99
1.0 ???
1.01 2.01
1.1 2.1

It looks like 2.
That's the Limit.

2. The Derivative

The Problem

Algebra says you need two points to find slope:

$$ m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1} $$

Calculus Question:
"What is the slope at this one single point?"

The Solution

Move the second point closer...

Secant Tangent

When distance $\to 0$, Secant $\to$ Tangent.

3. The Integral

The Problem

Geometry has formulas for squares ($L \times W$) and circles ($\pi r^2$).

Calculus Question:
"What is the area of this weird blob?"

The Solution

Slice it into infinite rectangles.

1 Rect Accurate

Integration = Accumulation of slices

Why Do We Learn This?

It's not just for grades. It's for Optimization.

Business

How do I maximize profit while minimizing cost?

Derivatives find the peak.

Engineering

How much fuel does a rocket need to escape gravity?

Integrals calculate the total energy.

Calculus is the math of Change and Prediction.

The "Algebra Trap"

Calculus is 10% Calculus, 90% Algebra.

Why Students Struggle

Most students don't fail because of the calculus concepts (Limits, Derivatives).

They fail because they cannot simplify the algebra afterwards.

"If you can't factor, you can't pass."
