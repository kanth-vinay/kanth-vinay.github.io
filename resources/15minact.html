Part I: Pre-Algebra Fluency
Question 1 Answer: B (-5)
Simplify: \(-8 + 3\)
Solution Logic: This is integer addition. Starting at \(-8\) on the number line, adding a positive number means moving to the right.

Movement: \(-8 \rightarrow -7 \rightarrow -6 \rightarrow -5\).
Alternatively, find the difference between the absolute values \(|8| - |3| = 5\), and keep the sign of the larger number (negative).
Question 2 Answer: C (8)
Evaluate: \(10 - 2 \times 3 + 4\)
Order of Operations (PEMDAS):
  1. Multiplication first: \(2 \times 3 = 6\). Equation becomes \(10 - 6 + 4\).
  2. Addition/Subtraction (Left to Right): \(10 - 6 = 4\). Equation becomes \(4 + 4\).
  3. Final Addition: \(4 + 4 = 8\).
Common Pitfall: Students often add \(3+4\) first before subtracting, which gives the incorrect answer of 3.
Question 3 Answer: A (\(2x + 10\))
Simplify: \(4x + 7 - 2x + 3\)
Combine Like Terms: Group the variable terms and the constant terms separately. $$ (4x - 2x) + (7 + 3) $$ $$ 2x + 10 $$
Question 4 Answer: C (\(3x - 15\))
Equivalent expression to: \(3(x - 5)\)
Distributive Property: Multiply the term outside the parenthesis (3) by each term inside. $$ 3 \cdot x = 3x $$ $$ 3 \cdot (-5) = -15 $$ Result: \(3x - 15\).
Question 5 Answer: B (13)
Evaluate \(2x + 5\) when \(x = 4\)
Substitution: Replace \(x\) with \(4\). Always assume parentheses when substituting. $$ 2(4) + 5 $$ $$ 8 + 5 $$ $$ 13 $$
Part II: Algebra I Concepts
Question 6 Answer: B (29)
Solve: \(x - 9 = 20\)
Inverse Operations: To isolate \(x\), verify what is happening to it (subtraction of 9). Do the inverse (add 9) to both sides. $$ x - 9 + 9 = 20 + 9 $$ $$ x = 29 $$
Question 7 Answer: B (5)
Solve: \(2x + 4 = 14\)
Two-Step Equation: 1. Remove the constant first by subtracting 4 from both sides: $$ 2x = 14 - 4 \Rightarrow 2x = 10 $$ 2. Remove the coefficient by dividing by 2: $$ \frac{2x}{2} = \frac{10}{2} \Rightarrow x = 5 $$
Question 8 Answer: B (5)
Solve: \(5x = 3x + 10\)
Variables on Both Sides: Move the smaller variable term (\(3x\)) to the left side by subtracting it. $$ 5x - 3x = 10 $$ $$ 2x = 10 $$ Divide by 2: $$ x = 5 $$
Question 9 Answer: B (3)
Slope of \(y = 3x + 2\)
Slope-Intercept Form: The standard linear equation is \(y = mx + b\), where \(m\) is the slope and \(b\) is the y-intercept.
Matching \(y = 3x + 2\), we see that \(m = 3\).
Question 10 Answer: A (Open circle, right)
Graph of \(x > 2\)
Inequality Logic: 1. Boundary: The symbol is \(>\) (strictly greater than), not \(\geq\). This means 2 is NOT included, so we use an Open Circle.
2. Direction: We want numbers greater than 2 (like 3, 4, 100). These are to the right on the number line.
Question 11 Answer: C (\(2x + 5 = 15\))
Translation: "Twice a number added to 5 equals 15"
Word Translation:
  • "Twice a number" \(\rightarrow 2x\)
  • "Added to 5" \(\rightarrow 5 + 2x\) (or \(2x + 5\) due to Commutative Property)
  • "Equals 15" \(\rightarrow = 15\)
Result: \(2x + 5 = 15\).
Question 12 Answer: B (\(0, -4\))
y-intercept of \(y = 2x - 4\)
Definition of Intercept: The y-intercept is the point where the line crosses the y-axis, which occurs when \(x = 0\). $$ y = 2(0) - 4 $$ $$ y = -4 $$ Coordinate: \((0, -4)\).
Question 13 Answer: B (\(x^5\))
Simplify \(x^2 \cdot x^3\)
Product Rule of Exponents: When multiplying terms with the same base, you add the exponents. $$ x^2 \cdot x^3 = x^{(2+3)} = x^5 $$ Conceptual Check: \((x \cdot x) \cdot (x \cdot x \cdot x) = x^5\).
Part III: Algebraic Reasoning (Proofs)
Question 14 Answer: B (Subtraction Property)
Reason for: \(x + 8 = 12 \rightarrow x = 4\)
Logic: To get from the first step to the second, the \(+8\) was removed. Mathematically, this is done by subtracting 8 from both sides. Therefore, this is the Subtraction Property of Equality.
Question 15 Answer: B (\(2x + 6 = 14\))
Missing Step 2 in solving \(2(x + 3) = 14\)
Logic Chain: Step 1 is the Given Equation.
Step 2 is justified by the "Distributive Property." We must apply distribution to \(2(x+3)\). $$ 2 \cdot x + 2 \cdot 3 = 14 $$ $$ 2x + 6 = 14 $$ Step 3 confirms this by showing the subtraction of 6 (\(2x = 8\)), which logically follows only if Step 2 was \(2x + 6 = 14\).
