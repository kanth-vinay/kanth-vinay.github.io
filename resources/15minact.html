Extreme Algebra: Linear Analysis

Instructions: These problems are designed to test your ability to think with lines. You will not just calculate answers; you will prove facts, find missing coordinates, and model movement. Always draw a sketch first.

1. The Collinearity Test Coordinate Proof
Points $A(-2, -5)$, $B(1, 4)$, and $C(3, 10)$ are given. Without graphing them, prove whether these three points lie on the same straight line.
Concept: If points are on the same line, the slope between A and B must equal the slope between B and C.
1. Slope $AB$: $m = \frac{4 - (-5)}{1 - (-2)} = \frac{9}{3} = 3$.
2. Slope $BC$: $m = \frac{10 - 4}{3 - 1} = \frac{6}{2} = 3$.
Conclusion: Since the slopes are equal (3), the points are collinear.
2. The Perpendicular Bisector Geometric Construction
Find the equation of the line that cuts the segment connecting $P(2, 4)$ and $Q(8, 12)$ exactly in half at a $90^\circ$ angle.
1. Midpoint: Average of x and y. $M = (\frac{2+8}{2}, \frac{4+12}{2}) = (5, 8)$.
2. Original Slope: $m = \frac{12-4}{8-2} = \frac{8}{6} = \frac{4}{3}$.
3. Perpendicular Slope: Negative reciprocal. $m_{\perp} = -\frac{3}{4}$.
4. Equation: Use point-slope with M(5,8). $y - 8 = -\frac{3}{4}(x - 5)$.
3. The Missing Vertex Vector Logic
Three corners of a parallelogram are $A(1, 1)$, $B(5, 3)$, and $C(6, 8)$. If these points are connected in order ($A \to B \to C \to D \to A$), find the coordinates of the fourth corner, $D$.
Concept: The "jump" from B to A must be the same as the "jump" from C to D (opposite sides are parallel and equal).
1. From B to A: Go left 4 (because $1-5=-4$) and down 2 (because $1-3=-2$).
2. Apply this to C: Start at $(6, 8)$.
3. $x = 6 - 4 = 2$.
4. $y = 8 - 2 = 6$.
Answer: $D(2, 6)$.
4. The Delayed Start Physics Modeling
Car A leaves a city traveling at 60 mph. Two hours later, Car B leaves the same city traveling on the same road at 80 mph. How many hours will Car B drive before it catches Car A?
1. Let $t$ be the time Car B drives.
2. Car B distance: $d = 80t$.
3. Car A has been driving for $t + 2$ hours. Distance: $d = 60(t + 2)$.
4. Equate: $80t = 60t + 120$.
5. $20t = 120 \Rightarrow t = 6$.
Answer: 6 hours.
5. Abstract Parallelism Parametric Algebra
Find the value of $k$ so that the line $kx + 5y = 10$ is parallel to the line $4x - 2y = 9$.
1. Find slope of first line: $5y = -kx + 10 \Rightarrow y = -\frac{k}{5}x + 2$. Slope is $-\frac{k}{5}$.
2. Find slope of second line: $-2y = -4x + 9 \Rightarrow y = 2x - 4.5$. Slope is $2$.
3. Parallel means equal slopes: $-\frac{k}{5} = 2$.
4. Multiply by -5: $k = -10$.
6. The Reflection Geometry Transformation
Point $P$ is at $(4, 1)$. Imagine reflecting this point across the line $y = x$ (the diagonal line). What are the coordinates of the new point $P'$? (Hint: The slope between P and P' must be -1).
Concept: Reflecting over $y=x$ simply swaps the x and y coordinates.
1. Proof: The line connecting $(4,1)$ and $(1,4)$ has a slope of $\frac{4-1}{1-4} = -1$, which is perpendicular to $y=x$ (slope 1).
2. The midpoint is $(2.5, 2.5)$, which lies on $y=x$.
Answer: $P'(1, 4)$.
7. Area by Intercepts Triangle Geometry
A line has the equation $3x + 4y = 24$. This line, along with the x-axis and the y-axis, forms a right triangle. Calculate the area of this triangle.
1. x-intercept (Base): Set $y=0$. $3x = 24 \Rightarrow x = 8$.
2. y-intercept (Height): Set $x=0$. $4y = 24 \Rightarrow y = 6$.
3. Area: $0.5 \times \text{base} \times \text{height}$.
4. $0.5 \times 8 \times 6 = 24$ square units.
8. The Head-On Collision Linear Modeling
Car A is at Mile Marker 0 traveling East at 40 mph. Car B is at Mile Marker 200 traveling West (towards A) at 60 mph. At what Mile Marker do they crash?
1. Car A Equation: $y = 40t$.
2. Car B Equation: $y = -60t + 200$ (Negative slope because it's going backwards towards 0).
3. Solve: $40t = -60t + 200 \Rightarrow 100t = 200 \Rightarrow t = 2$ hours.
4. Location: $y = 40(2) = 80$.
Answer: Mile Marker 80.
9. Lattice Points Number Theory
How many points with integer coordinates lie strictly on the line segment connecting $(0,0)$ and $(50, 75)$? (Do not count the start and end points).
1. Slope is $\frac{75}{50} = \frac{3}{2}$.
2. This means for every 2 steps right, we go 3 steps up to hit an integer.
3. How many "2-steps" fit in the width of 50? $50 \div 2 = 25$.
4. There are 25 segments, which means 24 internal connection points.
Answer: 24 points.
10. The Parameter Intersection Systems of Equations
Two lines are given by $y = 2x + 5$ and $y = kx + 3$. If these lines intersect at $x = 4$, what must the value of $k$ be?
1. Use the first equation to find the y-coordinate at $x=4$.
2. $y = 2(4) + 5 = 13$. Intersection is $(4, 13)$.
3. Plug this point into the second equation: $13 = k(4) + 3$.
4. $10 = 4k \Rightarrow k = 2.5$.
11. The Altitude Advanced Geometry
Triangle ABC has vertices $A(0,0)$, $B(4,0)$, and $C(2, 6)$. Find the equation of the altitude drawn from vertex B to side AC. (Note: An altitude is a line from a corner perpendicular to the opposite side).
1. Find slope of side AC (from 0,0 to 2,6): $m = \frac{6-0}{2-0} = 3$.
2. The altitude is perpendicular to this, so its slope is $-\frac{1}{3}$.
3. The altitude passes through B(4,0).
4. Eq: $y - 0 = -\frac{1}{3}(x - 4) \Rightarrow y = -\frac{1}{3}x + \frac{4}{3}$.
12. The Shortest Distance Optimization
What is the shortest distance from the origin $(0,0)$ to the line $y = -x + 4$? (Hint: The shortest path is always along the perpendicular line).
1. Original slope is -1. Perpendicular slope is 1.
2. Line from origin with slope 1 is $y = x$.
3. Find intersection of $y = -x + 4$ and $y = x$.
4. $x = -x + 4 \Rightarrow 2x = 4 \Rightarrow x = 2$. Point is $(2,2)$.
5. Distance from $(0,0)$ to $(2,2)$ is $\sqrt{2^2 + 2^2} = \sqrt{8} \approx 2.83$.
13. The Family of Lines Abstract Thinking
Write the equation of the line that passes through $(3, 4)$ but is also perpendicular to the line $x = 5$.
1. $x = 5$ is a vertical line.
2. Perpendicular to vertical is horizontal.
3. A horizontal line passing through height 4 is simply $y = 4$.
14. Midpoint Mapping Coordinates
The midpoint of a segment is $M(6, 10)$. One endpoint is $A(2, 4)$. Find the coordinates of the other endpoint $B$.
1. From A to M: $x$ goes up by 4 ($2 \to 6$), $y$ goes up by 6 ($4 \to 10$).
2. From M to B: Do the same. Add 4 to x, add 6 to y.
3. $x = 6+4=10$. $y = 10+6=16$.
Answer: $B(10, 16)$.
15. The Right Angle Test Geometric Proof
Do the points $A(0,0)$, $B(5,5)$, and $C(10,0)$ form a right triangle? Prove it using slopes.
1. Slope AB: $\frac{5-0}{5-0} = 1$.
2. Slope BC: $\frac{0-5}{10-5} = \frac{-5}{5} = -1$.
3. Since $1$ and $-1$ are negative reciprocals, angle B is $90^\circ$.
Answer: Yes, it is a right triangle.
16. The "Section" Point Ratio & Proportion
Find the coordinates of point $P$ that lies exactly 1/3 of the way from $A(0, 0)$ to $B(9, 12)$.
1. Total change in x is 9. One-third of 9 is 3.
2. Total change in y is 12. One-third of 12 is 4.
3. Add these to the start point $(0,0)$.
Answer: $P(3, 4)$.
17. The Impossible System Logic
Describe the solution to this system of equations without graphing:
Line A: $y = 3x + 4$
Line B: $2y - 6x = 20$
1. Rewrite Line B in slope-intercept form.
2. $2y = 6x + 20 \Rightarrow y = 3x + 10$.
3. Compare Line A ($y=3x+4$) and Line B ($y=3x+10$).
4. They have the same slope (3) but different intercepts. They are parallel.
Answer: No Solution (they never touch).
18. The Rectangle Check Quadrilaterals
Four points are given: $A(0,0)$, $B(2,4)$, $C(6,2)$, $D(4,-2)$. Prove whether this shape is a rectangle by checking the slopes of the adjacent sides.
1. Slope AB: $\frac{4-0}{2-0} = 2$.
2. Slope BC: $\frac{2-4}{6-2} = \frac{-2}{4} = -0.5$.
3. Since $2$ and $-0.5$ are negative reciprocals, the corner at B is $90^\circ$.
4. (You would repeat for other corners).
Answer: Yes, it is a rectangle.
19. The X-Intercept Analysis
Line L passes through $(1, 4)$ and has a slope of -2. At what x-coordinate does this line cross the x-axis?
1. Write equation: $y - 4 = -2(x - 1)$.
2. $y = -2x + 2 + 4 \Rightarrow y = -2x + 6$.
3. To find x-intercept, set $y=0$.
4. $0 = -2x + 6 \Rightarrow 2x = 6 \Rightarrow x = 3$.
Answer: $x = 3$.
20. Equidistant Point Challenge
Find a point on the y-axis (so $x=0$) that is equal distance from point $A(-3, 2)$ and point $B(5, 2)$. (Hint: You can use logic or the midpoint).
1. Notice $A$ and $B$ have the same y-value (2). The segment AB is horizontal.
2. The "middle" of them is $x = (-3+5)/2 = 1$.
3. Any point on the vertical line $x=1$ is equidistant.
4. But we need a point on the y-axis ($x=0$).
5. Wait! The perpendicular bisector is $x=1$. The y-axis is $x=0$. These lines are parallel and never meet.
Answer: There is no such point on the y-axis!
Proof: Distance $P(0,y)$ to A = Distance $P(0,y)$ to B.
$\sqrt{(0 - (-3))^2 + (y-2)^2} = \sqrt{(0-5)^2 + (y-2)^2}$
$9 = 25$. Impossible.
Logic: A is 3 units from axis, B is 5 units from axis. You can never be equal distance to both while sitting on the axis.
