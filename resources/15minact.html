A) 15-Minute Retrieval Quiz (paper-style)

Instructions. Write reasons clearly. Use parentheses on substitutions. If congruent, name the postulate and finish with CPCTC. If insufficient, say why and name the missing fact.

  1. State the reason: \(\angle 1\) and \(\angle 3\) are vertical ⇒ \(\angle 1 \cong \angle 3\). (Reason: ________)
  2. Reason: \(AD \perp BC\) at \(D\) ⇒ \(\angle ADC\) is a right angle. (Reason: ________)
  3. Reason: If \(m \parallel n\), corresponding angles are ________.
  4. Definition use: \(M\) is the midpoint of \(\overline{AB}\) ⇒ ________.
  5. Reflexive: The segment shared by \(\triangle ABC\) and \(\triangle DBC\) is ________ ⇒ congruent to itself.
  6. Equality property: From \(a=b\) infer \(a+c=b+c\). (Name: ________)
  7. Postulate ID: Two sides and the included angle match in order. (Name: ________)
  8. Postulate ID: Two angles and a non-included side match. (Name: ________)
  9. Right-triangle case: Hypotenuse and one leg match. (Name: ________)
  10. Insufficient pattern often mistaken for congruence: ________.
  11. Mini-proof. Lines \(AC\) and \(BD\) intersect at \(C\). Given \(AB=DB\). Prove \(\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DBC\). (State two reasons + postulate)
  12. In \(\triangle ABC\), ray \(AD\) bisects \(\angle A\), \(D\) on \(BC\), and \(AB=AC\). Prove \(\triangle ABD \cong \triangle ACD\). (Name postulate)
  13. Two right triangles share hypotenuse \(AC\); \(\angle B\) and \(\angle D\) are right; \(BC=DC\). Are they congruent? (Name postulate)
  14. Decide: A diagram shows two sides and a non-included angle equal. Congruent or insufficient? If insufficient, what extra fact would fix it?
  15. After proving \(\triangle XYZ \cong \triangle X'Y'Z'\), what principle justifies \(YZ=Y'Z'\)? (Name it.)
Quiz Key (for teacher) 1) Vertical angles are congruent. 2) Definition of perpendicular/right angle. 3) Congruent. 4) \(AM=MB\). 5) \(BC\) (or the named shared part). 6) APE. 7) SAS. 8) AAS. 9) HL. 10) SSA; also AAA. 11) \(BC\) reflexive + vertical angles at \(C\) + given side ⇒ SAS. 12) ASA (or AAS). 13) HL. 14) Insufficient (SSA). Missing: included angle or third side for SAS/SSS (etc.). 15) CPCTC.
B) Mini-Teach: Toolbox → Postulates → CPCTC
Toolbox (write these as reasons)
  • Definitions: midpoint, bisector, perpendicular, right angle, congruent.
  • Angle facts: vertical angles ≅; linear pair → supplementary; parallel lines → corresponding/alt-int angles ≅.
  • Reflexive: a segment/angle equals itself.
  • Equality: APE/SPE/MPE/DPE, Substitution, Transitive.
Congruence Postulates
  • SSS — three matching sides.
  • SAS — two sides + included angle.
  • ASA — two angles + included side.
  • AAS — two angles + non-included side.
  • HL — right triangles: hypotenuse + one leg.
Not sufficient: SSA, AAA. After congruence: CPCTC (corresponding parts of congruent triangles are congruent).
C) Guided Proof 1 — SAS (vertical angles + reflexive)

Given: Lines \(AC\) and \(BD\) intersect at \(C\). Also \(AB=DB\).

Prove: \(\triangle ABC \cong \triangle DBC\). Then conclude one matching angle by CPCTC.

Plan: Mark \(BC\) reflexive; use vertical angles at \(C\); combine with \(AB=DB\) → SAS.

Solution sketch \(BC\cong BC\) (Reflexive); \(\angle ACB\cong\angle DCB\) (vertical angles); \(AB=DB\) (given) ⇒ \(\triangle ABC\cong\triangle DBC\) by SAS. Then CPCTC for any needed corresponding part.
D) Guided Proof 2 — ASA/AAS (angle bisector)

Given: In \(\triangle ABC\), ray \(AD\) bisects \(\angle A\); \(D\) lies on \(BC\); \(AB=AC\).

Prove: \(\triangle ABD \cong \triangle ACD\). Then deduce \(BD=DC\) by CPCTC.

Plan: \(\angle BAD\cong\angle DAC\) (bisector); \(AB\cong AC\) (given); \(AD\) reflexive ⇒ ASA/AAS.

Solution sketch \(\angle BAD\cong\angle DAC\) (definition of angle bisector), \(AB\cong AC\) (given), \(AD\cong AD\) (Reflexive) ⇒ triangles congruent by ASA (or AAS). Then \(BD=DC\) by CPCTC.
E) Guided Proof 3 — HL (right triangles)

Given: \(\triangle ABC\) and \(\triangle ADC\) are right at \(B\) and \(D\). They share hypotenuse \(AC\). Also \(BC=DC\).

Prove: \(\triangle ABC \cong \triangle ADC\). Then state one CPCTC conclusion.

Plan: Right angles + common hypotenuse + a leg equality ⇒ HL.

Solution sketch \(\angle B\) and \(\angle D\) right; \(AC\) is the hypotenuse for both; \(BC=DC\) ⇒ \(\triangle ABC \cong \triangle ADC\) by HL. Then CPCTC as needed.
F) Practice Set — “Pick the Postulate” (independent)
  1. Two triangles share side \(AC\). Lines \(m\parallel n\) create \(\angle CAB \cong \angle A C'B'\). You also know \(AB= A'B'\). Is it SAS, ASA, or insufficient? Explain.
  2. Given \(DE\) is the perpendicular bisector of \(\overline{AB}\) at \(M\). Show \(\triangle AME \cong \triangle BME\); then conclude one CPCTC result.
  3. A diagram shows \(PQ=RS\), \(PR=QS\), and \(\angle QPR=\angle RSQ\). Choose the postulate and finish.
Hints / Key 1) If the marked angle is included between the matched sides, SAS; if not, ASA/AAS depending on data; if the angle is not properly corresponding, it’s insufficient. 2) Use midpoint/right-angle facts + reflexive \(ME\) to trigger SAS (or HL if right). 3) The information aligns as SAS ⇒ triangles congruent ⇒ CPCTC.
G) Misleaders & Diagnostics (SSA / AAA)

For each “near-miss,” decide if it’s truly congruent. If not, write the one extra fact that would make it valid.

  1. Two sides and a non-included angle are known (SSA).
  2. All three angles are known (AAA).
Answers 1) Insufficient; add included angle or third side to reach SAS/SSS. 2) Insufficient for congruence; gives similarity only. Add one side length in correspondence (with scale) to fix size.
H) Exit Proof — Paragraph Form

Given: Line \(m\) is the perpendicular bisector of \(\overline{AB}\) at \(M\). Line \(m\) is parallel to line \(CD\). Point \(C\) lies on \(m\); \(D\) lies on a line through \(B\) parallel to \(m\).

Prove: \(\triangle AMC \cong \triangle BMD\), then deduce \(\angle ACM \cong \angle MBD\) by CPCTC.

Deliverable: One clean paragraph proof using Target → Steps (with reasons) → Therefore.

Solution outline (teacher) \(AM=MB\) and \(\angle AMM=\angle MMB=90^\circ\) (perpendicular bisector). With \(m\parallel CD\), form a corresponding/alternate-interior angle pair. Combine with a shared/right structure to trigger SAS/HL. Conclude congruence, then CPCTC to claim the angle equality.
  • Rubric (8 pts): Givens marked (2) • Valid postulate named (2) • Reason chain (3) • CPCTC (1).
Writing checklist (use on every problem): Label diagram → write Target → show steps with named Reasons → conclude with a clear Therefore → box any CPCTC result.
