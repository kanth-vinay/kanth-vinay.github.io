10 Advanced Linear Inequalities Word Problems

These problems require careful setup, unit conversions, and contextual interpretation of continuous versus discrete variables. Set up the linear inequality, solve it algebraically, and state your final answer in the context of the real-world constraints.

1. Museum Group Entry with Hourly Rates

A museum charges a flat reservation fee of $45. You must also pay for a mandatory guide at a rate of $15.50 per hour. Your scheduled tour lasts 2.5 hours. Student tickets are an additional $7.25 each. Your school has allocated a budget strictly less than $200 for this trip. Let \(x\) be the number of students who can attend.

Question: Write and solve an inequality to determine the maximum number of students that can attend.

Reveal solution

Setup: Total cost = reservation fee + (guide hourly rate × hours) + (student ticket price × number of students).

\[ 45 + 15.50(2.5) + 7.25x < 200 \] \[ 45 + 38.75 + 7.25x < 200 \] \[ 83.75 + 7.25x < 200 \] \[ 7.25x < 116.25 \] \[ x < 16.034\ldots \]

Context: Since you cannot bring a fraction of a student, the maximum number of whole students who can attend is 16.

2. Streaming Data Limit and Conversions

A student's mobile data plan has a strict 15 GB total monthly limit. She has already used 4,250 MB (assume 1 GB = 1,000 MB for this provider). She needs to download a required 1.5 GB document package for school. After that, she wants to stream a video lecture series that consumes data at a rate of 1.2 GB per hour. Let \(m\) be the number of full minutes she can stream without exceeding her limit.

Question: Write and solve an inequality for the possible values of \(m\).

Reveal solution

Setup: First convert all units to GB. Since 4,250 MB = 4.25 GB, let \(h\) be hours of streaming.

\[ 4.25 + 1.5 + 1.2h \le 15 \] \[ 5.75 + 1.2h \le 15 \] \[ 1.2h \le 9.25 \] \[ h \le 7.70833\ldots \]

Context: Converting to minutes gives \(7.70833\ldots \times 60 = 462.5\) minutes. Since the question asks for full minutes, \(m \le 462\).

3. Elevator Safety Rule with Static Loads

An industrial elevator has a maximum safe carrying capacity of 1,200 pounds. A worker weighing 195 pounds is operating it, and his maintenance cart weighing 45.5 pounds is already inside. He is loading crates that weigh 32.4 pounds each. He also has his service dog, weighing 60.5 pounds, with him. Let \(c\) be the number of crates loaded.

Question: Write and solve an inequality to find the allowable number of crates he can load.

Reveal solution

Setup: Total weight = worker + cart + dog + (crate weight × number of crates).

\[ 195 + 45.5 + 60.5 + 32.4c \le 1200 \] \[ 301 + 32.4c \le 1200 \] \[ 32.4c \le 899 \] \[ c \le 27.746\ldots \]

Context: Crates must be whole units, so the worker can safely load at most 27 crates.

4. Fundraiser Profit Goal with Processing Fees

A club spent $115.75 on physical supplies for a fundraiser. They sell digital tickets for $12.50 each, but the ticketing platform takes a $2.25 processing fee per ticket sold. Additionally, the club must pay a flat $50.00 cleanup fee to the venue at the end of the night. Let \(t\) be the number of tickets sold.

Question: Write and solve an inequality to determine how many tickets must be sold to guarantee a net profit of at least $300.

Reveal solution

Setup: Net revenue per ticket is \(12.50 - 2.25 = 10.25\). Total profit is \((10.25)(t) - 115.75 - 50\).

\[ 10.25t - 115.75 - 50 \ge 300 \] \[ 10.25t - 165.75 \ge 300 \] \[ 10.25t \ge 465.75 \] \[ t \ge 45.439\ldots \]

Context: They must sell at least 46 tickets.

5. Proportional Fencing Constraints

A gardener is building a rectangular pen against a straight brick wall, meaning only three sides require fencing. She has no more than 68.5 meters of fencing available. The design requires that the single side parallel to the wall must be exactly 2.5 times the length of each of the two shorter perpendicular sides, each of length \(x\).

Question: Write and solve an inequality to find the maximum possible length for the shorter sides, \(x\).

Reveal solution

Setup: The three fenced sides are \(x\), \(x\), and \(2.5x\).

\[ x + x + 2.5x \le 68.5 \] \[ 4.5x \le 68.5 \] \[ x \le 15.222\ldots \]

Context: Length is continuous, so the shorter side can be any length up to 15.22 meters, approximately.

6. Weighted Exam Score Requirement

A student's first three test scores are 78, 84, and 91, each worth 15% of the final course grade. A term project, already completed with a score of 95, is worth 30% of the final grade. The fourth and final test is worth the remaining 25% of the course grade. Let \(x\) be the score on the fourth test.

Question: Write and solve an inequality to determine the score needed on the fourth test, out of 100, to guarantee a final weighted average of at least 88.

Reveal solution

Setup: Form the weighted sum of all components.

\[ 0.15(78 + 84 + 91) + 0.30(95) + 0.25x \ge 88 \] \[ 0.15(253) + 28.5 + 0.25x \ge 88 \] \[ 37.95 + 28.5 + 0.25x \ge 88 \] \[ 66.45 + 0.25x \ge 88 \] \[ 0.25x \ge 21.55 \] \[ x \ge 86.2 \]

Context: The student must score at least 86.2 on the fourth test.

7. Bike Rental Tiered Pricing Decision

Shop A charges a $25 flat reservation fee plus $3.50 per hour. Shop B charges a $5 flat fee, but costs $8.00 per hour for the first two hours and then drops to $6.00 per hour for every hour after that. Assuming a rental period of strictly more than 2 hours, let \(h\) be the total number of hours rented.

Question: Write and solve an inequality to determine for what total rental times \(h > 2\) Shop A is strictly cheaper than Shop B.

Reveal solution

Setup:

Cost of Shop A: \(25 + 3.50h\)

Cost of Shop B, for \(h > 2\): \(5 + 8(2) + 6(h - 2) = 9 + 6h\)

\[ 25 + 3.50h < 9 + 6h \] \[ 16 < 2.5h \] \[ 6.4 < h \]

Context: Shop A is strictly cheaper when the rental period is greater than 6.4 hours, that is, more than 6 hours 24 minutes.

8. Variable Rate Candle Burning

A decorative candle is initially 45 cm tall. For the first 3 hours, it burns at a constant rate of 1.8 cm per hour. After those 3 hours, the wax pools and the burning rate slows to 1.2 cm per hour. For a visual display, the candle must remain at least 15 cm tall. Let \(t\) be the number of additional hours the candle can burn after the initial 3-hour period.

Question: Write and solve an inequality for the allowable additional burn time \(t\).

Reveal solution

Setup: Initial height minus first-stage burn minus second-stage burn must be at least 15 cm.

\[ 45 - 1.8(3) - 1.2t \ge 15 \] \[ 45 - 5.4 - 1.2t \ge 15 \] \[ 39.6 - 1.2t \ge 15 \] \[ 24.6 \ge 1.2t \] \[ 20.5 \ge t \]

Context: The candle can burn for at most 20.5 additional hours after the first 3 hours.

9. Net Flow Water Tank System

A 500-liter tank currently contains 145.5 liters of water. Water is draining out at a constant rate of 8.4 liters per minute. To offset this, two refill hoses are turned on simultaneously: Hose A adds water at 3.2 liters per minute, and Hose B adds water at 4.5 liters per minute. The system must be shut down if the tank volume drops below 100 liters. Let \(m\) be the number of minutes the system can run.

Question: Write and solve an inequality for the possible values of \(m\).

Reveal solution

Setup: Net rate \(= (3.2 + 4.5) - 8.4 = -0.7\) liters per minute.

\[ 145.5 - 0.7m \ge 100 \] \[ -0.7m \ge -45.5 \] \[ m \le 65 \]

Context: The system can run for at most 65 minutes.

10. Reading Challenge with Compounding Penalties

A student starts a reading challenge with 150 points. She loses 4.5 points for every day a specific library book is overdue; this book is currently 12 days overdue. To recover her score, she can earn 3.25 points for each chapter review she submits online. She needs her final score to be strictly greater than 165 to qualify for the prize tier. Let \(r\) be the number of chapter reviews she must submit.

Question: Write and solve an inequality for the number of reviews required.

Reveal solution

Setup: Starting points minus penalty plus earned points must exceed 165.

\[ 150 - 4.5(12) + 3.25r > 165 \] \[ 150 - 54 + 3.25r > 165 \] \[ 96 + 3.25r > 165 \] \[ 3.25r > 69 \] \[ r > 21.230\ldots \]

Context: She must submit at least 22 chapter reviews.
