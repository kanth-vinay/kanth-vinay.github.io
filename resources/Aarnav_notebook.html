Unit 1: Foundations of Algebra

Before we can solve equations, we need to understand the building blocks: Real Numbers, Operations, and Properties.

1.1 Sets of Real Numbers

Classification
Natural Numbers (\(\mathbb{N}\))Counting numbers: 1, 2, 3...
Whole Numbers (\(\mathbb{W}\))Natural + 0: 0, 1, 2, 3...
Integers (\(\mathbb{Z}\))Whole + Negatives: ...-2, -1, 0, 1, 2...
Rational Numbers (\(\mathbb{Q}\))Can be written as fraction p/q. (Terminating/Repeating decimals)
Irrational Numbers (\(\mathbb{I}\))Cannot be written as fraction. (Non-terminating, non-repeating). e.g., \(\pi, \sqrt{2}\)

1.2 Order of Operations

PEMDAS: Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication/Division (L to R), Addition/Subtraction (L to R).

1.3 Properties of Real Numbers

CommutativeOrder doesn't matter.
\(a + b = b + a\)
AssociativeGrouping doesn't matter.
\((a+b)+c = a+(b+c)\)
DistributiveMultiply across sum.
\(a(b+c) = ab + ac\)

1.4 Expressions

Evaluating algebraic expressions by substitution.

Unit 2: Solving Equations

The goal is to isolate the variable. Whatever you do to one side, you must do to the other.

2.1 Multi-Step Equations

Problem: Solve \( 3(x - 2) + 5 = 11 \)

  • 1. Distribute: \( 3x - 6 + 5 = 11 \)
  • 2. Combine Like Terms: \( 3x - 1 = 11 \)
  • 3. Add 1: \( 3x = 12 \)
  • 4. Divide by 3: \( x = 4 \)

2.2 Variables on Both Sides

Move all variable terms to one side, constants to the other.

2.3 Literal Equations

Solving for a variable in terms of others.

Example: Solve \( A = \frac{1}{2}bh \) for \( h \).
Multiply by 2: \( 2A = bh \)
Divide by b: \( h = \frac{2A}{b} \)

2.4 Absolute Value Equations

Remember: Split into two equations! \( |x| = c \Rightarrow x = c \text{ or } x = -c \).

Unit 3: Inequalities

Remember: When you multiply or divide by a negative number, you FLIP the inequality sign.

3.1 Multi-Step Inequalities

Solve just like equations, but watch the negative division rule.

3.2 Compound Inequalities

AND (Intersection)Graph overlap. \( -2 < x \le 5 \)
OR (Union)Graph both tails. \( x < -2 \text{ or } x \ge 5 \)

3.3 Absolute Value Inequalities

Less ThAND vs GreatOR.

Unit 4: Introduction to Functions

A relation where every input (x) has exactly one output (y).

4.1 The Function Concept

Input-Output mappings. Vertical Line Test for graphs.

4.2 Function Notation

\( f(x) \) simply means the value of the function depending on x.

4.3 Domain & Range

Domain: All possible x-values. Range: All possible y-values.

4.4 Arithmetic Sequences

Recursive: \( a_n = a_{n-1} + d \). Explicit: \( a_n = a_1 + (n-1)d \).

Unit 5: Linear Functions

Lines hold a constant rate of change called Slope.

5.1 Slope & Rate of Change

m = \( \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1} \) = \( \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} \)

5.2 Slope-Intercept Form

\( y = mx + b \). m is slope, b is y-intercept.

5.3 Point-Slope Form

\( y - y_1 = m(x - x_1) \). Useful when you have a slope and ANY point.

5.4 Standard Form

\( Ax + By = C \). Useful for finding intercepts.

5.5 Parallel & Perpendicular

Parallel: Same slope. Perpendicular: Negative Reciprocal slopes.

5.6 Trend Lines

Line of Best Fit for scatter plots.

Unit 6: Systems of Equations

Finding where two lines intersect.

6.1 Solving by Graphing

Graph both lines. The intersection is the solution.

6.2 Substitution

Isolate one variable and plug it into the other equation.

6.3 Elimination

Add equations together to cancel out a variable.

6.4 Systems of Inequalities

Shade the valid regions. The solution is the overlapping shaded region.

Unit 7: Exponents & Radicals

Laws of exponents allow us to simplify complex expressions.

7.1 Laws of Exponents

Product Rule\( x^a \cdot x^b = x^{a+b} \)
Power Rule\( (x^a)^b = x^{a \cdot b} \)
Quotient Rule\( \frac{x^a}{x^b} = x^{a-b} \)
Negative Exp\( x^{-n} = \frac{1}{x^n} \)

7.2 Rational Exponents

\( x^{1/n} = \sqrt[n]{x} \). Fractional exponents represent roots.

7.3 Simplifying Radicals

Looking for perfect square factors in the radicand.

7.4 Geometric Sequences

Multiply by a common ratio. \( a_n = a_1 \cdot r^{n-1} \).

7.5 Exponential Functions

\( y = a \cdot b^x \). Growth if \( b > 1 \), Decay if \( 0 < b < 1 \).

Unit 8: Polynomials & Factoring

8.1 Naming Polynomials

Degree (Linear, Quadratic, Cubic) and Number of Terms (Monomial, Binomial, Trinomial).

8.2 Operations

Adding/Subtracting: Combine like terms. Multiplying: FOIL or Box Method.

8.3 Factoring GCF

The first step in any factoring problem! Pull out the Greatest Common Factor.

8.4 Factoring by Grouping

Used for 4-term polynomials.

8.5 Trinomials (ac Method)

Splitting the middle term to factor \( ax^2 + bx + c \).

8.6 Difference of Squares

\( a^2 - b^2 = (a+b)(a-b) \).

Unit 9: Quadratic Functions

Parabolas. \( f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c \)

9.1 Graphing Parabolas

Axis of Symmetry: \( x = -b/2a \). Vertex is max/min.

9.2 Vertex Form

\( y = a(x-h)^2 + k \). Vertex is at (h,k).

9.3 Solving by Factoring

Zero Product Property: If \( A \cdot B = 0 \), then \( A=0 \) or \( B=0 \).

9.4 Square Roots & Completing the Square

Isolate the squared term or force a perfect square trinomial.

9.5 The Quadratic Formula

\( x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} \)

Unit 10: Data & Statistics

10.1 Measures of Center

Mean, Median, Mode.

10.2 Box Plots

Shows Min, Q1, Median, Q3, Max (Five Number Summary).

10.3 Histograms

Shows frequency ranges (bins).

10.4 Scatter Plots

Visualizing the relationship between two variables (Correlation).
