Triangle Proofs Quiz

Test your understanding of postulates, theorems, and definitions for 9th-grade triangle proofs.

You have 15 minutes to complete 15 questions.

Time Remaining: 15:00

1. If three sides of one triangle are congruent to three sides of another triangle, which postulate proves congruence?

2. You have proven $\triangle XYZ \cong \triangle PQR$. What is the reason you can now state that $\angle Y \cong \angle Q$?

3. Two segments, $\overline{MN}$ and $\overline{OP}$, intersect at point $Q$. Which theorem states that $\angle MQO \cong \angle NPQ$?

4. If two angles and the included side of one triangle are congruent to the corresponding parts of another triangle, which postulate applies?

5. In a proof, you establish that a side $\overline{AB}$ is shared by two triangles. What property allows you to state $\overline{AB} \cong \overline{AB}$?

6. What is the sum of the measures of the interior angles of any triangle?

7. If $\triangle ABC$ has $\overline{AB} \cong \overline{AC}$, which theorem states that $\angle B \cong \angle C$?

8. A segment $\overline{PM}$ connects vertex $P$ to the midpoint $M$ of the opposite side $\overline{QR}$ in $\triangle PQR$. What is $\overline{PM}$ called?

9. Lines $l$ and $m$ are parallel and cut by transversal $t$. If $\angle 1$ and $\angle 2$ are on opposite sides of $t$ and between $l$ and $m$, which theorem states $\angle 1 \cong \angle 2$?

10. What is the reason to state that two angles created by an angle bisector are congruent?

11. If two right triangles have a congruent hypotenuse and a congruent leg, what theorem proves they are congruent?

12. A line segment is divided into two congruent segments by a point. What is that point called?

13. If you are given that $\angle A \cong \angle B$ in $\triangle ABC$, which theorem allows you to conclude that $\overline{AC} \cong \overline{BC}$?

14. What is a segment from a vertex of a triangle perpendicular to the opposite side called?

15. If two angles and a non-included side of one triangle are congruent to the corresponding two angles and non-included side of another triangle, which congruence rule applies?
