Rational Functions & Vertical Asymptotes

Rational Function

\(f(x)=\dfrac{P(x)}{Q(x)}\) where \(P,Q\) are polynomials and \(Q\neq 0\). The domain: all \(x\) with \(Q(x)\neq 0\). [Rational def]

Vertical Asymptote \(x=a\)

If any holds: \(\lim_{x\to a^-}f(x)=\pm\infty\) or \(\lim_{x\to a^+}f(x)=\pm\infty\).

If the simplified expression has denominator \(0\) at \(a\), \(x=a\) is a vertical asymptote.

Quick Example

For \(f(x)=\dfrac{x^2-2}{(x-1)(x+1)}\), \(x=1,-1\) make the denominator \(0\)\(\Rightarrow\) vertical asymptotes at \(x=1,-1\).

Check-your-thinking: Factor denominators to spot candidate vertical asymptotes.

Horizontal & Slant (Oblique) Asymptotes

Horizontal Asymptote \(y=b\)

If \(\lim_{x\to\infty}f(x)=b\) or \(\lim_{x\to-\infty}f(x)=b\).

For rationals: occurs when degree(numerator) ≤ degree(denominator). Graphs may cross horizontal asymptotes.

Slant Asymptote \(y=mx+c\)

Occurs for rationals when degree(numer.) is exactly 1 more than degree(denom.). Use polynomial long division to find it. Graphs can cross slant asymptotes.

Worked (Slant)

Divide numerator by denominator → write \(f(x)=\text{(linear)}+\text{(remainder term)}\). As \(|x|\to\infty\), remainder \(\to 0\) ⇒ line is the slant asymptote.

Practice: identify degrees first; if “one more” → do the division.

Asymptotes of Exponential & Log Functions

  • \(f(x)=a e^{bx}\), \(b>0\): horizontal asymptote \(y=0\) as \(x\to -\infty\).
  • \(f(x)=a e^{bx}\), \(b<0\): horizontal asymptote \(y=0\) as \(x\to \infty\).
  • \(f(x)=\ln(x-a)\): vertical asymptote \(x=a\) (approaching from the right).
  • \(f(x)=\ln(a-x)\): vertical asymptote \(x=a\) (approaching from the left).

Example

Find horizontal asymptotes of \(f(x)=\dfrac{200}{1+25e^{-0.04x}}\):

  • As \(x\to\infty\): \(e^{-0.04x}\to 0\Rightarrow f(x)\to 200\) (so \(y=200\)).
  • As \(x\to-\infty\): \(e^{-0.04x}\to\infty\Rightarrow f(x)\to 0\) (so \(y=0\)).

Strategy for Sketching Graphs

(a) Intercepts

Find x- and y-intercepts.

(b) Asymptotes

Vertical, horizontal, slant.

(c) Derivatives & Domain

Compute \(f'(x)\), \(f''(x)\); find domain.

(d) Critical Values

Where \(f'(x)=0\) or undefined.

(e)–(g)
  • Use Second Derivative Test when possible.
  • Inflection: where \(f''(x)=0\) or DNE; evaluate \(f\).
  • Concavity: sign of \(f''(x)\) on intervals.
Finally: (h) Sketch using all information.

Example (Rational): \(f(x)=\dfrac{8}{x^2-4}\)

(a) Intercepts: no x-intercept; \(f(0)=-2\) ⇒ y-intercept at (0, −2).

(b) Asymptotes: \(x^2-4=(x-2)(x+2)=0 \Rightarrow x=\pm2\) vertical; degree numerator < denominator ⇒ horizontal \(y=0\); no slant.

(c)–(d) Use \(f'\), \(f''\) to get critical value at \(x=0\) (relative max), monotonicity & concavity per intervals.

\(f'(x)=\dfrac{-16x}{(x^2-4)^2}\), \(f''(x)=\dfrac{16(3x^2+4)}{(x^2-4)^3}\).

Criticals from \(f'(x)=0\): \(x=0\) (allowed) ⇒ relative max at \(x=0\). Vertical asymptotes split concavity checks.

Quick Checks — Asymptotes

Q1. Vertical asymptotes of \(f(x)=\dfrac{1}{x(x-4)(x+4)}\)?
At \(x=0,4,-4\).
Q2. Horizontal asymptote of \(f(x)=\dfrac{(x-1)(x+1)}{(3x+2)(x+5)}\)?
Degrees equal ⇒ divide by \(x^2\): tends to \(\dfrac{2}{15}\); so \(y=\dfrac{2}{15}\).

Absolute Max & Min (3.4)

Definitions

On domain \(I\): \(f(c)\) is an absolute min if \(f(c)\le f(x)\) for all \(x\in I\); absolute max if \(f(c)\ge f(x)\) for all \(x\in I\).

Extreme Value Theorem: continuous on \([a,b]\) ⇒ has both an absolute max and min on \([a,b]\).

Two Principles

  • Max–Min Principle 1 (closed \([a,b]\)): Evaluate \(f\) at endpoints and all critical points; largest/smallest are the absolute extrema.
  • Max–Min Principle 2 (one critical in interval \(I\)): If \(f'(c)=0\) and \(f''(c)<0\) ⇒ absolute max; if \(f''(c)>0\) ⇒ absolute min (on \(I\)).

Example (Closed Interval)

Find absolute extrema of \(f(x)=x^3-3x+2\) on \([-2,\tfrac{3}{2}]\).

  1. \(f'(x)=3x^2-3\Rightarrow x=\pm1\) (both in interval).
  2. Evaluate \(f(-2),f(-1),f(1),f(\tfrac{3}{2})\).

\(f(-2)=0,\ f(-1)=4,\ f(1)=0,\ f(3/2)=\frac{27}{8}-\frac{9}{2}+2=\frac{4}{8}=0.5\).

Abs max: 4 at \(x=-1\). Abs min: 0 at \(x=1\) and \(x=-2\).

Example (Open / Semi-infinite Interval)

On \((0,\infty)\), let \(f(x)=\dfrac{35}{x}+5x\).

  1. \(f'(x)=-\dfrac{35}{x^2}+5=0\Rightarrow x=\sqrt{7}\).
  2. Only one critical in \((0,\infty)\); use Principle 2.

\(f''(x)=\dfrac{70}{x^3}>0\Rightarrow\) absolute minimum at \(x=\sqrt{7}\).

Value: \(f(\sqrt{7})=\dfrac{35}{\sqrt{7}}+5\sqrt{7}=10\sqrt{7}\).

Optimization Strategy (3.5)

  1. Read carefully; draw if helpful.
  2. List variables/constants; units; what varies.
  3. Translate to a quantity \(Q\) to max/min.
  4. Express \(Q\) as a function of one variable; use calculus (critical points, endpoints as needed).

Business Note

Maximum profit occurs where \(R'(x)=C'(x)\) and \(R''(x)<C''(x)\).

Example: Open-Top Box

Cut squares of side \(x\) from each corner of an \(8\text{ in}\times 8\text{ in}\) sheet and fold.

\(V(x)=(8-2x)(8-2x)x= -4x^3+32x^2-64x\).

On \(0<x<4\): \(V'(x)=-12x^2+64x-64=0\Rightarrow x=\dfrac{4}{3}\).

\(V''(x)=-24x+64\Rightarrow V''(4/3)=-32<0\) ⇒ maximum at \(x=4/3\) in.

Max volume \(V(4/3)=\dfrac{512}{27}\,\text{in}^3\approx 18.96\,\text{in}^3\).

Example: Revenue, Cost, Profit

Price function \(p(x)=1000-x\), cost \(C(x)=3000+2x\).

Revenue \(R(x)=xp(x)=1000x-x^2\). Profit \(P(x)=R(x)-C(x)=-x^2+980x-3000\).

\(P'(x)=-2x+980=0\Rightarrow x=490\).

\(P''(x)=-2<0\) ⇒ max at \(x=490\).

Max profit \(P(490)=\$237{,}100\). Price \(p(490)=\$510\).
