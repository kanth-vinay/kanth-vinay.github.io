Unit 1: Basics of Geometry

Geometry begins with three undefined terms: Point, Line, and Plane. From these, we define everything else.

1.1 Points, Lines, and Planes

Definitions
PointZero dimensions. A location in space. Label: \( A \)
LineOne dimension. Infinite length. Defined by 2 points. Label: \( \overleftrightarrow{AB} \)
PlaneTwo dimensions. Flat surface extending forever. Defined by 3 non-collinear points.

1.2 Segments and Distance

A segment \( \overline{AB} \) consists of endpoints A and B and all points between them.

  • Distance Formula: Calculate distance between \( (x_1, y_1) \) and \( (x_2, y_2) \).
    \( d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2} \)
  • Midpoint Formula: Average of the coordinates.
    \( M = (\frac{x_1+x_2}{2}, \frac{y_1+y_2}{2}) \)

Unit 2: Logic & Proofs

Proofs are the backbone of mathematics. We use deductive reasoning to move from given information to a proven conclusion.

2.1 Logic Statements

Conditional Statements

Conditional: If \( p \), then \( q \). Notation: \( p \rightarrow q \)

ConverseSwitch hypothesis and conclusion. \( q \rightarrow p \)
InverseNegate both. \( \sim p \rightarrow \sim q \)
ContrapositiveSwitch and Negate. \( \sim q \rightarrow \sim p \)
(Logically equivalent to the original!)

2.2 Triangle Congruence

Two triangles are congruent if all corresponding sides and angles differ only in position. We don't need to prove all 6 parts; we have shortcuts.

Congruence Postulates
SSSSide-Side-Side
SASSide-Angle-Side (Included Angle)
ASAAngle-Side-Angle (Included Side)
AASAngle-Angle-Side (Non-included)
HLHypotenuse-Leg (Right \(\Delta\)s only)
CPCTC

Corresponding Parts of Congruent Triangles are Congruent.
Use this only AFTER you have proven triangles are congruent!

3.2 Key Theorems

  • Triangle Sum Theorem: All angles sum to \( 180^\circ \).
  • Exterior Angle Theorem: \( m\angle Ext = m\angle A + m\angle B \).
  • Triangle Inequality: Sum of any two sides > third side. \( a+b > c \).

Unit 5: Similarity

Similar figures have the same shape but different sizes. Angles are congruent, sides are proportional.

5.1 Similarity Shortcuts

AA~ (Angle-Angle)If 2 angles are congruent, triangles are similar.
SAS~If 2 sides are proportional and the included angle is congruent.
SSS~If all 3 sides are proportional.

5.2 Geometric Mean

In a Right Triangle, drawing an altitude to the hypotenuse defines Geometric Mean relationships.

\( \frac{Piece_1}{Altitude} = \frac{Altitude}{Piece_2} \Rightarrow Altitude^2 = P_1 \cdot P_2 \)

Unit 8: Transformations

A transformation changes the position, size, or shape of a figure. We focus on Rigid Motions (Isometries) which preserve distance and angles.

Coordinate Rules (The "Cheatsheet")
Translation\( (x,y) \to (x+a, y+b) \)
Reflection (x-axis)\( (x,y) \to (x, -y) \)
Reflection (y-axis)\( (x,y) \to (-x, y) \)
Reflection (y=x)\( (x,y) \to (y, x) \)
Rotation (90° CCW)\( (x,y) \to (-y, x) \)
Rotation (180°)\( (x,y) \to (-x, -y) \)

8.1 Worked Example: The Process

Don't do it in your head! Follow the Rule → Substitution → Solve method.

Problem: Rotate Point \( P(3, 5) \) 90° CCW.

Step 1
Select the Rule.
90° CCW Rule is: \( (x,y) \to (-y, x) \)
Step 2
Substitute.
My \( x=3 \), my \( y=5 \).
So \( -y \) becomes \( -5 \). \( x \) becomes \( 3 \).
Step 3
Final Coordinate.
\( P'(-5, 3) \)
Check
Intuition Check: (3,5) is in Quadrant I. Rotating 90° Left should land in Quadrant II (Negative x, Positive y). (-5, 3) matches!
Common Errors
  • Mixing up Reflections: x-axis changes y \( (x,-y) \). y-axis changes x \( (-x,y) \). Don't swap them!
  • Order of Composition: \( T \circ R(P) \) means do R (Reflection) FIRST, then T (Translation). Reading left-to-right is fatal here.
  • Negating Zero: \( -0 \) is just \( 0 \). Don't panic.

8.2 Practice Set

Apply the 3-step process. Write it down.

  • 1. Rotation: Rotate \( A(-2, 4) \) 180° about the origin.
    Rule: \( (x,y) \to (-x, -y) \)
    Sub: \( -(-2) \to 2 \), \( -(4) \to -4 \)
    Ans: \( A'(2, -4) \)
  • 2. Reflection: Reflect \( B(5, 1) \) over the line \( y=x \).
    Rule: \( (x,y) \to (y, x) \)
    Sub: Swap them.
    Ans: \( B'(1, 5) \)
  • 3. Composition: \( C(3, -2) \). Reflect over y-axis, then Translate \( \langle 4, 1 \rangle \).
    Step 1 (Reflect y-axis): Rule \( (-x, y) \) \(\to (-3, -2)\)
    Step 2 (Translate): Rule \( (x+4, y+1) \) \(\to (-3+4, -2+1)\)
    Ans: \( (1, -1) \)

Unit 9: Introduction to Functions

A function is a relation where every input has exactly one output.

9.1 Function Notation

Terminology
\( f(x) \)Read "f of x". It is the output value (y) for input x.
DomainAll possible x-values (Inputs).
RangeAll possible y-values (Outputs).
