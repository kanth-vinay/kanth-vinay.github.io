Question 1

Differentiate the function: $$f(x) = -8e^{4x}$$
  • \( -32e^{x} \)
  • \( -32e^{4x} \)
  • \( -8e^{4x} \)
  • \( 4e^{4x} \)

Solution

We use the Chain Rule and the Constant Multiple Rule. The derivative of \(e^u\) is \(e^u \cdot u'\).

Let the outer function be \(\color{var(--accent-orange)}{-8e^u}\) and the inner function be \(\color{var(--accent-green)}{u = 4x}\).

1. The derivative of the inner function is \(\frac{d}{dx}[\color{var(--accent-green)}{4x}] = \color{var(--accent-red)}{4}\).

2. Apply the Chain Rule: \(f'(x) = (\text{derivative of outer}) \times (\text{derivative of inner})\).

$$f'(x) = {\color{#89592a}{-8}}e^{\color{var(--accent-green)}{4x}} \cdot {\color{var(--accent-red)}{4}}$$

3. Simplify the expression:

$$f'(x) = -32e^{4x}$$

Final Answer: B. \( -32e^{4x} \)

Question 2

Find \(\frac{dy}{dx}\) for: $$y = -\frac{80}{x^6}$$

Solution

1. Rewrite the function using a negative exponent to apply the Power Rule.

$$y = -80x^{-6}$$

2. Apply the Power Rule, \(\frac{d}{dx}[x^n] = nx^{n-1}\), along with the Constant Multiple Rule.

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = {\color{#89592a}{-80}} \cdot \frac{d}{dx}[x^{-6}]$$

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = {\color{#89592a}{-80}} \cdot ({\color{var(--accent-red)}{-6}}x^{-6-1})$$

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = 480x^{-7}$$

3. Finally, rewrite the expression with a positive exponent.

Final Answer: \(\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{480}{x^7}\)

Question 3

Differentiate: $$f(x)=(3x^{4}+8)^{2}$$
  • \(f'(x)=144x^{15}+96x^{3}\)
  • \(f'(x)=6x^{4}+16\)
  • \(f'(x)=72x^{7}+192x^{3}\)
  • \(f'(x)=9x^{16}+64\)

Solution

This requires the Chain Rule (specifically, the General Power Rule).

Let the outer function be \(\color{var(--accent-orange)}{u^2}\) and the inner function be \(\color{var(--accent-green)}{u = 3x^4+8}\).

1. Derivative of the outer function w.r.t u is \(2u\).

2. Derivative of the inner function w.r.t x is \(\frac{d}{dx}[\color{var(--accent-green)}{3x^4+8}] = \color{var(--accent-red)}{12x^3}\).

3. Apply the Chain Rule: \(f'(x) = (\text{deriv of outer}) \cdot (\text{deriv of inner})\).

$$f'(x) = {\color{var(--accent-red)}{2}}(\overbrace{\color{var(--accent-green)}{3x^4+8}}^{u}) \cdot (\overbrace{\color{var(--accent-red)}{12x^3}}^{u'})$$

4. Simplify by distributing:

$$f'(x) = (24x^3)(3x^4+8) = 72x^7 + 192x^3$$

Final Answer: C. \(f'(x) = 72x^{7}+192x^{3}\)

Question 4

Solve the equation: $$\ln(x^{2}-25)-\ln(x+5)=\ln 1$$
  • \(\pm5\)
  • \(\pm26\)
  • \(5\)
  • \(6\)

Solution

1. Use the logarithm property \(\ln a - \ln b = \ln(\frac{a}{b})\) to combine the terms on the left.

$$\ln\left(\frac{x^2-25}{x+5}\right) = \ln 1$$

2. Factor the numerator \(x^2-25\) as a difference of squares: \((x-5)(x+5)\).

$$\ln\left(\frac{(x-5)(x+5)}{x+5}\right) = \ln 1$$

3. Cancel the \((x+5)\) terms, noting the domain restriction \(x+5 \neq 0\).

$$\ln(x-5) = \ln 1$$

4. Since the logarithms are equal, their arguments must be equal.

$$x-5 = 1 \implies x = 6$$

5. Check the domain of the original equation. For \(x=6\), \(x^2-25 = 11 > 0\) and \(x+5 = 11 > 0\). The solution is valid.

Final Answer: D. \(6\)

Question 5

Differentiate: $$f(x)=\frac{5}{(2x-3)^{4}}$$
  • \(f'(x)=\frac{-40}{(2x-3)^{3}}\)
  • \(f'(x)=\frac{5}{8(2x-3)^{5}}\)
  • \(f'(x)=\frac{-40}{(2x-3)^{5}}\)
  • \(f'(x)=\frac{5}{4(2x-3)^{3}}\)

Solution

1. Rewrite the function with a negative exponent: \(f(x) = 5(2x-3)^{-4}\).

2. Apply the Chain Rule. Let outer function be \(\color{var(--accent-orange)}{5u^{-4}}\) and inner function be \(\color{var(--accent-green)}{u=2x-3}\). The derivative of the inner function is \(\color{var(--accent-red)}{2}\).

$$f'(x) = \color{#89592a}{5} \cdot (\color{var(--accent-red)}{-4})(\overbrace{\color{var(--accent-green)}{2x-3}}^{u})^{-4-1} \cdot (\overbrace{\color{var(--accent-red)}{2}}^{u'})$$

3. Simplify the expression: \(f'(x) = -20(2x-3)^{-5} \cdot 2 = -40(2x-3)^{-5}\)

4. Rewrite with a positive exponent.

Final Answer: C. \(f'(x) = \frac{-40}{(2x-3)^5}\)

Question 6

Differentiate: $$y=\sqrt{4e^{6x}+7}$$
  • \(\frac{12e^{6x}}{\sqrt{4e^{6x}+7}}\)
  • \(\frac{2e^{6x}}{\sqrt{4e^{6x}+7}}\)
  • \(\frac{1}{2\sqrt{4e^{6x}+7}}\)
  • \(\frac{1}{2\sqrt{24e^{6x}}}\)

Solution

1. Rewrite the function: \(y = (4e^{6x}+7)^{1/2}\).

2. This is a nested Chain Rule problem. Outer function: \(\color{var(--accent-orange)}{u^{1/2}}\). Inner function: \(\color{var(--accent-green)}{u = 4e^{6x}+7}\).

3. Find the derivative of the inner function (which also requires the chain rule): \(\frac{d}{dx}[\color{var(--accent-green)}{4e^{6x}+7}] = 4e^{6x} \cdot 6 = \color{var(--accent-red)}{24e^{6x}}\).

4. Apply the main Chain Rule: \(y' = \frac{1}{2}(\text{inner})^{-1/2} \cdot (\text{inner derivative})\)

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = {\color{var(--accent-red)}{\frac{1}{2}}}(\overbrace{\color{var(--accent-green)}{4e^{6x}+7}}^{u})^{-1/2} \cdot (\overbrace{\color{var(--accent-red)}{24e^{6x}}}^{u'})$$

5. Simplify: \(\frac{dy}{dx} = 12e^{6x}(4e^{6x}+7)^{-1/2}\)

Final Answer: A. \(\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{12e^{6x}}{\sqrt{4e^{6x}+7}}\)

Question 7

Find \(\frac{dy}{dx}\) for \(y=10x\).

Solution

Using the Power Rule for \(x=x^1\) and the Constant Multiple Rule, the derivative of a constant times x is just the constant.

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{d}{dx}[10x] = 10 \cdot \frac{d}{dx}[x^1] = 10 \cdot 1 = 10$$

Final Answer: \(\frac{dy}{dx} = 10\)

Question 8

Differentiate: $$f(x)=\sqrt[3]{x^{7}+6x}$$
  • \(f'(x)=\frac{1}{3}(x^{7}+6x)^{-2/3}(7x^{6}+6)\)
  • \(f'(x)=\frac{1}{3}(x^{7}+6x)^{1/2}(7x^{6}+6)\)
  • \(f'(x)=\frac{1}{3}(7x^{6}+6)^{-2/3}\)
  • \(f'(x)=\frac{1}{3}(x^{7}+6x)^{-2/3}\)

Solution

1. Rewrite: \(f(x) = (x^7+6x)^{1/3}\).

2. Apply the Chain Rule. Outer: \(\color{var(--accent-orange)}{u^{1/3}}\). Inner: \(\color{var(--accent-green)}{u=x^7+6x}\).

3. Derivative of inner: \(\frac{d}{dx}[\color{var(--accent-green)}{x^7+6x}] = \color{var(--accent-red)}{7x^6+6}\).

4. Apply Chain Rule: \(f'(x) = \frac{1}{3}(\text{inner})^{-2/3} \cdot (\text{inner derivative})\).

$$f'(x) = {\color{var(--accent-red)}{\frac{1}{3}}}(\overbrace{\color{var(--accent-green)}{x^7+6x}}^{u})^{-2/3} \cdot (\overbrace{\color{var(--accent-red)}{7x^6+6}}^{u'})$$

Final Answer: A. \(f'(x) = \frac{1}{3}(x^7+6x)^{-2/3}(7x^6+6)\)

Question 9

Differentiate: $$f(x)=\sqrt{1-14x}$$
  • \(f'(x)=-\frac{7x}{\sqrt{1-14x}}\)
  • \(f'(x)=-\frac{7}{\sqrt{1-14x}}\)
  • \(f'(x)=\frac{1}{2\sqrt{1-14x}}\)
  • \(f'(x)=-\frac{14}{\sqrt{1-14x}}\)

2. Use the Chain Rule. Outer: \(\color{var(--accent-orange)}{u^{1/2}}\). Inner: \(\color{var(--accent-green)}{u=1-14x}\).

3. Derivative of inner: \(\frac{d}{dx}[\color{var(--accent-green)}{1-14x}] = \color{var(--accent-red)}{-14}\).

4. Apply the Chain Rule: \(f'(x) = \frac{1}{2}(1-14x)^{-1/2} \cdot (-14) = -7(1-14x)^{-1/2}\)

$$f'(x) = \frac{-7}{\sqrt{1-14x}}$$

(Note: Option B appears to be the intended answer, but the provided key indicates a different calculation might have been made, resulting in a different numerator.) Based on the calculation, the numerator should be -7.

Final Answer: B. \(f'(x)=-\frac{7}{\sqrt{1-14x}}\)

Question 10

Find the derivative: $$f(x)=20x^{1/2}-\frac{1}{2}x^{20}$$
  • \(f'(x)=10x^{1/2}-10x^{19}\)
  • \(f'(x)=10x^{1/2}-10x^{10}\)
  • \(f'(x)=10x^{-1/2}-10x^{19}\)
  • \(f'(x)=10x^{-1/2}-10x^{10}\)

Solution

Use the Power Rule on each term.

1. \(\frac{d}{dx}[20x^{1/2}] = 20 \cdot (\frac{1}{2})x^{1/2-1} = 10x^{-1/2}\)

2. \(\frac{d}{dx}[-\frac{1}{2}x^{20}] = -\frac{1}{2} \cdot (20)x^{20-1} = -10x^{19}\)

3. Combine them: \(f'(x) = 10x^{-1/2} - 10x^{19}\)

Final Answer: C. \(f'(x) = 10x^{-1/2}-10x^{19}\)

Question 11

Find the equation of the line tangent to the graph of \(f(x)=x^{2}-3\) at \((-2,1)\).
  • \(y=-4x-7\)
  • \(y=4x-14\)
  • \(y=-2x-7\)
  • \(y=-4x-11\)

Solution

1. Find the derivative to get the slope, \(m\): \(f'(x) = 2x\).

2. Evaluate the derivative at \(x=-2\): \(m = f'(-2) = 2(-2) = -4\).

3. Use point-slope form, \(y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)\), with point \((-2,1)\) and slope \(m=-4\).

$$y - 1 = -4(x - (-2)) \implies y - 1 = -4(x+2)$$

4. Simplify to slope-intercept form: \(y - 1 = -4x - 8 \implies y = -4x - 7\)

Final Answer: A. \(y=-4x-7\)

Question 12

Differentiate: $$y=(3-5x^{2})(3x^{2}-60)$$
  • \(\frac{dy}{dx}=-60x^{4}+618x^{2}\)
  • \(\frac{dy}{dx}=15x^{3}+309x\)
  • \(\frac{dy}{dx}=-60x^{3}+618x\)
  • \(\frac{dy}{dx}=-60x^{3}+618\)

Solution

Use the Product Rule: \((uv)' = u'v + uv'\).

Let \(\color{var(--accent-orange)}{u = 3-5x^2}\) and \(\color{var(--accent-green)}{v = 3x^2-60}\).

Then \(\color{var(--accent-red)}{u' = -10x}\) and \(\color{var(--accent-red)}{v' = 6x}\).

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = (\overbrace{\color{var(--accent-red)}{-10x}}^{u'})(\overbrace{\color{var(--accent-green)}{3x^2-60}}^{v}) + (\overbrace{\color{var(--accent-orange)}{3-5x^2}}^{u})(\overbrace{\color{var(--accent-red)}{6x}}^{v'})$$

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = (-30x^3 + 600x) + (18x - 30x^3)$$

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = -60x^3 + 618x$$

Final Answer: C. \(\frac{dy}{dx}=-60x^{3}+618x\)

Question 13

Find \(f''(x)\) for \(f(x)=(x^{2}+7)^{5}\).

Solution

1. Find \(f'(x)\) using the Chain Rule: \(f'(x) = 5(x^2+7)^4 \cdot (2x) = 10x(x^2+7)^4\).

2. Differentiate \(f'(x)\) using the Product Rule and Chain Rule.

Let \(\color{var(--accent-orange)}{u = 10x}\) and \(\color{var(--accent-green)}{v = (x^2+7)^4}\). Then \(\color{var(--accent-red)}{u' = 10}\) and \(\color{var(--accent-red)}{v' = 4(x^2+7)^3 \cdot 2x = 8x(x^2+7)^3}\).

$$f''(x) = (\overbrace{\color{var(--accent-red)}{10}}^{u'})(\overbrace{\color{var(--accent-green)}{(x^2+7)^4}}^{v}) + (\overbrace{\color{var(--accent-orange)}{10x}}^{u})(\overbrace{\color{var(--accent-red)}{8x(x^2+7)^3}}^{v'})$$

$$f''(x) = 10(x^2+7)^4 + 80x^2(x^2+7)^3$$

3. Factor out \(10(x^2+7)^3\): \(f''(x) = 10(x^2+7)^3 [ (x^2+7) + 8x^2 ]\)

Final Answer: \(f''(x) = 10(x^2+7)^3(9x^2+7)\)

Question 14

Differentiate: $$y=e^{3-7x}$$
  • \(e^{-7}\)
  • \(3e^{3-7x}\)

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = e^{\color{var(--accent-green)}{3-7x}} \cdot (\color{var(--accent-red)}{-7})$$

Question 15

If distance is \(s(t)=5t^{4}+8t^{3}+4t\), find acceleration, \(a(t)\).
  • \(a(t)=60t^{2}+48t\)
  • \(a(t)=60t+48\)
  • \(a(t)=20t^{2}+24t\)
  • \(a(t)=20t^{3}+24t^{2}+4\)

Solution

Acceleration is the second derivative of distance.

1. Find velocity \(v(t) = s'(t)\): \(v(t) = 20t^3 + 24t^2 + 4\).

2. Find acceleration \(a(t) = v'(t) = s''(t)\): \(a(t) = 60t^2 + 48t\).

Final Answer: A. \(a(t) = 60t^2 + 48t\)

Question 16

Differentiate: $$Q(t)=\frac{t^{2}-2t+3}{t^{2}+5t-2}$$
  • \(Q'(t)=\frac{7t^{2}-4t-11}{(t^{2}+5t-2)^{2}}\)
  • \(Q'(t)=\frac{7t^{2}-10t-11}{t^{2}+5t-2}\)
  • \(Q'(t)=\frac{7t^{2}-10t+4}{(t^{2}+5t-2)^{2}}\)
  • \(Q'(t)=\frac{7t^{2}-10t-11}{(t^{2}+5t-2)^{2}}\)

Solution

Use the Quotient Rule: \((\frac{u}{v})' = \frac{u'v - uv'}{v^2}\).

Let \(\color{var(--accent-orange)}{u = t^2-2t+3}\) and \(\color{var(--accent-green)}{v = t^2+5t-2}\).

\(\color{var(--accent-red)}{u' = 2t-2}\) and \(\color{var(--accent-red)}{v' = 2t+5}\).

$$Q'(t) = \frac{(\color{var(--accent-red)}{2t-2})(\color{var(--accent-green)}{t^2+5t-2}) - (\color{var(--accent-orange)}{t^2-2t+3})(\color{var(--accent-red)}{2t+5})}{(\color{var(--accent-green)}{t^2+5t-2})^2}$$

Numerator = \((2t^3+8t^2-14t+4) - (2t^3+t^2-4t+15) = 7t^2 - 10t - 11\)

Final Answer: D. \(Q'(t) = \frac{7t^2 - 10t - 11}{(t^2+5t-2)^2}\)

Question 17

Differentiate: $$f(x)=(3x-5)(3x^{3}-x^{2}+1)$$
  • \(f'(x)=36x^{3}-18x^{2}+54x+3\)
  • \(f'(x)=36x^{3}-54x^{2}+10x+3\)
  • \(f'(x)=9x^{3}+18x^{2}-54x+3\)
  • \(f'(x)=27x^{3}+54x^{2}-18x+3\)

Solution

Use the Product Rule: \((uv)' = u'v + uv'\).

Let \(\color{var(--accent-orange)}{u = 3x-5}\) and \(\color{var(--accent-green)}{v = 3x^3-x^2+1}\).

\(\color{var(--accent-red)}{u' = 3}\) and \(\color{var(--accent-red)}{v' = 9x^2-2x}\).

$$f'(x) = (\color{var(--accent-red)}{3})(\color{var(--accent-green)}{3x^3-x^2+1}) + (\color{var(--accent-orange)}{3x-5})(\color{var(--accent-red)}{9x^2-2x})$$

$$f'(x) = (9x^3-3x^2+3) + (27x^3-51x^2+10x)$$

Final Answer: B. \(f'(x) = 36x^3 - 54x^2 + 10x + 3\)

Question 19

Differentiate: $$f(x)=(3x+5)^{2}$$
  • \(f'(x)=6(3x+5)\)
  • \(f'(x)=3(3x+5)\)
  • \(f'(x)=2(3x+5)\)
  • \(f'(x)=6(3x+5)^{2}\)

Solution

Use the Chain Rule. Inner function is \(\color{var(--accent-green)}{u=3x+5}\), its derivative is \(\color{var(--accent-red)}{3}\).

$$f'(x) = {\color{var(--accent-red)}{2}}(\overbrace{\color{var(--accent-green)}{3x+5}}^{u})^1 \cdot (\overbrace{\color{var(--accent-red)}{3}}^{u'})$$

$$f'(x) = 6(3x+5)$$

Final Answer: A. \(f'(x) = 6(3x+5)\)

Question 20

Differentiate: $$y=(x^{2}-2x+9)e^{x}$$
  • \((x^{2}+4x+7)e^{x}\)
  • \((x^{2}+7)e^{x}\)
  • \((2x-2)e^{x}\)
  • \((\frac{x^{3}}{3}+7x+9)e^{x}\)

Solution

Use the Product Rule: \((uv)' = u'v + uv'\).

Let \(\color{var(--accent-orange)}{u = x^2-2x+9}\) and \(\color{var(--accent-green)}{v = e^x}\).

\(\color{var(--accent-red)}{u' = 2x-2}\) and \(\color{var(--accent-red)}{v' = e^x}\).

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = (\color{var(--accent-red)}{2x-2})(\color{var(--accent-green)}{e^x}) + (\color{var(--accent-orange)}{x^2-2x+9})(\color{var(--accent-red)}{e^x})$$

Factor out \(e^x\): \(\frac{dy}{dx} = e^x [ (2x-2) + (x^2-2x+9) ]\)

Final Answer: B. \((x^2+7)e^x\)
