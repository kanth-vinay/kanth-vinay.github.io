A Guide to Foundational Math Errors

By Vinay Kanth Rao Kodipelly

Algebra, Exponents, and Radicals

Distributing Exponents Over Addition

The Mistake

Thinking an exponent can be distributed across terms that are added or subtracted.

\((x + 5)^2 = x^2 + 5^2 = x^2 + 25\)

The Correction

Expand the expression and multiply all terms, typically using FOIL.

\((x + 5)^2 = (x + 5)(x + 5)\)

\(= x^2 + 5x + 5x + 25\)

\(= x^2 + 10x + 25\)

Distributing Radicals Over Addition

The Mistake

Incorrectly simplifying a square root containing a sum.

\(\sqrt{x^2 + 9} = \sqrt{x^2} + \sqrt{9} = x + 3\)

The Correction

A radical cannot be distributed over addition. The expression is often already in its simplest form.

\(\sqrt{x^2 + 9}\) cannot be simplified further.

Confusing the Base of a Negative Number

The Mistake

Treating \(-3^2\) and \((-3)^2\) as if they are the same calculation.

-3Â² = 9

The Correction

The exponent applies only to its immediate base. Parentheses are critical.

In \(-3^2\), the base is 3: \(-(3 \cdot 3) = -9\).

In \((-3)^2\), the base is -3: \((-3) \cdot (-3) = 9\).

Errors with Fractions

Incorrectly Cancelling Terms

The Mistake

Cancelling terms in a fraction that are part of a sum, not common factors.

\(\frac{2x + 8}{2} = x + 8\)

The Correction

You can only cancel common factors. Either divide every term in the numerator or factor first.

Method 1: \(\frac{2x}{2} + \frac{8}{2} = x + 4\)

Method 2: \(\frac{2(x+4)}{2} = x+4\)

Simplifying Complex Fractions

The Mistake

Becoming confused by the structure and attempting to cancel terms incorrectly.

The Correction

Rewrite as a division problem, then multiply by the reciprocal ("Keep-Change-Flip").

\(\frac{x/3}{2/x} = \frac{x}{3} \div \frac{2}{x} = \frac{x}{3} \cdot \frac{x}{2} = \frac{x^2}{6}\)

Errors in Solving Equations

Order of Operations with Distribution

The Mistake

Performing subtraction before multiplication when a negative is outside a parenthesis.

\(10 - 2(x - 3) = 8(x - 3) = 8x - 24\)

The Correction

Per PEMDAS, multiplication (distribution) must be done before subtraction. Distribute the -2 first.

\(10 - 2(x-3) = 10 - 2x + 6 = 16 - 2x\)

Forgetting "Plus or Minus" (Â±)

The Mistake

When solving an equation by taking a square root, only finding the positive solution.

If \(x^2 = 25\), then \(x = 5\).

The Correction

When taking the square root of both sides, you must account for both positive and negative solutions.

\(x^2 = 25 \implies \sqrt{x^2} = \pm\sqrt{25}\)

This gives two solutions: \(x = 5\) and \(x = -5\).

Dividing by a Variable

The Mistake

Dividing both sides by a variable, which can eliminate a valid solution.

For \(x^2 = 3x\), dividing by \(x\) gives \(x = 3\), losing a solution.

The Correction

Set the equation to zero and factor to find all solutions.

\(x^2 = 3x \implies x^2 - 3x = 0\)

\(x(x - 3) = 0\)

The two solutions are \(x=0\) and \(x=3\).
