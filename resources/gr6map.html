Part 2: Applied Reasoning Check

11.Geometry & Coordinate Reasoning

The point \((3,-4)\) is plotted on the coordinate plane. The point is reflected across the x-axis. What are the coordinates of the reflected point?

12.Number System & Operations

Which number is equivalent to \(\frac{5}{6} \div \frac{10}{3}\)?

13.Expressions & Equations

The length of a banner is \(7\) feet more than its width. Write an equation that represents the length, \(l\), in terms of the width, \(w\).

14.Geometry & Coordinate Reasoning

15.Number System & Operations

A baker has \(36.85\) pounds of flour. She uses \(12.478\) pounds. How many pounds remain?

16.Expressions & Equations

Maya uses the inequality \(\frac{3}{4}x - 5 > 13\) to decide how many bracelets, \(x\), she must sell to earn a profit. What is the least number of bracelets she must sell?

18.Expressions & Equations

Diego earns \($9\) for each lawn he mows. He uses the expression \(9x\) to calculate his earnings. What does the variable \(x\) represent?

19.Number System & Operations

A team lost \(6\) points in the first quarter and gained \(4\) points in the second quarter. Which expression describes the total change in points?

20.Expressions & Equations

A theater sold \(t\) tickets on Friday. On Saturday it sold \(8\) fewer than \(3\) times as many tickets as on Friday. Write an expression for the number of tickets sold on Saturday.

21.Ratios & Rates

Lena sells \(6\) sketchbooks for \($15\). Noah sells \(8\) sketchbooks for \($18\). Which statement correctly explains who has the lower cost per sketchbook?

22.Expressions & Equations

Select the two expressions equivalent to \(3(5+2)+6\).
