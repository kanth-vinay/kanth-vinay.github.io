Rule to give Samanvi: "Use a pie chart when you want to show how a whole is divided into parts. Use a bar chart when you want to compare how often something happens across categories. 2025 ASA Winning DNA — Applied to Samanvi's Project

Adapted for grades 7–9 · Survey-based project

The 2025 winners used methods well beyond 7th grade (z-score normalisation, ANOVA, Tukey HSD, Beer-Lambert Law). The methods are not applicable here. But the principles are fully reachable and are what distinguish a strong submission from an average one.

Show, don't tell

Winners documented the exact mechanics of data collection — which sticky notes, which shelf, which random number generator.

For Samanvi: Write one paragraph describing exactly how the survey was shared — which WhatsApp groups, community networks, on which dates, how many people were asked. Don't just say "survey was distributed."

Prove the math

Winners never ran a test without explicitly checking its underlying conditions first.

For Samanvi: Before conclusions, explicitly state: "This is a convenience sample, not a random sample. My findings describe this specific group and cannot be generalised to all children ages 4–18."

Control the environment

Winners engineered physical or procedural barriers to block confounders.

For Samanvi: Name the confounders: parental influence on younger children's responses, self-report bias, health-conscious families more likely to respond, and the anonymity preventing verification.

Embrace flaws honestly

Winners openly analysed what went wrong and proposed concrete improvements for future studies.

For Samanvi: Identify two specific improvements — e.g., adding a question about screen content type, or using stratified recruitment to balance age groups. Judges reward this thinking.

Practical significance

Winners asked not just "is there a difference?" but "does the difference actually matter in real life?"

For Samanvi: After any percentage comparison, ask and write: "What would this mean for a family trying to improve their child's sleep? Is this difference large enough to act on?"

Transparent sampling narrative

Winners left "zero ambiguity" about how their sample was acquired — every step documented.

For Samanvi: Include a short paragraph: "Data was collected via Google Form between May 24–27, 2026. The survey was shared through WhatsApp community groups and personal outreach. 33 complete responses were received." Include raw data as an appendix.
