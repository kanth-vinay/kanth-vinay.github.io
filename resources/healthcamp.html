About Us

Why We Created This Camp

Created by high school students with a strong interest in healthcare and medicine.

As high school students with a strong interest in healthcare and medicine, we wanted to create a program that introduces younger students to the medical field in an engaging and interactive way. While many students learn about science in the classroom, they do not often have opportunities to experience how medicine is applied in real-world settings.

We created this camp to provide middle school students with hands-on activities and opportunities to learn directly from healthcare professionals. By exploring different medical specialties and participating in interactive experiences, students can gain a deeper understanding of what healthcare careers involve.

Our inspiration came from our own experiences exploring medicine and realizing how valuable early exposure can be. We wanted to create a space where students can ask questions, learn new skills, and discover the many fields that make up modern healthcare.

Created by Sruthi Gullapalli and Nityasri Bhaskar

Goals and Impact

Our goal is to provide students with meaningful exposure to the medical field through interactive learning, hands-on activities, and engagement with healthcare professionals.

By fostering curiosity, critical thinking, and scientific understanding, we aim to inspire the next generation of healthcare leaders and empower students to explore future opportunities in medicine and related fields.

Event Details

Three Camp Sessions

Each camp day will feature a guest speaker who is a healthcare professional specializing in the day's topic. Students will have the opportunity to learn directly from experienced physicians, participate in hands-on activities, and explore different areas of medicine.

  1. Monday

    Introduction to Surgery & Anesthesiology

    Students will explore the world of surgery and learn how anesthesiologists help keep patients safe and comfortable during medical procedures. During our hands-on "Banana Surgery" activity, students will make a small incision in a banana and use beginner-friendly surgical tools to practice stitching techniques. This activity introduces basic surgical skills while teaching the importance of teamwork, precision, and patient care. A guest speaker working in anesthesiology will share insights about their career and role in the operating room.

  2. Wednesday

    Internal Medicine – The Human Body Court

    Students will become medical detectives in an interactive case study called "The Human Body Court." A fictional patient arrives at the hospital with several symptoms, and students must work together to determine which organ system is responsible. Teams will represent the heart, lungs, brain, and digestive system, using evidence to build arguments and defend their conclusions. Throughout the activity, new medical clues will be revealed, challenging students to think critically and adapt their reasoning just like real physicians. An internal medicine physician will discuss how doctors diagnose complex medical conditions and care for patients.

  3. Friday

    Oncology – The Tumor Biopsy Challenge

    Students will learn about cancer and how oncologists use science and technology to understand and treat diseases. Through a hands-on DNA extraction activity, students will analyze a mock "tumor biopsy" using strawberries to represent tissue samples. They will extract DNA, investigate a simulated genetic mutation, and identify the correct targeted treatment through a fun puzzle-solving challenge. This activity demonstrates how personalized medicine helps doctors choose treatments based on a patient's unique genetic information. A guest oncologist will share their experiences caring for patients and advancing cancer research.

Frequently Asked Questions

FAQ

Information for students and families considering the Medical Exploration Summer Camp.

Who can attend?

The camp is open to all students entering grades 5–8.

Is there a cost to attend?

No. The Medical Exploration Summer Camp is completely free for all participants.

What should I bring?

Students should bring:

  • A notebook
  • A pencil or pen
  • A positive attitude and willingness to learn
Is food provided?

Yes. Small snacks will be provided during each camp session.

Do I need prior medical knowledge?

No prior medical knowledge is required. All activities and lessons are designed for students in grades 5–8.

Will there be guest speakers?

Yes. Each camp day will feature a healthcare professional who will discuss their specialty, career path, and experiences in the medical field.
