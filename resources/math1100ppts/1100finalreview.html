The "Trap Detector" Review (30 Qs)

This rigorous review targets where students lose points. Don't just solve it—find the trap.

Packet 1: Coordinate Geometry & Lines Qs 1-8
Topics: Distance, Midpoint, Slope, Parallel/Perpendicular
1. Find the slope of a line PERPENDICULAR to the line containing \((2, 5)\) and \((-1, 4)\).
The Trap: Calculating the slope (\(1/3\)) and stopping there! You need the negative reciprocal. Flip it and switch the sign.
2. Which equation represents the relationship between Consecutive Interior Angles?
The Trap: Assuming "Parallel lines mean everything is equal." Consecutive Interior (Same-Side) angles are the odd ones out—they are supplementary (add to 180).
3. Midpoint Formula check: Find the midpoint of \((-4, 2)\) and \((6, -8)\).
The Trap: Subtracting instead of adding! Midpoint is an average. Add the x's and divide by 2. \((-4+6)/2 = 1\).
4. Find the distance between \((2, -3)\) and \((-4, 5)\).
The Trap: Sign errors. \((-4 - 2)^2 = (-6)^2 = 36\). \((5 - (-3))^2 = (8)^2 = 64\). \(\sqrt{36+64} = \sqrt{100} = 10\).
5. Line \(m\) has a slope of 0. Line \(n\) is perpendicular to \(m\). The slope of \(n\) is:
The Trap: Thinking "negative reciprocal of 0 is 0". Slope 0 is Horizontal. Perpendicular is Vertical (Undefined).
6. Given lines \(y = 3x + 2\) and \(2y = 6x - 4\). Are they:
The Trap: Not simplifying the second equation! \(2y = 6x - 4 \rightarrow y = 3x - 2\). Same slope (3), different y-intercept.
7. Angle 1 and Angle 2 are Vertical Angles. If \(m\angle 1 = (5x + 5)\) and \(m\angle 2 = (6x - 10)\), find \(x\).
The Trap: Setting them to add to 180. Vertical angles are CONGRUENT (Equal). \(5x+5 = 6x-10 \rightarrow 15 = x\).
8. If two lines are parallel, consecutive interior angles are:
The Trap: Forgetting the "C" rule. Consecutive (C-shaped) angles add to 180.
Packet 2: Logic & Reasoning Qs 9-16
Topics: Inverse/Converse/Contrapositive, Laws of Logic
9. Which symbolic statement represents the INVERSE of \(p \rightarrow q\)?
The Trap: Confusing Inverse with Converse.
Inverse: Insert "NOT" (tildes).
Converse: Change Order (flip letters).
10. To prove the statement "All prime numbers are odd" is FALSE, which counterexample works?
The Trap: A counterexample must satisfy the "If" (it must be Prime) but fail the "Then" (it is NOT odd). 2 is the only even prime.
11. "If p then q. If q then r. Therefore, if p then r." This is an example of:
The Trap: Mixing up the laws. Syllogism is the "Chain Rule" (linking 3 parts). Detachment only has 2 steps (If p->q. p happens. Therefore q).
12. What is the CONTRAPOSITIVE of "If today is Monday, then I have school"?
The Trap: Contrapositive does TWO things: Swaps order AND Negates both. (Not q -> Not p).
13. Which symbol represents a Disjunction ("OR")?
The Trap: V stands for "Vel" (Latin for OR). The up-caret (∧) is AND.
14. A Biconditional Statement ("p if and only if q") is true when:
The Trap: Biconditional means the arrow goes BOTH ways. Both directions must work.
15. In a Venn Diagram, the area where two circles overlap represents:
The Trap: Overlap means it is in Circle A AND Circle B.
16. If \(p \rightarrow q\) is true, and \(q\) is true, what can we conclude?
The Trap: This is the "Converse Error". Just because the result (q) happened doesn't mean p caused it. (If it rains -> grass is wet. Grass is wet -> maybe sprinkler?)
Packet 3: Relationships & Congruence Qs 17-23
Topics: Inequalities, Ordering Sides, SSS/SAS/ASA/AAS/HL
17. Two sides of a triangle measure 8 and 11. Which inequality shows the possible length of side \(x\)?
The Trap: The "Equal To" sign! The side cannot be equal to the sum or difference, or the triangle collapses into a flat line. It must be a strict inequality (<).
18. In \(\triangle ABC\), \(\angle A = 50^\circ\) and \(\angle B = 60^\circ\). Which is the longest side?
The Trap: Guessing without finding the 3rd angle! \(\angle C = 180 - (50+60) = 70^\circ\). Since \(\angle C\) is largest, the side opposite it (\(\overline{AB}\)) is longest.
19. Which of these is NOT a valid congruence theorem?
The Trap: SSA (Side-Side-Angle) spells a bad word backwards and is never a valid proof (unless it's a Right Triangle, then we call it HL).
20. In an isosceles triangle, the vertex angle is \(50^\circ\). What is the measure of one base angle?
The Trap: Remembering to subtract from 180 first. \((180 - 50) / 2 = 130 / 2 = 65\).
21. What reason justifies \(\overline{AB} \cong \overline{AB}\)?
The Trap: Reflexive is "like looking in a mirror" (A=A). Symmetric is flipping (If A=B then B=A).
22. An exterior angle of a triangle is \(120^\circ\). One of the remote interior angles is \(50^\circ\). What is the other remote interior angle?
The Trap: Exterior Angle = Sum of Two Remote Interior Angles. \(120 = 50 + x \rightarrow x = 70\).
23. In \(\triangle ABC\), \(\angle C = 90^\circ\). The longest side must be:
The Trap: The hypotenuse is opposite the right angle. \(\overline{AB}\) is opposite \(C\).
Packet 4: Similarity & Trigonometry Qs 24-30
Topics: Pythagorean Thm, Special Right Triangles, SOH CAH TOA
24. In a \(30-60-90\) triangle, if the hypotenuse is 10, what is the longer leg?
The Trap: Confusing \(\sqrt{2}\) and \(\sqrt{3}\).
\(30-60-90\) uses \(\sqrt{3}\) (3 numbers).
\(45-45-90\) uses \(\sqrt{2}\) (2 numbers).
Hypotenuse = 10 \(\to\) Short Leg = 5 \(\to\) Long Leg = \(5\sqrt{3}\).
25. \(\sin(A)\) is always equal to:
The Trap: Forgetting Cofunctions. Sine and Cosine are "Co" (Complementary) functions. \(\sin(20) = \cos(70)\).
26. Which set of lengths forms a Right Triangle?
The Trap: Assuming numbers that "look nice" work. You must check \(a^2 + b^2 = c^2\).
\(8^2 + 15^2 = 64 + 225 = 289\).
\(17^2 = 289\). It works.
27. If the legs of a 45-45-90 triangle are 7, what is the hypotenuse?
The Trap: Doubling the leg instead of multiplying by \(\sqrt{2}\). Pattern: \(x, x, x\sqrt{2}\).
28. Which trig function compares Opposite and Adjacent sides?
The Trap: Mixing up SOH CAH TOA. TOA = Tangent Opposite Adjacent.
29. Find the geometric mean of 4 and 9.
The Trap: Finding the Arithmetic Mean (Average). Geometric Mean is \(\sqrt{4 \times 9} = \sqrt{36} = 6\).
30. If two triangles are similar, the ratio of their perimeters is equal to:
The Trap: Confusing Perimeter Ratio (1D) with Area Ratio (2D). Perimeter uses \(k\). Area uses \(k^2\).
