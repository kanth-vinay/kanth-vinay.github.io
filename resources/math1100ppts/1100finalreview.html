Problem 1

Use the graph of $F$ below to find the given limit. When necessary, state that the limit does not exist.

$$ \lim_{x\rightarrow-5}F(x) $$

Solution

To find the limit as $x \to -5$, we look at the graph near $x=-5$.

  • Left Approach: As we follow the curve from the left towards -5, the y-values approach the height of the open circle, which is 3.
  • Right Approach: As we follow the line from the right towards -5, the y-values also approach the height of the open circle, 3.

Since both sides approach the same value:

$$ \lim_{x\rightarrow-5}F(x) = 3 $$

(Note: The solid dot at $(-5, 7)$ represents the function value $F(-5)$, but it does not affect the limit.)

Problem 2

Use the graph of $F$ below to find the given limit. When necessary, state that the limit does not exist.

$$ \lim_{x\rightarrow5}F(x) $$

Solution

Look at the behavior near $x=5$.

  • From the left, the line goes up to the point $(5, 6)$.
  • From the right, the line comes down from the point $(5, 6)$.

Since the graph connects continuously at $x=5$ (there is no break or jump), the limit is simply the value at that point.

$$ \lim_{x\rightarrow5}F(x) = 6 $$

Problem 3

Find the relative extrema of the function, if they exist.

$$ f(x)=x^{3}-12x-3 $$

Solution

1. Find the derivative:

$$ f'(x) = 3x^2 - 12 $$

2. Find critical numbers ($f'(x)=0$):

$$ 3x^2 - 12 = 0 \implies 3(x^2-4) = 0 \implies x = \pm 2 $$

3. Test points (Second Derivative Test):

$$ f''(x) = 6x $$
  • At $x=-2$: $f''(-2) = -12 < 0$ (Concave Down $\to$ Relative Max)
  • At $x=2$: $f''(2) = 12 > 0$ (Concave Up $\to$ Relative Min)

4. Find $y$-values:

  • Max at $(-2, 13)$: $f(-2) = -8 + 24 - 3 = 13$
  • Min at $(2, -19)$: $f(2) = 8 - 24 - 3 = -19$

Problem 4

Find the derivative of the function.

$$ y=\ln(3x^{3}-x^{2}) $$

Solution

Use the chain rule for natural logs: $\frac{d}{dx}[\ln(u)] = \frac{u'}{u}$.

Here, $u = 3x^3 - x^2$ and $u' = 9x^2 - 2x$.

$$ y' = \frac{9x^2 - 2x}{3x^3 - x^2} $$

Simplifying by factoring out $x$:

$$ y' = \frac{x(9x - 2)}{x^2(3x - 1)} = \frac{9x - 2}{x(3x - 1)} $$

Problem 5

Differentiate.

$$ f(x)=(4x^{2}+6)^{4} $$

Solution

Use the General Power Rule (Chain Rule):

$$ \frac{d}{dx}[u^n] = n \cdot u^{n-1} \cdot u' $$

Let $u = 4x^2 + 6$, then $u' = 8x$.

$$ f'(x) = 4(4x^2+6)^3 \cdot (8x) $$ $$ f'(x) = 32x(4x^2+6)^3 $$

Problem 6

Differentiate.

$$ f(x)=x^{8}-9e^{7x} $$

Solution

Differentiate term by term using the Power Rule and Chain Rule for exponents.

1. Derivative of $x^8$ is $8x^7$.

2. Derivative of $-9e^{7x}$ is $-9 \cdot e^{7x} \cdot 7 = -63e^{7x}$.

$$ f'(x) = 8x^7 - 63e^{7x} $$

Problem 7

Differentiate the function.

$$ y=\frac{4x^{2}-3}{7x^{3}+2} $$

Solution

Use the Quotient Rule: $\left(\frac{f}{g}\right)' = \frac{f'g - fg'}{g^2}$.

Let $f = 4x^2-3 \implies f' = 8x$.

Let $g = 7x^3+2 \implies g' = 21x^2$.

$$ y' = \frac{(8x)(7x^3+2) - (4x^2-3)(21x^2)}{(7x^3+2)^2} $$

Expand numerator:

$$ y' = \frac{56x^4 + 16x - (84x^4 - 63x^2)}{(7x^3+2)^2} $$ $$ y' = \frac{-28x^4 + 63x^2 + 16x}{(7x^3+2)^2} $$

Problem 8

Evaluate.

$$ \int\frac{51}{x}dx $$

Solution

Recall that $\int \frac{1}{x} dx = \ln|x| + C$.

$$ 51 \int \frac{1}{x} dx = 51 \ln|x| + C $$

Problem 9

Evaluate.

$$ \int25x^{1/5}dx $$

Solution

Use the Power Rule for Integration: $\int x^n dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1}$.

$n = 1/5$, so $n+1 = 6/5$.

$$ 25 \cdot \frac{x^{6/5}}{6/5} + C $$ $$ = 25 \cdot \frac{5}{6} x^{6/5} + C $$ $$ = \frac{125}{6} x^{6/5} + C $$

Problem 10

Evaluate.

$$ \int3x^{8}dx $$

Solution

Use the Power Rule.

$$ 3 \int x^8 dx = 3 \cdot \frac{x^9}{9} + C $$ $$ = \frac{x^9}{3} + C $$

Problem 11

Find the average value over the given interval.

$$ y=3x^{4}; \quad [-2,2] $$

Solution

Formula: $Avg = \frac{1}{b-a} \int_a^b f(x) dx$.

Here $a=-2, b=2$, so $b-a = 4$.

$$ Avg = \frac{1}{4} \int_{-2}^{2} 3x^4 dx $$ $$ = \frac{1}{4} \left[ \frac{3x^5}{5} \right]_{-2}^{2} $$ $$ = \frac{3}{20} [ (2)^5 - (-2)^5 ] = \frac{3}{20} [ 32 - (-32) ] $$ $$ = \frac{3}{20} (64) = \frac{3 \cdot 16}{5} = \frac{48}{5} = 9.6 $$

Problem 12

Find the area under the given curve over the indicated interval.

$$ y=x^{2}+3; \quad [0,2] $$

Solution

The area is given by the definite integral:

$$ A = \int_{0}^{2} (x^2 + 3) dx $$ $$ = \left[ \frac{x^3}{3} + 3x \right]_0^2 $$ $$ = \left( \frac{2^3}{3} + 3(2) \right) - (0) $$ $$ = \frac{8}{3} + 6 = \frac{8}{3} + \frac{18}{3} = \frac{26}{3} $$

Problem 13

Evaluate the indefinite integral.

$$ \int(2x+4)^{2}dx $$

Solution

Method 1: Expansion

$$ (2x+4)^2 = 4x^2 + 16x + 16 $$ $$ \int (4x^2 + 16x + 16) dx = \frac{4x^3}{3} + 8x^2 + 16x + C $$

Method 2: U-Substitution

Let $u=2x+4$, $du=2dx \implies dx=du/2$.

$$ \int u^2 \frac{du}{2} = \frac{1}{2} \frac{u^3}{3} = \frac{(2x+4)^3}{6} + C $$

(Both answers are correct and equivalent.)

Problem 14

Find the area under the graph of the function over the interval given.

$$ y=\frac{1}{\sqrt{x}}; \quad [1,4] $$

Solution

Rewrite as a power: $y = x^{-1/2}$.

$$ A = \int_{1}^{4} x^{-1/2} dx $$

Integrate ($n = -1/2$, so $n+1 = 1/2$):

$$ = \left[ \frac{x^{1/2}}{1/2} \right]_1^4 = \left[ 2\sqrt{x} \right]_1^4 $$ $$ = 2\sqrt{4} - 2\sqrt{1} = 2(2) - 2(1) = 4 - 2 = 2 $$

Problem 15

Find the particular solution determined by the given condition.

$$ f'(x)=6x^{2}-4x+9; \quad f(1)=25 $$

Solution

Integrate the derivative to obtain the general form of the function:

$$ f(x)=\int (6x^2 - 4x + 9)\,dx $$ $$ f(x)=2x^{3} - 2x^{2} + 9x + C $$

Use the initial condition \( f(1)=25 \):

$$ 2(1)^3 - 2(1)^2 + 9(1) + C = 25 $$ $$ 2 - 2 + 9 + C = 25 $$ $$ 9 + C = 25 \implies C = 16 $$

Final Answer:

$$ f(x)=2x^{3} - 2x^{2} + 9x + 16 $$

Problem 16

Solve.

$$ \frac{dy}{dx}=7x^{6}y $$

Solution

Separate variables ($y$ on left, $x$ on right):

$$ \frac{1}{y} dy = 7x^6 dx $$

Integrate both sides:

$$ \int \frac{1}{y} dy = \int 7x^6 dx $$ $$ \ln|y| = x^7 + C $$

Solve for $y$ by exponentiating:

$$ |y| = e^{x^7+C} = e^C e^{x^7} $$ $$ y = Ce^{x^7} $$

Problem 17

Find the particular solution determined by the given condition.

$$ f'(x)=6x^{2}-4x+18; \quad f(1)=22 $$

Solution

Integrate $f'(x)$:

$$ f(x) = \int (6x^2 - 4x + 18) dx $$ $$ f(x) = 2x^3 - 2x^2 + 18x + C $$

Apply condition $f(1) = 22$:

$$ 2(1)^3 - 2(1)^2 + 18(1) + C = 22 $$ $$ 2 - 2 + 18 + C = 22 $$ $$ 18 + C = 22 \implies C = 4 $$

Solution: $f(x) = 2x^3 - 2x^2 + 18x + 4$

Problem 18

Find (a) the general solution and (b) the particular solution for the given initial condition.

$$ y'=3x^{2}; \quad y(0)=-5 $$

Solution

(a) General Solution:

$$ y = \int 3x^2 dx = x^3 + C $$

(b) Particular Solution:

Plug in $x=0, y=-5$:

$$ -5 = 0^3 + C \implies C = -5 $$ $$ y = x^3 - 5 $$

Problem 19

For the function $z=8x^{3}-3y^{2}+3xy$, find:

$$ \frac{\partial z}{\partial x}, \quad \frac{\partial z}{\partial y}, \quad \frac{\partial}{\partial x}z(-4,-5), \quad \text{and} \quad \frac{\partial}{\partial y}z(-4,-5). $$

Solution

1. Partial with respect to x (treat y as constant):

$$ \frac{\partial z}{\partial x} = 24x^2 + 3y $$

2. Partial with respect to y (treat x as constant):

$$ \frac{\partial z}{\partial y} = -6y + 3x $$

3. Evaluate at (-4, -5):

$$ \frac{\partial z}{\partial x}(-4,-5) = 24(-4)^2 + 3(-5) = 24(16) - 15 = 384 - 15 = 369 $$ $$ \frac{\partial z}{\partial y}(-4,-5) = -6(-5) + 3(-4) = 30 - 12 = 18 $$

Problem 20

Find the minimum of $f$ subject to the given constraint.

$$ f(x,y)=x^{2}+4y^{2}+6 \quad \text{subject to} \quad 2x-8y=20 $$

Solution

1. Solve constraint for x:

$$ 2x = 8y + 20 \implies x = 4y + 10 $$

2. Substitute into f:

$$ g(y) = (4y+10)^2 + 4y^2 + 6 $$ $$ g(y) = 16y^2 + 80y + 100 + 4y^2 + 6 = 20y^2 + 80y + 106 $$

3. Minimize g(y):

$$ g'(y) = 40y + 80 = 0 \implies y = -2 $$

Find x: $x = 4(-2) + 10 = 2$.

4. Minimum Value:

$$ f(2, -2) = (2)^2 + 4(-2)^2 + 6 = 4 + 16 + 6 = 26 $$

Problem 21

For the function $z=-7x^{3}-3y^{2}-8xy$, find partial derivatives and evaluate at $(-5, -4)$.

Solution

1. Partials:

$$ \frac{\partial z}{\partial x} = -21x^2 - 8y $$ $$ \frac{\partial z}{\partial y} = -6y - 8x $$

2. Evaluate at (-5, -4):

$$ z_x = -21(-5)^2 - 8(-4) = -21(25) + 32 = -525 + 32 = -493 $$ $$ z_y = -6(-4) - 8(-5) = 24 + 40 = 64 $$

Problem 22

Find the relative maximum and minimum values.

$$ f(x,y)=x^{3}+y^{3}-18xy $$

Solution

1. Find Critical Points ($f_x = 0, f_y = 0$):

$$ f_x = 3x^2 - 18y = 0 \implies y = x^2/6 $$ $$ f_y = 3y^2 - 18x = 0 \implies x = y^2/6 $$

Substitute $y=x^2/6$ into $x=y^2/6$: $x = (x^2/6)^2/6 = x^4/216$.

$$ x^4 - 216x = 0 \implies x(x^3 - 216) = 0 $$

So $x=0$ (implies $y=0$) or $x=6$ (implies $y=6$).

2. Second Partials Test ($D = f_{xx}f_{yy} - (f_{xy})^2$):

$$ f_{xx} = 6x, \quad f_{yy} = 6y, \quad f_{xy} = -18 $$ $$ D(x,y) = 36xy - (-18)^2 = 36xy - 324 $$
  • At $(0,0)$: $D = -324 < 0 \implies$ Saddle Point.
  • At $(6,6)$: $D = 36(36) - 324 = 1296 - 324 > 0$. $f_{xx}(6,6)=36 > 0$.
  • Therefore, Relative Minimum at $(6,6)$.

Problem 24

Find the minimum of $f(x,y)=x^{2}-14x+y^{2}-16y$ subject to $2x+3y=12$.

Solution

We want to minimize

$$ f(x,y) = x^{2}-14x + y^{2}-16y $$

subject to the constraint

$$ 2x + 3y = 12. $$

1. Use the constraint to write $x$ in terms of $y$.

$$ 2x + 3y = 12 \implies 2x = 12 - 3y \implies x = 6 - \frac{3}{2}y. $$

2. Substitute into $f$ to get a single-variable function $g(y)$.

$$ g(y) = f\!\left(6 - \frac{3}{2}y,\;y\right) = (6 - \tfrac{3}{2}y)^2 - 14\left(6 - \tfrac{3}{2}y\right) + y^2 - 16y. $$

Simplifying gives

$$ g(y) = \frac{13}{4}y^2 - 13y - 48. $$

3. Minimize $g(y)$ by taking the derivative and setting it equal to zero.

$$ g'(y) = \frac{13}{2}y - 13 = 0 \implies \frac{13}{2}y = 13 \implies y = 2. $$

Use the constraint to find $x$:

$$ 2x + 3(2) = 12 \implies 2x + 6 = 12 \implies 2x = 6 \implies x = 3. $$

4. Evaluate $f$ at this point to get the minimum value.

$$ f(3,2) = 3^2 - 14(3) + 2^2 - 16(2) = 9 - 42 + 4 - 32 = -61. $$

Answer: The minimum occurs at $(x,y) = (3,2)$ and the minimum value is

$$ f_{\min} = -61. $$

Find the four second-order partial derivatives ($f_{xx}, f_{xy}, f_{yx}, f_{yy}$).

Problem 26

Find the relative maximum and minimum values.

$$ f(x,y)=x^{2}+y^{2}+4x-8y $$

Solution

Find Critical Points:

$$ f_x = 2x + 4 = 0 \implies x = -2 $$ $$ f_y = 2y - 8 = 0 \implies y = 4 $$

Critical Point is $(-2, 4)$.

Check Second Partials:

$$ f_{xx} = 2, \quad f_{yy} = 2, \quad f_{xy} = 0 $$ $$ D = (2)(2) - 0 = 4 > 0 $$

Since $D>0$ and $f_{xx} > 0$, this is a Relative Minimum.

Value: $f(-2,4) = 4 + 16 - 8 - 32 = -20$.

Problem 27

Find $f_{x}$ and $f_{y}$.

$$ f(x,y)=-6e^{6x-7y} $$

Solution

Use chain rule. Let $u = 6x - 7y$.

$$ f_x = -6 e^u \cdot \frac{\partial u}{\partial x} = -6 e^{6x-7y} \cdot (6) = -36e^{6x-7y} $$ $$ f_y = -6 e^u \cdot \frac{\partial u}{\partial y} = -6 e^{6x-7y} \cdot (-7) = 42e^{6x-7y} $$

Problem 28

Determine where the given function is concave up and where it is concave down.

$$ h(x)=\frac{4}{3}x^{3}-12x^{2}+10x+49 $$

Solution

Concavity depends on the second derivative $h''(x)$.

$$ h'(x) = 4x^2 - 24x + 10 $$ $$ h''(x) = 8x - 24 $$

Find inflection point ($h''=0$):

$$ 8x - 24 = 0 \implies x = 3 $$

Test intervals:

  • For $x < 3$ (e.g., $x=0$): $h''(0) = -24 < 0$ (Concave Down).
  • For $x > 3$ (e.g., $x=4$): $h''(4) = 8 > 0$ (Concave Up).

Problem 30

Determine the vertical asymptote(s) of the given function.

$$ R(x)=\frac{-3x^{2}}{x^{2}+10x-24} $$

Solution

Vertical asymptotes occur where the denominator is zero (and numerator is not).

Factor the denominator:

$$ x^2 + 10x - 24 = 0 $$ $$ (x + 12)(x - 2) = 0 $$

The zeros are $x = -12$ and $x = 2$.

Since the numerator $-3x^2$ is not zero at these points, both are vertical asymptotes.

Answer: $x = -12, \quad x = 2$

Problem 31

Determine the horizontal asymptote of the given function.

$$ R(x)=\frac{-3x^{2}+1}{x^{2}+8x-20} $$

Solution

Compare the degrees of the numerator and denominator.

  • Degree of top: 2 (from $-3x^2$)
  • Degree of bottom: 2 (from $x^2$)

Since degrees are equal, the Horizontal Asymptote is the ratio of leading coefficients.

$$ y = \frac{-3}{1} = -3 $$
