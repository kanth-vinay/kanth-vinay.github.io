Part I: Unconstrained Optimization (Problems 1–13)

Problem 1

Find the relative maximum and minimum values of \( f(x,y) = x^2 + xy + y^2 - 2y + 1 \).

Step 1: Find the Partial Derivatives

\( f_x = 2x + y \)

\( f_y = x + 2y - 2 \)

Step 2: Set Each Partial Derivative Equal to Zero

From \( f_x = 0 \): \( 2x + y = 0 \implies y = -2x \)

Substitute into \( f_y = 0 \): \( x + 2(-2x) - 2 = 0 \implies -3x = 2 \implies x = -\tfrac{2}{3} \)

\( y = -2(-\tfrac{2}{3}) = \tfrac{4}{3} \). Critical point: \( \left(-\tfrac{2}{3}, \tfrac{4}{3}\right) \)

Step 3: Second Derivative Test (D-Test)

\( f_{xx} = 2,\; f_{yy} = 2,\; f_{xy} = 1 \)

\( D = f_{xx} f_{yy} - (f_{xy})^2 = 4 - 1 = 3 \)

Since \( D = 3 > 0 \) and \( f_{xx} = 2 > 0 \), there is a Relative Minimum.

\( f\!\left(-\tfrac{2}{3}, \tfrac{4}{3}\right) = \tfrac{4}{9} - \tfrac{8}{9} + \tfrac{16}{9} - \tfrac{8}{3} + 1 = -\tfrac{1}{3} \)

Answer: No relative maximum. Relative minimum of \( -\frac{1}{3} \) at \( \left(-\frac{2}{3},\frac{4}{3}\right) \).

Problem 2

Find the relative maximum and minimum values of \( f(x,y) = 6xy - x^3 - 3y^2 \).

Step 1: Partial Derivatives

\( f_x = 6y - 3x^2 \)

\( f_y = 6x - 6y \)

Step 2: Critical Points

From \( f_y = 0 \): \( y = x \). Substitute into \( f_x = 0 \): \( 6x - 3x^2 = 0 \implies 3x(2-x) = 0 \)

So \( x = 0 \) or \( x = 2 \). Critical points: \( (0,0) \) and \( (2,2) \).

Step 3: D-Test

\( f_{xx} = -6x,\; f_{yy} = -6,\; f_{xy} = 6 \)

\( D = (-6x)(-6) - 36 = 36x - 36 \)

  • At \( (0,0) \): \( D = -36 < 0 \) → Saddle Point
  • At \( (2,2) \): \( D = 36 > 0 \) and \( f_{xx} = -12 < 0 \) → Relative Maximum

\( f(2,2) = 24 - 8 - 12 = 4 \)

Answer: Relative maximum of \( 4 \) at \( (2,2) \). No relative minimum.

Problem 3

Find the relative maximum and minimum values of \( f(x,y) = x^3 + y^3 - 27xy \).

Step 1: Partial Derivatives

\( f_x = 3x^2 - 27y \)

\( f_y = 3y^2 - 27x \)

Step 2: Critical Points

From \( f_x = 0 \): \( y = \frac{x^2}{9} \). Substitute into \( f_y = 0 \):

\( 3\left(\frac{x^2}{9}\right)^2 - 27x = 0 \implies \frac{x^4}{27} = 27x \implies x^4 = 729x \implies x(x^3 - 729) = 0 \)

So \( x = 0,\, y = 0 \) or \( x = 9,\, y = 9 \).

Step 3: D-Test

\( f_{xx} = 6x,\; f_{yy} = 6y,\; f_{xy} = -27 \)

\( D = 36xy - 729 \)

  • At \( (0,0) \): \( D = -729 < 0 \) → Saddle Point
  • At \( (9,9) \): \( D = 36(81) - 729 = 2187 > 0 \), \( f_{xx} = 54 > 0 \) → Relative Minimum

\( f(9,9) = 729 + 729 - 27(81) = 1458 - 2187 = -729 \)

Answer: No relative maximum. Relative minimum of \( -729 \) at \( (9,9) \).

Problem 4

Find the relative maximum and minimum values of \( f(x,y) = x^2 + y^2 - 8x + 20y - 14 \).

Step 1: Partial Derivatives

\( f_x = 2x - 8,\quad f_y = 2y + 20 \)

Step 2: Critical Point

\( 2x - 8 = 0 \implies x = 4 \). \( 2y + 20 = 0 \implies y = -10 \). Point: \( (4, -10) \)

Step 3: D-Test

\( f_{xx} = 2,\; f_{yy} = 2,\; f_{xy} = 0 \implies D = 4 > 0 \) and \( f_{xx} > 0 \) → Relative Minimum

\( f(4,-10) = 16 + 100 - 32 - 200 - 14 = -130 \)

Answer: No relative maximum. Relative minimum of \( -130 \) at \( (4,-10) \).

Problem 5

Find the relative maximum and minimum values of \( f(x,y) = x^2 + y^2 + 6x - 12y \).

Step 1: Partial Derivatives

\( f_x = 2x + 6,\quad f_y = 2y - 12 \)

Step 2: Critical Point

\( x = -3,\; y = 6 \). Point: \( (-3, 6) \)

Step 3: D-Test

\( f_{xx} = 2,\; f_{yy} = 2,\; f_{xy} = 0 \implies D = 4 > 0,\; f_{xx} > 0 \) → Relative Minimum

\( f(-3,6) = 9 + 36 - 18 - 72 = -45 \)

Answer: No relative maximum. Relative minimum of \( -45 \) at \( (-3,6) \).

Problem 6

Find the relative maximum and minimum values of \( f(x,y) = 10x^2 - 3y^2 \).

Step 1: Partial Derivatives

\( f_x = 20x,\quad f_y = -6y \)

Step 2: Critical Point

\( x = 0,\; y = 0 \). Point: \( (0,0) \)

Step 3: D-Test

\( f_{xx} = 20,\; f_{yy} = -6,\; f_{xy} = 0 \)

\( D = (20)(-6) - 0 = -120 < 0 \)

Since \( D < 0 \), \( (0,0) \) is a Saddle Point.

Answer: No relative maximum. No relative minimum.

Problem 7

Find the relative maximum and minimum values of \( f(x,y) = e^{7x^2 + 6y^2 + 10} \).

Step 1: Partial Derivatives (Chain Rule)

\( f_x = 14x\, e^{7x^2+6y^2+10} = 0 \implies x = 0 \)

\( f_y = 12y\, e^{7x^2+6y^2+10} = 0 \implies y = 0 \)

Step 2: Classification

Since \( e^u > 0 \) always, the exponential is never zero. The exponent \( 7x^2 + 6y^2 + 10 \) is minimized when \( x = 0, y = 0 \). Since \( e^u \) is increasing, \( f \) is minimized there too.

Answer: No relative maximum. Relative minimum of \( e^{10} \) at \( (0,0) \).

Problem 8

A company produces \( x \) thousand type A and \( y \) thousand type B solar panels. Revenue: \( R(x,y) = 5x + 7y \). Cost: \( C(x,y) = x^2 - 3xy + 6y^2 + 6x - 17y - 9 \). Find production levels that maximize profit.

Step 1: Profit Function

\( P = R - C = -x^2 + 3xy - 6y^2 - x + 24y + 9 \)

Step 2: Partial Derivatives

\( P_x = -2x + 3y - 1 = 0 \)

\( P_y = 3x - 12y + 24 = 0 \implies x = 4y - 8 \)

Step 3: Solve

\( -2(4y-8) + 3y - 1 = 0 \implies -5y + 15 = 0 \implies y = 3 \)

\( x = 4(3) - 8 = 4 \)

Step 4: D-Test

\( P_{xx} = -2,\; P_{yy} = -12,\; P_{xy} = 3 \implies D = 24 - 9 = 15 > 0,\; P_{xx} < 0 \) → Maximum

\( P(4,3) = -16 + 36 - 54 - 4 + 72 + 9 = 43 \)

Answer: Sell 4,000 type A and 3,000 type B for max profit of $43 million.

Problem 9

Profit is \( P(a,p) = 2ap + 150p - 20p^2 - \tfrac{1}{10}a^2 p - 100 \) where \( a \) = advertising (millions) and \( p \) = price. Find maximum profit.

Step 1: Partial Derivatives

\( P_a = 2p - \tfrac{1}{5}ap = 0 \implies p(2 - 0.2a) = 0 \). Since \( p \neq 0 \), \( a = 10 \).

\( P_p = 2a + 150 - 40p - 0.1a^2 = 0 \)

Step 2: Solve

Plug \( a = 10 \): \( 20 + 150 - 40p - 10 = 0 \implies p = 4 \)

\( P(10,4) = 80 + 600 - 320 - 40 - 100 = 220 \)

Answer: \( a = \$10 \) million, \( p = \$4 \). Maximum profit = $220 million.

Problem 10

An open-top rectangular container has volume \( 1715\;\text{ft}^3 \). Bottom costs $5/ft², sides cost $4/ft². Find dimensions minimizing cost.

Step 1: Set Up

Let \( L, W, H \) be dimensions. Constraint: \( LWH = 1715 \implies H = \frac{1715}{LW} \)

Cost = \( 5LW + 4 \cdot 2(LH + WH) = 5LW + \frac{13720}{W} + \frac{13720}{L} \)

Step 2: Partial Derivatives

\( C_L = 5W - \frac{13720}{L^2} = 0 \implies 5L^2 W = 13720 \)

\( C_W = 5L - \frac{13720}{W^2} = 0 \implies 5LW^2 = 13720 \)

Step 3: Solve

Dividing: \( \frac{L^2 W}{LW^2} = 1 \implies L = W \). Then \( 5L^3 = 13720 \implies L = 14 \).

\( H = \frac{1715}{196} = 8.75 \)

Answer: \( L = 14 \) ft, \( W = 14 \) ft, \( H = 8.75 \) ft.

Problem 11

Temperature on a plate: \( T(x,y) = x^2 + 2y^2 - 6x + 16y \). Find the minimum temperature. Is there a maximum?

Step 1: Partial Derivatives

\( T_x = 2x - 6 = 0 \implies x = 3 \)

\( T_y = 4y + 16 = 0 \implies y = -4 \)

Step 2: D-Test

\( T_{xx} = 2,\; T_{yy} = 4,\; T_{xy} = 0 \implies D = 8 > 0,\; T_{xx} > 0 \) → Minimum

\( T(3,-4) = 9 + 32 - 18 - 64 = -41 \)

Answer: Minimum temperature is \( -41°\text{F} \) at \( (3,-4) \). There is no maximum temperature (the plate extends infinitely).

Problem 12

Population density \( P(x,y) = -25x^2 - 20y^2 + 350x + 520y + 180 \). Find the maximum population density.

Step 1: Partial Derivatives

\( P_x = -50x + 350 = 0 \implies x = 7 \)

\( P_y = -40y + 520 = 0 \implies y = 13 \)

Step 2: D-Test

\( P_{xx} = -50,\; P_{yy} = -40,\; P_{xy} = 0 \implies D = 2000 > 0,\; P_{xx} < 0 \) → Maximum

\( P(7,13) = -1225 - 3380 + 2450 + 6760 + 180 = 4785 \)

Answer: Maximum density is 4785 people/mi² at \( (7, 13) \).

Problem 13

Find relative maxima, minima, and saddle points of \( f(x,y) = 18y^2 + x^2 - x^2 y \).

Step 1: Partial Derivatives

\( f_x = 2x - 2xy = 2x(1 - y) \)

\( f_y = 36y - x^2 \)

Step 2: Critical Points

From \( f_x = 0 \): \( x = 0 \) or \( y = 1 \).

Case 1: \( x = 0 \implies 36y = 0 \implies y = 0 \). Point: \( (0,0) \).

Case 2: \( y = 1 \implies 36 - x^2 = 0 \implies x = \pm 6 \). Points: \( (6,1), (-6,1) \).

Step 3: D-Test

\( f_{xx} = 2 - 2y,\; f_{yy} = 36,\; f_{xy} = -2x \)

\( D = 36(2-2y) - 4x^2 \)

  • At \( (0,0) \): \( D = 72 > 0,\; f_{xx} = 2 > 0 \) → Relative Minimum
  • At \( (6,1) \): \( D = 0 - 144 = -144 < 0 \) → Saddle Point
  • At \( (-6,1) \): \( D = 0 - 144 = -144 < 0 \) → Saddle Point

Answer: No relative maxima. Relative minimum at \( (0,0) \). Saddle points at \( (6,1) \) and \( (-6,1) \).

Part II: Lagrange Multipliers (Problems 14–18)

Problem 14

Find the extremum of \( f(x,y) = xy \) subject to \( 13x + y = 6 \).

Step 1: Lagrange Function

\( F(x,y,\lambda) = xy - \lambda(13x + y - 6) \)

Step 2: Partial Derivatives

\( F_x = y - 13\lambda = 0 \implies \lambda = \frac{y}{13} \)

\( F_y = x - \lambda = 0 \implies \lambda = x \)

\( F_\lambda = -(13x + y - 6) = 0 \)

Step 3: Solve

Set \( x = \frac{y}{13} \implies y = 13x \). Constraint: \( 13x + 13x = 6 \implies x = \frac{3}{13},\; y = 3 \).

Why is this a maximum? The constraint \( 13x + y = 6 \) is a line. As we move along it, \( y = 6 - 13x \), so \( f = x(6-13x) = 6x - 13x^2 \), a downward parabola. Its vertex is the highest point, so this must be a maximum. You can verify: \( f(0,6) = 0 \) and \( f(1,-7) = -7 \), both less than \( \frac{9}{13} \).

Answer: Maximum value of \( \frac{9}{13} \) at \( \left(\frac{3}{13}, 3\right) \).

Problem 15

Find the extremum of \( f(x,y) = x^2 + y^2 \) subject to \( 4x + 4y = 64 \).

Step 1: Lagrange Function

\( F = x^2 + y^2 - \lambda(4x + 4y - 64) \)

Step 2: Partial Derivatives

\( F_x = 2x - 4\lambda = 0 \implies x = 2\lambda \)

\( F_y = 2y - 4\lambda = 0 \implies y = 2\lambda \)

Step 3: Solve

\( x = y \). Constraint: \( 8x = 64 \implies x = 8,\; y = 8 \).

Why is this a minimum? \( f = x^2 + y^2 \) measures squared distance from the origin. The constraint is a line. Moving along the line away from \( (8,8) \), the distance to the origin grows without bound (e.g., \( f(16,0) = 256 \), \( f(0,16) = 256 \)). So the critical point gives the shortest distance — a minimum.

Answer: Minimum value of \( 128 \) at \( (8,8) \).

Problem 16

Find the extremum of \( f(x,y) = 4x^2 + 2y^2 - xy \) subject to \( x + y = 14 \).

Step 1: Lagrange Function

\( F = 4x^2 + 2y^2 - xy - \lambda(x + y - 14) \)

Step 2: Partial Derivatives

\( F_x = 8x - y - \lambda = 0 \implies \lambda = 8x - y \)

\( F_y = 4y - x - \lambda = 0 \implies \lambda = 4y - x \)

Step 3: Solve

\( 8x - y = 4y - x \implies 9x = 5y \implies y = \frac{9x}{5} \)

Constraint: \( x + \frac{9x}{5} = 14 \implies \frac{14x}{5} = 14 \implies x = 5,\; y = 9 \)

Why is this a minimum? Substitute the constraint: \( y = 14-x \), so \( f = 4x^2 + 2(14-x)^2 - x(14-x) = 7x^2 - 70x + 392 \). This is an upward parabola (\( a = 7 > 0 \)), so its vertex is a minimum.

\( f(5,9) = 100 + 162 - 45 = 217 \)

Answer: Minimum value of \( 217 \) at \( (5,9) \).

Problem 17

Find the extremum of \( f(x,y) = 3y^2 - 4x^2 \) subject to \( 4x + 2y = 32 \).

Step 1: Lagrange Function

\( F = 3y^2 - 4x^2 - \lambda(4x + 2y - 32) \)

Step 2: Partial Derivatives

\( F_x = -8x - 4\lambda = 0 \implies \lambda = -2x \)

\( F_y = 6y - 2\lambda = 0 \implies \lambda = 3y \)

Step 3: Solve

\( -2x = 3y \implies x = -\frac{3y}{2} \)

Constraint: \( 4(-\frac{3y}{2}) + 2y = 32 \implies -4y = 32 \implies y = -8 \)

\( x = -\frac{3(-8)}{2} = 12 \)

Why is this a minimum? Substitute \( y = 16 - 2x \): \( f = 3(16-2x)^2 - 4x^2 = 8x^2 - 192x + 768 \). Since the coefficient of \( x^2 \) is positive (\( 8 > 0 \)), this parabola opens upward, so the vertex is a minimum.

\( f(12,-8) = 3(64) - 4(144) = 192 - 576 = -384 \)

Answer: Minimum value of \( -384 \) at \( (12,-8) \).

Problem 18

Find the extremum of \( f(x,y,z) = x^2 + y^2 + z^2 \) subject to \( 2x + 3y - 2z = 17 \).

Step 1: Lagrange System

\( F_x = 2x - 2\lambda = 0 \implies x = \lambda \)

\( F_y = 2y - 3\lambda = 0 \implies y = \frac{3\lambda}{2} \)

\( F_z = 2z + 2\lambda = 0 \implies z = -\lambda \)

Step 2: Solve

Constraint: \( 2\lambda + \frac{9\lambda}{2} + 2\lambda = 17 \implies \frac{17\lambda}{2} = 17 \implies \lambda = 2 \)

\( x = 2,\; y = 3,\; z = -2 \)

Why is this a minimum? \( f = x^2 + y^2 + z^2 \) is the squared distance from the origin. The constraint is a plane. The closest point on a plane to the origin is unique and gives a minimum. There is no maximum since the plane extends infinitely.

\( f(2,3,-2) = 4 + 9 + 4 = 17 \)

Answer: Minimum value of \( 17 \) at \( (2, 3, -2) \).

Part III: Word Problems (Problems 19–25)

Problem 19

Of all the numbers whose sum is 34, find the two that have the maximum product.

Step 1: Set Up

Maximize \( f(x,y) = xy \) subject to \( x + y = 34 \).

Step 2: Substitution Method

\( y = 34 - x \implies P(x) = x(34 - x) = 34x - x^2 \)

\( P'(x) = 34 - 2x = 0 \implies x = 17,\; y = 17 \)

Why is this a maximum? \( P(x) = -x^2 + 34x \) is a downward parabola. Its vertex is the highest point — a maximum.

Answer: The two numbers are 17 and 17.

Problem 20

Of all numbers whose difference is 18, find the two that have the minimum product.

Step 1: Set Up

Minimize \( f(x,y) = xy \) subject to \( x - y = 18 \).

Step 2: Substitution

\( y = x - 18 \implies P(x) = x(x-18) = x^2 - 18x \)

\( P'(x) = 2x - 18 = 0 \implies x = 9,\; y = -9 \)

Why is this a minimum? \( P(x) = x^2 - 18x \) is an upward parabola. Its vertex is the lowest point — a minimum.

Answer: The two numbers are 9 and −9.

Problem 21

Of all points \( (x,y,z) \) satisfying \( x + y + 2z = 8 \), find the one that minimizes \( (x-1)^2 + (y-1)^2 + (z-1)^2 \).

Step 1: Lagrange System

\( F_x = 2(x-1) - \lambda = 0 \implies x - 1 = \frac{\lambda}{2} \)

\( F_y = 2(y-1) - \lambda = 0 \implies y - 1 = \frac{\lambda}{2} \)

\( F_z = 2(z-1) - 2\lambda = 0 \implies z - 1 = \lambda \)

Step 2: Solve

From first two: \( x = y \). From first and third: \( z - 1 = 2(x-1) \implies z = 2x - 1 \).

Constraint: \( x + x + 2(2x-1) = 8 \implies 6x = 10 \implies x = \frac{5}{3} \)

\( y = \frac{5}{3},\; z = \frac{7}{3} \)

Why is this a minimum? The function measures squared distance from the point \( (1,1,1) \). A plane has one closest point to any given point — that closest point gives the minimum distance. There is no maximum since the plane extends infinitely.

Answer: The minimum occurs at \( \left(\frac{5}{3}, \frac{5}{3}, \frac{7}{3}\right) \).

Problem 22

A carpenter builds a rectangular room with a fixed perimeter of 156 ft. What dimensions maximize the area? What is the area?

Step 1: Set Up

Maximize \( A = xy \) subject to \( 2x + 2y = 156 \implies x + y = 78 \).

Step 2: Lagrange

\( F_x = y - \lambda = 0 \implies \lambda = y \)

\( F_y = x - \lambda = 0 \implies \lambda = x \)

So \( x = y \). Constraint: \( 2x = 78 \implies x = 39 \).

Why is this a maximum? With \( y = 78-x \), \( A = x(78-x) = -x^2 + 78x \), a downward parabola. The vertex is the maximum. (A room with dimensions near 0 has near-zero area.)

\( A = 39 \times 39 = 1521 \)

Answer: Dimensions are 39 ft × 39 ft. Area = 1521 ft².

Problem 23

A cylindrical can has volume \( 16\pi \) cm³. Find dimensions that yield the minimum surface area.

Step 1: Set Up

Volume: \( \pi r^2 h = 16\pi \implies h = \frac{16}{r^2} \)

Surface Area: \( S = 2\pi r^2 + 2\pi r h = 2\pi r^2 + \frac{32\pi}{r} \)

Step 2: Optimize

\( \frac{dS}{dr} = 4\pi r - \frac{32\pi}{r^2} = 0 \implies 4r^3 = 32 \implies r = 2 \)

\( h = \frac{16}{4} = 4 \)

Step 3: Verify Minimum

\( \frac{d^2S}{dr^2} = 4\pi + \frac{64\pi}{r^3} > 0 \) for all \( r > 0 \), confirming a minimum.

Answer: Radius = 2 cm, Height = 4 cm.

Problem 24

Total sales \( S(L,M) = ML - L^2 \) where \( M \) = materials cost, \( L \) = labor cost. Maximize subject to \( M + L = 115 \).

Step 1: Substitution

\( M = 115 - L \implies S(L) = (115-L)L - L^2 = 115L - 2L^2 \)

Step 2: Optimize

\( S'(L) = 115 - 4L = 0 \implies L = 28.75 \)

\( M = 115 - 28.75 = 86.25 \)

Why is this a maximum? \( S(L) = -2L^2 + 115L \) is a downward parabola (\( a = -2 < 0 \)). Its vertex is the maximum.

\( S = 86.25(28.75) - (28.75)^2 = 2479.6875 - 826.5625 = 1653.13 \)

Answer: Maximum sales = $1653.13.

Problem 25

Machine A: \( C(x) = 60 + \frac{x^2}{6} \). Machine B: \( C(y) = 180 + \frac{y^3}{9} \). Minimize total cost \( C(x) + C(y) \) subject to \( x + y = 22{,}650 \).

Step 1: Lagrange — Equate Marginal Costs

\( C'_A(x) = \frac{x}{3},\quad C'_B(y) = \frac{y^2}{3} \)

Set equal: \( \frac{x}{3} = \frac{y^2}{3} \implies x = y^2 \)

Step 2: Solve

\( y^2 + y = 22650 \implies y^2 + y - 22650 = 0 \)

Discriminant: \( 1 + 90600 = 90601 = 301^2 \). So \( y = \frac{-1+301}{2} = 150 \).

\( x = 150^2 = 22500 \)

Why is this a minimum? Both cost functions have increasing marginal costs (\( C''_A > 0 \), \( C''_B > 0 \)). Setting marginal costs equal distributes production optimally. Overloading either machine raises costs dramatically.

Answer: 22,500 units on Machine A, 150 units on Machine B.

Part IV: Absolute Extrema & Advanced (Problems 26–30)

Problem 26

Find the absolute max and min of \( g(x,y) = 5x^2 + y^2 \) on \( -4 \le x \le 4,\; -4 \le y \le 5 \).

Step 1: Interior Critical Point

\( g_x = 10x = 0,\; g_y = 2y = 0 \implies (0,0) \). \( g(0,0) = 0 \).

Step 2: Boundary Check (corners give extremes)

\( g(\pm4, 5) = 80 + 25 = 105 \)

\( g(\pm4, -4) = 80 + 16 = 96 \)

\( g(0, \pm4) = 16 \), \( g(0, 5) = 25 \)

Answer: Absolute minimum = 0. Absolute maximum = 105.

Problem 27

Find the absolute max and min of \( k(x,y) = -x^2 - y^2 + 10x + 10y \) on \( 0 \le x \le 6,\; y \ge 0,\; x+y \le 12 \).

Step 1: Interior Critical Point

\( k_x = -2x + 10 = 0 \implies x = 5 \). \( k_y = -2y + 10 = 0 \implies y = 5 \).

\( (5,5) \) lies inside the region. \( k(5,5) = -25-25+50+50 = 50 \).

Step 2: Boundary and Corner Check

  • \( k(0,0) = 0 \), \( k(6,0) = 24 \), \( k(0,12) = -24 \), \( k(6,6) = 48 \)
  • Edge \( x=0 \): max at \( y=5 \), \( k=25 \)
  • Edge \( y=0 \): max at \( x=5 \), \( k=25 \)
  • Edge \( x+y=12 \): \( k = -2x^2+24x-24 \), max at \( x=6 \), \( k=48 \)

Answer: Minimum = −24 (at \( (0,12) \)). Maximum = 50 (at \( (5,5) \)).

Problem 28

Find the minimum of \( f(x,y) = 10x^2 + y^2 + 2xy + 19x + 2y \) subject to \( y = x + 12 \).

Step 1: Substitute Constraint

\( f = 10x^2 + (x+12)^2 + 2x(x+12) + 19x + 2(x+12) \)

\( = 10x^2 + x^2 + 24x + 144 + 2x^2 + 24x + 19x + 2x + 24 \)

\( = 13x^2 + 69x + 168 \)

Step 2: Optimize

\( f'(x) = 26x + 69 = 0 \implies x = -\frac{69}{26} = -\frac{69}{26} \)

\( f'' = 26 > 0 \) confirms minimum.

\( f\!\left(-\frac{69}{26}\right) = 13\cdot\frac{69^2}{26^2} + 69\cdot\left(-\frac{69}{26}\right) + 168 = \frac{61893}{676} - \frac{4761}{26} + 168 \)

\( = \frac{61893 - 123786 + 113568}{676} = \frac{51675}{676} \)

Answer: Minimum value is \( \frac{51675}{676} \).

Problem 29

Find the maximum of \( f(x,y,z) = xyz \) subject to \( 2x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = 3 \).

Step 1: Lagrange System

\( F_x = yz - 4x\lambda = 0 \)

\( F_y = xz - 2y\lambda = 0 \)

\( F_z = xy - 2z\lambda = 0 \)

Step 2: Solve

From the equations: \( \lambda = \frac{yz}{4x} = \frac{xz}{2y} = \frac{xy}{2z} \)

From \( \frac{yz}{4x} = \frac{xz}{2y} \): \( 2y^2 = 4x^2 \implies y^2 = 2x^2 \)

From \( \frac{xz}{2y} = \frac{xy}{2z} \): \( z^2 = y^2 \implies z = y \) (taking positive values)

Constraint: \( 2x^2 + 2x^2 + 2x^2 = 3 \implies x^2 = \frac{1}{2} \implies x = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \)

\( y = z = 1 \)

\( f = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \cdot 1 \cdot 1 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \)

Answer: Maximum value is \( 1 \).

Problem 30

Find the minimum of \( f(x,y,z) = x^2 + y^2 + z^2 \) subject to \( 8x - 2y + 6z = -52 \).

Step 1: Lagrange System

\( 2x = 8\lambda \implies x = 4\lambda \)

\( 2y = -2\lambda \implies y = -\lambda \)

\( 2z = 6\lambda \implies z = 3\lambda \)

Step 2: Solve

Constraint: \( 8(4\lambda) - 2(-\lambda) + 6(3\lambda) = -52 \)

\( 32\lambda + 2\lambda + 18\lambda = -52 \implies 52\lambda = -52 \implies \lambda = -1 \)

\( x = -4,\; y = 1,\; z = -3 \)

Why is this a minimum? \( f = x^2 + y^2 + z^2 \) measures squared distance from the origin to a point on the plane. The closest point on a plane gives the unique minimum.

\( f(-4,1,-3) = 16 + 1 + 9 = 26 \)

Answer: Minimum value is 26.
