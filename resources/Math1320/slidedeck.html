    We use the Sample to make a "best guess" about the Population.

    Sampling Techniques

    How do we pick the sample? (Goal: Reduce Bias)

    Simple Random

    Names in a hat. Every possible group has equal chance.

    Systematic

    Every $k^{th}$ person. (Start random, then jump by $k$).

    Cluster

    Select random GROUPS -> Take EVERYONE in them.

    All of Some

    Stratified

    Take SOME from EVERY group.

    Some of All

    Experimental Design

    Control

    Compare treatments to a baseline (Placebo/Control group) to isolate effects.

    Randomization

    Randomly assign subjects to groups to even out external factors.

    Replication

    Use enough subjects so the result isn't a fluke.

    Organizing Data

    Qualitative

    Categorical / Labels

    • Hair Color, Party, Yes/No
    • Visuals: Bar Chart (Gaps), Pie Chart

    Quantitative

    Numerical / Counts

    • Discrete: Countable (Gaps, e.g., # of kids)
    • Continuous: Measurements (No gaps, e.g., Height)
    • Visuals: Histogram (Touching), Boxplot

    Measures of Center

    Question: Which one is "Typical"?

    Measure Definition Resistance
    Mean ($\bar{x}$) Average (Sum / n) Low (Pulled by outliers)
    Median Exact Middle High (Ignores outliers)
    Mode Most Frequent High

    Measures of Variation (Spread)

    Standard Deviation ($s$)

    Average distance from the Mean.

    Formula divides by n-1.

    Distribution Rules

    Chebyshev: ANY Shape.
    $1 - 1/k^2$ → k=2 means at least 75%
    Empirical: BELL Shape Only.
    See next slide for interactive visualization.

    The Empirical Rule (68-95-99.7)

    68% of data falls within 1 SD

    Five-Number Summary & Boxplots

    Min • Q1 • Median • Q3 • Max

    IQR = Q3 - Q1 (The Box Width). Middle 50%.

    Outliers = Beyond $1.5 \times IQR$.

    CRITICAL WARNING

    Population SD ($\sigma$) vs Sample SD ($s$)

    R Function sd() = SAMPLE ($n-1$).

    If homework asks for POPULATION ($N$),
    you MUST calculate it manually!

    sqrt(mean((x - mean(x))^2))
