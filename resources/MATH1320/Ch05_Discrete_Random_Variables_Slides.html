    Standard Deviation ($\sigma$)

    Definition Formula

    $\sigma = \sqrt{\sum (x - \mu)^2 P(X=x)}$

    Measures the spread or variability of the random variable (Average distance from the mean).

    Computing Formula

    $\sigma = \sqrt{\sum x^2 P(X=x) - \mu^2}$

    Often easier for hand calculations as it avoids subtracting the mean at every step.

    Binomial Foundations

    Factorials ($n!$)

    Product of the first $k$ positive integers.

    $k! = k(k-1)\cdots2\cdot1$
    $0! = 1$

    Ex: $3! = 3 \times 2 \times 1 = 6$

    Binomial Coefficient $\binom{n}{x}$

    Number of ways to choose $x$ successes in $n$ trials.

    $\binom{n}{x} = \frac{n!}{x!(n-x)!}$

    Read as "n choose x".

    Bernoulli Trials (Conditions)

    Repeated trials are Bernoulli trials if these conditions are met:

    B

    Binary

    Success ($s$) or Failure ($f$).

    I

    Independent

    Trials must not affect each other.

    N

    Number

    Fixed number of trials ($n$).

    SSuccess Probability ($p$) must remain constant.
    Binomial Random Variable ($X$): The total number of successes in $n$ Bernoulli trials.

    The Binomial Formula

    $P(X=x) = \binom{n}{x} p^x (1-p)^{n-x}$

    Shape of Binomial Distributions

    $p < 0.5$
    Right Skewed

    $p = 0.5$
    Symmetric

    Formula Shortcuts

    Mean: $\mu = np$
    SD: $\sigma = \sqrt{np(1-p)}$
    Sampling without Replacement:Approximation valid if sample size $n \le 5\%$ of population size.

    Solved Example: Binomial

    Problem: A student guesses randomly on a 5-question T/F quiz. What is the probability of getting exactly 3 correct?

    1. Identify Conditions (BINS):
    Binary, Independent, $n=5$ Fixed, $p=0.5$ Constant.
    2. Choose Formula:
    $\binom{5}{3} (0.5)^3 (0.5)^{5-3}$
    3. Calculate:
    $\binom{5}{3} = \frac{5!}{3!2!} = 10 \quad | \quad (0.5)^3 = 0.125$
    $P(3) = 10 \times 0.125 \times 0.25 = \mathbf{0.3125}$
    4. Mean (Just for fun):
    $\mu = n \cdot p = 5 \cdot 0.5 = 2.5$ expected correct answers.

    Poisson Distribution

    Modeling events occurring in a fixed interval of time or space.

    Formula

    $P(X=x) = e^{-\lambda} \frac{\lambda^x}{x!}$

    $\lambda$: Average number of events per interval.

    Stats

    $\mu = \lambda$
    $\sigma = \sqrt{\lambda}$
    Approximation Rule:Poisson can approximate Binomial if $n \ge 100$ and $np \le 10$. Then set $\lambda = np$.

    Binomial vs Poisson

    Binomial: Counting Successes in $n$ trials.
    Poisson: Counting Events in an interval (Time/Area).

    Why Approximate? Easier to calculate$e^{-\lambda}$ than massive factorials in Binomial.

    Solved Example: Poisson

    Problem: A coffee shop averages 4 customers per minute during rush hour. What is the probability of getting exactly 6 customers in the next minute?

    1. Identify Parameter:
    $\lambda = 4$ (Average rate), $x = 6$ (Target number)
    (Interval is "next minute", matching rate unit)
    2. Choose Formula:
    $P(6) = \frac{e^{-4} 4^6}{6!}$
    3. Calculate:
    $e^{-4} \approx 0.0183 \quad | \quad 4^6 = 4096 \quad | \quad 6! = 720$
    $P(6) = \frac{0.0183 \times 4096}{720} = \mathbf{0.1042}$
    4. Stats:
    Expected: $\mu = \lambda = 4$ customers.
    SD: $\sigma = \sqrt{4} = 2$ customers.
