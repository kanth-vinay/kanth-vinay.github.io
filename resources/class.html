Comprehensive Lesson: Parabolas as Conic Sections

1. The Geometric Definition of a Parabola

You've studied parabolas as functions of the form $y = ax^2+bx+c$. The conic section definition is geometric and more powerful.

A parabola is the set of all points $(x,y)$ in a plane that are equidistant from a fixed point (the focus) and a fixed line (the directrix).

  • The Vertex is the midpoint between the focus and the directrix. It's the "turning point" of the parabola.
  • The Axis of Symmetry is the line that passes through the vertex and focus, dividing the parabola into two mirror images.
  • The parabola always "hugs" or opens around the focus and away from the directrix.
  • The distance from the vertex to the focus is denoted by the variable '$p$'. The distance from the vertex to the directrix is also '$p$'.

2. The Four Standard Equations of a Parabola

The equation of a parabola changes based on its orientation (vertical or horizontal) and the location of its vertex $(h, k)$.

A. Vertical Parabolas (Opens Up or Down)

Standard Equation: $(x-h)^2 = 4p(y-k)$

  • Opens Up if $p > 0$. The focus is above the vertex.
  • Opens Down if $p < 0$. The focus is below the vertex.
  • Vertex: $(h, k)$
  • Focus: $(h, k+p)$
  • Directrix: $y = k-p$
  • Axis of Symmetry: $x = h$

B. Horizontal Parabolas (Opens Left or Right)

Standard Equation: $(y-k)^2 = 4p(x-h)$

  • Opens Right if $p > 0$. The focus is to the right of the vertex.
  • Opens Left if $p < 0$. The focus is to the left of the vertex.
  • Vertex: $(h, k)$
  • Focus: $(h+p, k)$
  • Directrix: $x = h-p$
  • Axis of Symmetry: $y = k$

4. Key Skill: Completing the Square

Often, the parabola's equation is given in a "general form" and you must use the skill of completing the square to rewrite it in standard form to find its characteristics.

Fully Solved Example

Find the vertex, focus, directrix, and axis of symmetry of the parabola given by the equation: $y^2 + 8y + 12x + 4 = 0$.

Step 1: Isolate the terms with the squared variable. Since 'y' is squared, we know this is a horizontal parabola. Group the y-terms on one side and move everything else to the other.

$y^2 + 8y = -12x - 4$

Step 2: Complete the square. Take half of the coefficient of the y-term, square it, and add it to both sides. Half of 8 is 4, and $4^2 = 16$.

$y^2 + 8y + 16 = -12x - 4 + 16$

Step 3: Factor the perfect square trinomial.

$(y+4)^2 = -12x + 12$

Step 4: Factor out the coefficient of the x-term to match the standard form $(y-k)^2 = 4p(x-h)$.

$(y+4)^2 = -12(x-1)$

Step 5: Identify h, k, and p.

  • The vertex $(h,k)$ is $(1, -4)$. (Note the sign changes!)
  • From $4p = -12$, we find that $p = -3$.

Step 6: Determine the characteristics.

  • Vertex: $(1, -4)$
  • Orientation: Horizontal (y is squared) and opens left (since $p$ is negative).
  • Focus: $(h+p, k) = (1 + (-3), -4) = (-2, -4)$
  • Directrix: $x = h-p = 1 - (-3) = 4$. The equation is $x=4$.
  • Axis of Symmetry: $y = k$, which is $y=-4$.

5. Practice Problems

Find the vertex, focus, and directrix for each parabola.

1. $(x-2)^2 = 8(y+1)$

Click to Reveal Solution

Standard Form: Already in form $(x-h)^2 = 4p(y-k)$. Vertical parabola.

Vertex: $(h,k) = (2, -1)$

p-value: $4p=8 \implies p=2$. Since $p>0$, it opens up.

Focus: $(h, k+p) = (2, -1+2) = (2, 1)$

Directrix: $y = k-p = -1-2 = -3$. So, $y=-3$.

2. $y^2 = -16x$

Click to Reveal Solution

Standard Form: $(y-0)^2 = -16(x-0)$. Horizontal parabola.

Vertex: $(h,k) = (0, 0)$

p-value: $4p=-16 \implies p=-4$. Since $p<0$, it opens left.

Focus: $(h+p, k) = (0-4, 0) = (-4, 0)$

Directrix: $x = h-p = 0-(-4) = 4$. So, $x=4$.

3. $x^2 + 4x - 4y = 0$

Click to Reveal Solution

Complete the Square: $x^2 + 4x = 4y \implies x^2+4x+4 = 4y+4 \implies (x+2)^2 = 4(y+1)$.

Vertex: $(h,k) = (-2, -1)$

p-value: $4p=4 \implies p=1$. Opens up.

Focus: $(h, k+p) = (-2, -1+1) = (-2, 0)$

Directrix: $y = k-p = -1-1 = -2$. So, $y=-2$.

4. $(y+5)^2 = 12(x-2)$

Click to Reveal Solution

Standard Form: Already in form $(y-k)^2 = 4p(x-h)$. Horizontal parabola.

Vertex: $(h,k) = (2, -5)$

p-value: $4p=12 \implies p=3$. Opens right.

Focus: $(h+p, k) = (2+3, -5) = (5, -5)$

Directrix: $x = h-p = 2-3 = -1$. So, $x=-1$.

5. $x = y^2 - 6y + 5$

Click to Reveal Solution

Complete the Square: $x-5 = y^2-6y \implies x-5+9 = y^2-6y+9 \implies x+4 = (y-3)^2$.

Standard Form: $(y-3)^2 = 1(x+4)$.

Vertex: $(h,k) = (-4, 3)$

p-value: $4p=1 \implies p=1/4$. Opens right.

Focus: $(h+p, k) = (-4+1/4, 3) = (-15/4, 3)$

Directrix: $x = h-p = -4-1/4 = -17/4$. So, $x=-17/4$.

6. $x^2 = -2y$

Click to Reveal Solution

Standard Form: $(x-0)^2 = -2(y-0)$. Vertical parabola.

Vertex: $(h,k) = (0, 0)$

p-value: $4p=-2 \implies p=-1/2$. Opens down.

Focus: $(h, k+p) = (0, 0-1/2) = (0, -1/2)$

Directrix: $y = k-p = 0-(-1/2) = 1/2$. So, $y=1/2$.

7. $y^2 + 2y + 4x - 7 = 0$

Click to Reveal Solution

Complete the Square: $y^2+2y = -4x+7 \implies y^2+2y+1 = -4x+7+1 \implies (y+1)^2 = -4x+8$.

Standard Form: $(y+1)^2 = -4(x-2)$.

Vertex: $(h,k) = (2, -1)$

p-value: $4p=-4 \implies p=-1$. Opens left.

Focus: $(h+p, k) = (2-1, -1) = (1, -1)$

Directrix: $x = h-p = 2-(-1) = 3$. So, $x=3$.

8. $y = \frac{1}{16}x^2$

Click to Reveal Solution

Rewrite in Standard Form: Multiply by 16 to get $16y = x^2$. So, $x^2 = 16y$.

Vertex: $(h,k) = (0, 0)$

p-value: $4p=16 \implies p=4$. Opens up.

Focus: $(h, k+p) = (0, 0+4) = (0, 4)$

Directrix: $y = k-p = 0-4 = -4$. So, $y=-4$.

9. $x^2 - 12x + 20y - 4 = 0$

Click to Reveal Solution

Complete the Square: $x^2-12x = -20y+4 \implies x^2-12x+36 = -20y+4+36 \implies (x-6)^2 = -20y+40$.

Standard Form: $(x-6)^2 = -20(y-2)$.

Vertex: $(h,k) = (6, 2)$

p-value: $4p=-20 \implies p=-5$. Opens down.

Focus: $(h, k+p) = (6, 2-5) = (6, -3)$

Directrix: $y = k-p = 2-(-5) = 7$. So, $y=7$.

10. $x = -\frac{1}{4}y^2 - y - 2$

Click to Reveal Solution

Rewrite and Complete the Square: $x+2 = -\frac{1}{4}(y^2+4y)$. Multiply by -4: $-4(x+2) = y^2+4y$.

$-4x-8 = y^2+4y \implies -4x-8+4 = y^2+4y+4 \implies -4x-4 = (y+2)^2$.

Standard Form: $(y+2)^2 = -4(x+1)$.

Vertex: $(h,k) = (-1, -2)$

p-value: $4p=-4 \implies p=-1$. Opens left.

Focus: $(h+p, k) = (-1-1, -2) = (-2, -2)$

Directrix: $x = h-p = -1-(-1) = 0$. So, $x=0$.
