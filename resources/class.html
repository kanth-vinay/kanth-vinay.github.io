MATH 1320 — Hypothesis Testing Worksheet: Answer Key (Quick Check)

  1. Problem 1 — z-test (right-tailed)

    • Test statistic: z = 1.760
    • P-value (right): 0.0392
    • α = 0.01 ⇒ Fail to reject H₀
    • Conclusion: Not enough evidence at 1% that the course increases SAT mean.

  2. Problem 2 — t-test (left-tailed)

    • df = 13
    • Test statistic: t = -1.871
    • P-value (left): ≈ 0.0420
    • α = 0.10 ⇒ Reject H₀
    • Conclusion: Evidence that the mean monthly cost is less than \$90.

  3. Problem 3 — z-test (two-tailed)

    • Test statistic: z = 3.776
    • P-value (two-tailed): ≈ 0.000159
    • α = 0.01 ⇒ Reject H₀
    • Conclusion: Evidence the true mean lecture length differs from 3000 words.

  4. Problem 4 — t-test (two-tailed)

    • df = 84
    • Test statistic: t = 1.475
    • P-value (two-tailed): ≈ 0.1439
    • α = 0.01 ⇒ Fail to reject H₀
    • Conclusion: No evidence (at 1%) that the American mean time differs from 2.6 hr.

  5. Problem 5 — z-test (left-tailed)

    • Test statistic: z = -1.309
    • P-value (left): ≈ 0.0952
    • α = 0.05 ⇒ Fail to reject H₀
    • Conclusion: Insufficient evidence that the mean is less than \$35.

  6. Problem 6 — t-test (right-tailed)

    • df = 42
    • Test statistic: t = 1.561
    • P-value (right): ≈ 0.0630
    • α = 0.05 ⇒ Fail to reject H₀
    • Conclusion: Not enough evidence that this university’s mean GPA is higher than 3.1.

Note: Small rounding differences (±0.001 on test stats, ±0.001–0.005 on P-values) are acceptable depending on calculators/software.
