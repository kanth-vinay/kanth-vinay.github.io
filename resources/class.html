Asymptotes — What & How

Vertical (VA)

Lines \(x=a\) where \(f\) blows up as \(x\to a^\pm\) (usually zeros of denominator not canceled).

Check: factor & simplify first.

Horizontal (HA)

Lines \(y=b\) with \(\lim_{x\to\pm\infty}f(x)=b\). For rationals, compare degrees.

  • deg(num) < deg(den) → \(y=0\)
  • deg(num)=deg(den) → leading coeff ratio
  • deg(num) > deg(den) → no HA (maybe slant)

Slant/Oblique

When deg(num)=deg(den)+1; polynomial long division gives \(y=mx+b\).

Quick Drill — Vertical Asymptotes

\(f(x)=\dfrac{9x-8}{x-9}\)
Denominator zero at \(x=9\); no cancellation.
\(f(x)=\dfrac{2x}{x^2-4}\)
Factor \(x^2-4=(x-2)(x+2)\); check cancellation (none).
\(f(x)=\dfrac{x+4}{x^2+6x+8}\)
Factor \(x^2+6x+8=(x+2)(x+4)\); simplify first.
Note: \(f(x)=\ln(x^2+4)\) has **no** VA (domain all real; inside ln is always >0).

Quick Drill — Horizontal Asymptotes

\(f(x)=\dfrac{12x}{21x-2}\)

Same degree → \(y=\dfrac{12}{21}=\dfrac{4}{7}\).

\(f(x)=\dfrac{7x}{x^2-3x}\)

deg top < deg bottom → \(y=0\).

\(f(x)=\dfrac{7x^7+2x^5-3}{2x^8-x+6}\)

deg top < deg bottom → \(y=0\).

Exponential: \(f(x)=e^{8x}\) has **no** HA (tends to \(0\) as \(x\to-\infty\), \(+\infty\) as \(x\to+\infty\)).

Graph Sketching — Workflow

  1. Domain & asymptotes (V/H/S)
  2. Intercepts
  3. First derivative → ↑/↓, extrema
  4. Second derivative → concavity, inflection
  5. Assemble the graph

Case A

\(f(x)=\dfrac{-1}{x-3}\)

VA \(x=3\); HA \(y=0\); no x-int, y-int at \((0,\tfrac{1}{3})\) negative; monotone behavior by sign of derivative.

Case B

\(f(x)=\dfrac{4x-3}{x}\)

VA \(x=0\); HA \(y=4\); x-int \((\tfrac{3}{4},0)\); analyze signs and concavity.

Case C

\(f(x)=\dfrac{x+5}{x^2-3x-40}\)

Factor denom \((x-8)(x+5)\); simplification changes intercepts & VAs; HA \(y=0\); analyze intervals.

Limits at ±∞ — Practice

\(\displaystyle \lim_{x\to-\infty}\frac{2x^2+x}{x+8}\)
Divide by \(x^2\) or use leading term division: behaves like \(2x\) for large |x|.
\(\displaystyle \lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{-3x^3+6x}{6x^2-6x-8}\)
Degrees: 3 over 2 → grows like \(-\frac{3}{6}x=-\tfrac12 x \to -\infty\).

Build a Rational Function from Constraints

VA at \(x=9\), HA \(y=4\), \(f(0)=0\)
HA \(4\Rightarrow\) same degree & ratio 4; VA at 9 \(\Rightarrow\) factor \(x-9\) in denom; impose \(f(0)=0\).
VAs at \(x=-6,5\), HA \(y=0\), \(h(1)=3\)
HA 0 \(\Rightarrow\) deg(den)>deg(num); include \((x+6)(x-5)\) below; choose numerator meeting \(h(1)=3\).

Optimization — Modeling Playbook

Steps

  1. Define variables & units
  2. Write objective \(F(x)\)
  3. Constraints → reduce to one variable
  4. Domain (physical/closed)
  5. Critical points & endpoints
  6. Interpret result (context)

Examples in this set

  • Inventory value \(V(t)=40-\frac{25t^2}{(t+2)^2}\)
  • Pollution cost \(C(p)=\frac{48000}{100-p}\)
  • Medication \(A(t)=\frac{A_0}{t^2+1},\ A_0=120\)

Absolute Extrema

Closed intervals: test endpoints + critical points.

Open/∞: analyze limits for existence.

Application — Inventory Value

\(V(t)=40-\dfrac{25t^2}{(t+2)^2}\) (thousand \$), \(t\) in months.

  • Compute \(V(0),V(5),V(10),V(70)\)
  • Find max of \(V\) on \([0,\infty)\)
  • Horizontal asymptote?
Quotient rule or simplify with \(u=t/(t+2)\). As \(t\to\infty\), \(\frac{t^2}{(t+2)^2}\to 1\) ⇒ HA.

Application — Pollution Cost

\(C(p)=\dfrac{48000}{100-p}\), cost to remove \(p\%\).

  • Evaluate \(C(0),C(15),C(60),C(90)\)
  • Domain?
  • Interpret near \(p\to 100^-\)
Vertical asymptote at \(p=100\). Domain \([0,100)\) from context; cost grows unbounded.

Application — Medication Amount

\(A(t)=\dfrac{120}{t^2+1}\) (cc), \(t\) in hours.

  • Compute \(A(0),A(1),A(2),A(8),A(10)\)
  • Max on \([0,\infty)\)
  • Does \(A(t)\) ever reach 0?
Decreasing for \(t>0\); HA \(y=0\) — approaches but never equals 0.

Absolute Extrema — Practice Set

\(f(x)=4+x-x^2\) on \([0,4]\) Parabola; derivative test + endpoints
Compute \(f'(x)=1-2x\); test \(x=\tfrac12\) with endpoints \(0,4\).
\(f(x)=x^2-8x-1\) on \([2,7]\) Complete square or derivative
Critical at \(x=4\); compare \(f(2),f(4),f(7)\).
\(f(x)=x^3-x^2-x+4\) on \([-1,0]\) Find criticals in interval + endpoints
\(f'(x)=3x^2-2x-1\); check roots within \([-1,0]\).
\(f(x)=3x^4-2x^3\) on \([-4,4]\) Even/odd cues; derivative zeros
\(f'(x)=12x^3-6x^2=6x^2(2x-1)\); test \(x=0,\tfrac12,\pm4\).
\(f(x)=x+\dfrac{16}{x}\) on \([4,10]\) AM–GM intuition + derivative
\(f'(x)=1-\dfrac{16}{x^2}=0\Rightarrow x=4\) (in domain).

Constrained Optimization — Geometry

Max area: beach rectangle

Rope length 2300 yd; shoreline is one side.

Perimeter rope \(=2x+L=2300\Rightarrow L=2300-2x\); area \(A=xL\) with \(x>0\).

Cardboard box (open top)

Sheet \(29\!\times\!16\) in; cut squares; max volume.

Volume \(V(x)=x(29-2x)(16-2x)\); domain from geometry.

Dumpster SA min

Volume \(30\,\text{yd}^3\), length \(=2w\), open top.

\(V=2w^2h=30\Rightarrow h=\dfrac{15}{w^2}\); \(SA= \text{base}+ \text{sides}\) (no top).

Open cylinder SA min

Volume \(1728\text{ cm}^3\); minimize \(S\).

\(V=\pi r^2h\Rightarrow h=\dfrac{1728}{\pi r^2}\); \(S=\pi r^2+2\pi rh\) (open top).

Profit & EOQ-type Models

Max Profit

\(R(x)=60x-0.5x^2,\ C(x)=4x+10\). Maximize \(P(x)=R-C\).

Quadratic in \(x\). Vertex gives optimal units and profit.

EOQ (general & numeric)

Lot size \(q^*=\sqrt{\dfrac{2bQ}{d}}\); orders/yr \(=\dfrac{Q}{q^*}\).

Use \(Q=100,\ d=\$18,\ b=\$36,\ c=\$11\) as in prompt; compute \(q^*\) then orders/yr.

Placement & Path — Minimization

Power line A→S→C

Underwater \$5400/mi, land \$3000/mi; choose S to min cost.

Cost \(=3000\cdot AS + 5400\cdot SC\) with \(SC=\sqrt{5^2+x^2}\). Differentiate.

Bridge placement (general)

Total distance \(D(x)=\sqrt{a^2+(p-x)^2}+\sqrt{b^2+x^2}+r\).

\(D'(x)=\frac{x}{\sqrt{b^2+x^2}}-\frac{p-x}{\sqrt{a^2+(p-x)^2}}=0\) ⇒ check second derivative & interpret.
