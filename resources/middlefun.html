Complex Numbers & Polar Coordinates

Section I: Operations with Complex Numbers
1. Let $z = 3(\cos 130^\circ + i \sin 130^\circ)$ and $w = 4(\cos 270^\circ + i \sin 270^\circ)$. Find the product $zw$.
2. Let $z = 4(\cos \frac{3\pi}{8} + i \sin \frac{3\pi}{8})$ and $w = 2(\cos \frac{9\pi}{16} + i \sin \frac{9\pi}{16})$. Find the quotient $\frac{z}{w}$.
3. Let $z = 2+2i$ and $w = \sqrt{3}-i$. Find the product $zw$ in rectangular form.
4. Let $z = 1-i$ and $w = 1-\sqrt{3}i$. Find the quotient $\frac{z}{w}$ in rectangular form.
Section II: Converting Between Polar and Rectangular Coordinates
5. Convert the polar coordinate $(4, 120^\circ)$ to rectangular coordinates $(x, y)$.
6. Convert the polar coordinate $(6, \frac{7\pi}{6})$ to rectangular coordinates $(x, y)$.
7. Convert the rectangular coordinate $(3, -3)$ to polar coordinates $(r, \theta)$, where $r > 0$ and $0^\circ \le \theta < 360^\circ$.
8. Convert the rectangular coordinate $(0, 4)$ to polar coordinates $(r, \theta)$, where $r > 0$ and $0 \le \theta < 2\pi$.
Section III: Exponents with Complex Numbers
9. Simplify the expression: $\left[3(\cos 80^\circ + i \sin 80^\circ)\right]^3$.
10. Simplify the expression: $\left[\sqrt{2}\left(\cos \frac{5\pi}{16} + i \sin \frac{5\pi}{16}\right)\right]^4$.
