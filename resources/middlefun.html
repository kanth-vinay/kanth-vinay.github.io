Complex Numbers & Polar Coordinates (Form B)

Section I: Operations with Complex Numbers
1. Let $z = 5(\cos 110^\circ + i \sin 110^\circ)$ and $w = 2(\cos 200^\circ + i \sin 200^\circ)$. Find the product $zw$.
2. Let $z = 8\left(\cos \frac{4\pi}{5} + i \sin \frac{4\pi}{5}\right)$ and $w = 2\left(\cos \frac{\pi}{4} + i \sin \frac{\pi}{4}\right)$. Find the quotient $\frac{z}{w}$.
3. Let $z = 3-i$ and $w = 2+4i$. Find the product $zw$ in rectangular form.
4. Let $z = 4+2i$ and $w = 1-i$. Find the quotient $\frac{z}{w}$ in rectangular form.
Section II: Converting Between Polar and Rectangular Coordinates
5. Convert the polar coordinate $(8, 330^\circ)$ to rectangular coordinates $(x, y)$.
6. Convert the polar coordinate $(10, \frac{2\pi}{3})$ to rectangular coordinates $(x, y)$.
7. Convert the rectangular coordinate $(-4\sqrt{3}, 4)$ to polar coordinates $(r, \theta)$, where $r > 0$ and $0^\circ \le \theta < 360^\circ$.
8. Convert the rectangular coordinate $(0, -7)$ to polar coordinates $(r, \theta)$, where $r > 0$ and $0 \le \theta < 2\pi$.
Section III: Exponents with Complex Numbers
9. Simplify the expression: $\left[2(\cos 50^\circ + i \sin 50^\circ)\right]^4$.
10. Simplify the expression: $\left[\sqrt{3}\left(\cos \frac{2\pi}{9} + i \sin \frac{2\pi}{9}\right)\right]^6$.
