College Admissions Journey: Timeline

Foundations 9th Grade

  • Maintain strong GPA in math, science, and English.
  • Engage in clubs: Math, Robotics, Science Olympiad.
  • Volunteer weekly & meticulously track service hours.
  • Attend local STEM summer workshops and camps.
  • Start a personal blog or science journal to document interests.
  • Explore online resources like Khan Academy, MIT OCW, STEM YouTube channels.
  • Visit a local college (virtual or in-person) to get a feel for campus life.

Build & Explore 10th Grade

  • Enroll in AP or honors courses, with a strong STEM focus.
  • Prepare for PSAT 10, AMC 10, and participate in MathCounts, Science Fairs.
  • Seek opportunities to shadow local STEM professionals.
  • Initiate a passion project or a youth-led community initiative.
  • Summer: focus on research preparation or advanced coding camps.
  • Continue long-term volunteering or tutoring commitments.

Refine & Lead 11th Grade

  • Take challenging APs: Calculus, Biology, Chemistry, Computer Science.
  • Prepare for SAT/ACT and PSAT/NMSQT for National Merit consideration.
  • Apply to prestigious summer programs: NIH SIP, RSI, Simons, MITES, etc.
  • Seek leadership roles in clubs and school organizations.
  • Begin shortlisting colleges and identifying potential scholarships.
  • Start drafting your Common App personal statement.

Apply & Shine 12th Grade

  • Finalize Common App essay and all college-specific supplements.
  • Submit Early Action (EA)/Early Decision (ED) applications by Nov 1–15.
  • Complete and submit FAFSA and CSS Profile for financial aid.
  • Prepare thoroughly for college interviews, especially for BS/MD programs.
  • Thank your recommenders; organize final resume.
  • Celebrate milestones and decisions!

Our Credentials & Approach

Scholarship-Boosting Extras

Beyond the basics, we emphasize key areas that significantly enhance scholarship prospects:

  • Achieving National Merit Finalist status via PSAT scores.
  • Participation and success in prestigious STEM competitions (Regeneron, AMC, USACO).
  • Securing meaningful summer research experiences or internships.
  • Demonstrating strong leadership and measurable community impact.
  • Crafting a powerful personal essay with a clear, authentic narrative.
