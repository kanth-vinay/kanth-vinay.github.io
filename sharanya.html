My Story

My journey in education began while pursuing my B.Sc. in Mathematics, teaching intermediate students who needed extra support for their board exams. I earned a place at the National Institute of Technology Warangal (securing the 30th rank on the entrance exam) for my M.Sc. in Applied Mathematics. During my studies, I nurtured my interest in Vedic Mathematics and taught core concepts to students in grades 5 to 8. I later pursued a second Master's degree in Statistics at Osmania University, fascinated by its problem-solving depth. While there, I taught Integral Calculus and Linear Algebra at Leela Junior College, focusing on making mathematics intuitive rather than memorizing steps.

Driven by a vision for innovative math education, I conducted extensive robotics outreach across Warangal in 2017, demonstrating to principals how robotics could equip students for the modern world. In 2018, I co-founded the Active Learning Institute—which today continues as ActiveSTEM in St. Louis—with support from the University of Southern Mississippi. I was involved in every step of its creation, from designing non-traditional classrooms to recruiting students. The institute raised $15,000 in support, provided free education to 27 students, and trained teachers in tools like GeoGebra while mentoring teaching interns.

After moving to the United States, I volunteered as a Math Tutor in the University of Missouri–St. Louis Math Academic Center under Al Stagner, assisting students in calculus and statistics. Today, I coach K-8 and middle-school students in rigorous mathematics—the algebra and geometry foundations that carry into high school, MAP and standardized-test prep, and competition-math problem solving. My approach draws on certified ABACUS and Vedic mental-math techniques alongside conceptual, thinking-first instruction, helping students build the confidence and initiative they need to thrive.
