About Us

Our Mission & Origins

Note from our mentor

Arogya Med Explorers was envisioned by Prof. Vinay Kodipelly. Recognizing the substantial body of medical research and work we have accumulated, he saw a unique opportunity for middle schoolers to benefit from our experiences. Understanding the value of early exposure, he encouraged us to bring this initiative to life.

Stepping into leadership roles, Nityasri and Sruthi took charge of the camp's curriculum and overall direction, shaping it into an interactive, hands-on experience. Meanwhile, Vihari leads our technology efforts, managing all digital platforms and artifacts to ensure a seamless experience for participants and parents alike.

As high school mentors with a strong foundation in healthcare research, we are passionate about sharing our knowledge. We created this camp to provide younger students with engaging, real-world applications of medicine, hoping to inspire the next generation of healthcare leaders.

Goals and Impact

Our goal is to provide students with meaningful exposure to the medical field through interactive learning, hands-on activities, and engagement with healthcare professionals.

By fostering curiosity, critical thinking, and scientific understanding, we aim to inspire the next generation of healthcare leaders and empower students to explore future opportunities in medicine and related fields.

Event Details

Three Day Camp

Each camp day will feature a guest speaker who is a healthcare professional specializing in the day's topic. Students will have the opportunity to learn directly from experienced physicians, participate in hands-on activities, and explore different areas of medicine.

  1. Monday

    Oncology – The Tumor Biopsy Challenge

    Students will learn about cancer and how oncologists use science and technology to understand and treat diseases. Through a hands-on DNA extraction activity, students will analyze a mock "tumor biopsy" using strawberries to represent tissue samples. They will extract DNA, investigate a simulated genetic mutation, and identify the correct targeted treatment through a fun puzzle-solving challenge. This activity demonstrates how personalized medicine uses a patient's unique genetic information to guide treatment. This session will feature Prof. Vinay Kodipelly, who will share his work in math and statistics and explain how we can use basic statistics to understand health analytics—demonstrating how data-analysis helps researchers spot critical patterns in patient biopsies and treatments.

    View Activity Materials

    Materials for Each Group: 2-3 strawberries, Zip-top plastic bag, Clear plastic cup, Coffee filter, Funnel, Measuring spoon.

    DNA Extraction Supplies: Water, Dish soap, Table salt, Very cold rubbing alcohol.

    Observation & Cleanup: Wooden stir sticks, Magnifying glasses, Disposable gloves, Paper towels.

    Genetic Mutation Challenge: Clue cards, Treatment matching cards, DNA puzzles, Colored beads/pipe cleaners, Student worksheets, Team score sheets.

    Teaching Materials: DNA model, Poster showing cancer treatments, Diagram showing how doctors use genetic info.

  2. Wednesday

    Internal Medicine – Interactive Stations

    Students will rotate through four hands-on internal medicine stations where they act as medical investigators exploring how different body systems function and fail. In the Lung Pressure and Breathing Model, students build a simple lung system and then modify it to simulate conditions like asthma or airway blockage to see how structure affects breathing. In the Kidney Filtration and Dialysis Model, students test how different filters separate "waste" from a blood-like mixture and compare their results to how kidneys clean the body. In the Nervous System Reaction and Stress Test, students measure how quickly their body responds using a ruler drop test and then see how distraction and physical activity change reaction time. In the Blood Clot Rescue Lab, students use a tubing system with a simulated clot to observe blocked blood flow and test different methods to restore circulation. Together, these activities help students understand how internal medicine doctors study and treat problems involving breathing, circulation, kidney function, and the nervous system. Dr. Jaladi, specializing in Internal Medicine, will discuss how doctors diagnose complex medical conditions and care for patients.

    Lung Pressure Model: 1-2 liter plastic bottles, Balloons, Straws, Rubber bands, Modeling clay, Tape, Scissors, Cotton balls.

    Kidney Filtration Model: Clear plastic cups, Water, Food coloring, Rice/beans, Coffee filters, Cheesecloth, Funnels, Spoons, Measuring cups.

    Nervous System Test: 30cm rulers, Stopwatches, Pencils, Data sheets, Simple math worksheets.

    Blood Clot Rescue Lab: Clear flexible tubing, Large syringes, Red food coloring, Water, Sponge pieces, Plastic cups, Clips/clamps, Paper towels.

    General Supplies: Lab worksheets, Markers, Safety goggles, Disinfecting wipes, Trash bags, Name tags.

  3. Friday

    Introduction to Surgery & Anesthesiology

    Students will explore the world of surgery and learn how anesthesiologists help keep patients safe and comfortable during medical procedures. During our hands-on "Banana Surgery" activity, students will make a small incision in a banana and use beginner-friendly surgical tools to practice stitching techniques. This activity introduces basic surgical skills while teaching the importance of teamwork, precision, and patient care. This session will be led by Dr. Anand, an Anesthesiologist.

    View Activity Materials

    Materials for Each Student or Pair: 1 banana, Tweezers, Safety scissors, Large plastic needle, Thick string or yarn (to stitch the banana back up), Disposable gloves, Paper towels.

    Materials for the Instructor: Extra banana for demonstration, Picture or poster showing the members of a surgical team, Examples of surgical tools to show students, Hand sanitizer, Trash bags for cleanup.

Frequently Asked Questions

FAQ

Information for students and families considering Arogya Med Explorers.

Who can attend?

The camp is open to all students entering grades 5–8.

Is there a cost to attend?

No. Arogya Med Explorers is completely free for all participants.

What should I bring?

Students should bring:

  • A notebook
  • A pencil or pen
  • A positive attitude and willingness to learn

Note: First aid kits will be provided.

What harmful materials will we be using?

For the Friday banana suturing activity, students use blunt, child-safe plastic practice needles and yarn — no sharp medical instruments are used at any point.

Is food provided?

Yes. Small snacks will be provided during each camp session.

Do I need prior medical knowledge?

No prior medical knowledge is required. All activities and lessons are designed for students in grades 5–8.

Will there be guest speakers?

Yes. Each camp day will feature a healthcare professional who will discuss their specialty, career path, and experiences in the medical field.

Will there be a feedback form?

Yes, at the end of the camp, we will provide an optional feedback form for students to share what they learned and how the camp helped them. With parent permission, we may feature anonymized student quotes on our portfolios to showcase the impact of our community programs.

How do I contact the organizers?

Please visit the Contact Us page and send an email to the organizers. We will be happy to answer any questions you may have.

When is the registration deadline?

The registration deadline is June 19, 2026.
