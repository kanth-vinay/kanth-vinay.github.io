Understanding the Power Rule for Derivatives

The power rule states that if you have a function of the form:

\( \frac{d}{dx} \) \(x\)\(n\) = \(n\) \(x\)\(n-1\)

Notice how the exponent \(n\) (in red) comes down in front of \( x \), and the new exponent becomes \( n-1 \) (in green).

Example:

\( \frac{d}{dx} \) \(x\)\(5\) = \(5\) \(x\)\(4\)

Try this with different values of \( n \) and see the pattern!
