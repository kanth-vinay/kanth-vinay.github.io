Community STEM Events & Math Bowls

We regularly design and run math and STEM events for local communities—family evenings at libraries, neighborhood competitions, and hands-on labs where students work side by side with their peers, parents, and teachers. These are not shows or assemblies; they are working sessions where participants solve problems, talk through their reasoning, and experience "active learning" together.

Each event is built around a clear mathematical or STEM idea—middle school math bowls, ACT and AP Calculus problem labs, robotics and ABACUS stations, or parent sessions on supporting children in We bring structured activities, ready-to-run materials, and professor-led facilitation, while your organization provides the space and the families. Long-Term Student Development

1-on-1 Academic Coaching

In-home, 1-on-1 mentoring for motivated students in grades 3–12.

For many families, our coaching becomes a years-long apprenticeship rather than a short-term fix. We take on only a small number of students so we can work closely with each one—guiding them not just through coursework, but through the habits, structure, and intellectual maturity that strong STEM preparation requires.

Our coaching is fully personalized and, whenever possible, conducted in-person and in-home for the deepest impact. Students work 1-on-1 with Prof. Vinay Kodipelly, a university mathematics educator, rather than rotating instructors or college students. This brings university-level clarity, structure, and long-term academic strategy into the home environment.

Under this model, students gradually develop the tools they need for advanced coursework, AP readiness, competition problem-solving, and long-term academic confidence. As they mature, many begin building portfolios, independent study habits, and early exposure to fields such as data science and computer graphics—guidance that often continues through high school graduation.

For K–8 learners, this work is led by Sharanya Narra, K–8 Lead Instructor, whose experience spans early mathematics, enrichment pathways, ABACUS training, and foundational problem-solving habits. For high school students, Prof. Vinay Kanth Rao Kodipelly provides direct coaching rooted in his university teaching, curriculum development, and background as a reviewer and evaluator for several mathematics education organizations, as well as his experience supervising math competitions and advanced problem-solving programs.

Because this level of mentorship requires depth, continuity, and trust, we intentionally keep our coaching selective and work with only a small number of students each year. Most families stay with us for several years, often through graduation, as the relationship naturally evolves into an academic apprenticeship.

Students typically progress through a coherent pathway—strong K–8 foundations, algebra readiness, advanced high school coursework, and, for some, competition preparation and portfolio development. Skills are intentionally revisited and compounded over time, allowing students to move from procedural confidence to independent mathematical thinking.

Our goal is not short-term acceleration, but helping students develop habits that support advanced coursework.

ActiveSTEM runs alongside school instruction, increasing practice density and feedback cycles so skills consolidate earlier and compound over time.

Our Story

About Active Learning Institute

Now based in Saint Louis, Missouri, the Active Learning Institute LLC is the U.S. chapter of an initiative that first began in Warangal, India in 2018, founded by Prof. Vinay Kanth Rao Kodipelly and Prof. Sharanya Narra with early support from the Center for Science and Mathematics Education at the University of Southern Mississippi. What started as a grassroots, community-based math enrichment effort has steadily evolved into a multi-layered platform for STEM learning, academic coaching, and educator development.

Instructional decisions are guided by a problem-first approach, structured reasoning, and deliberate opportunities for students to explain, revise, and consolidate their thinking. Technology and AI tools are used selectively—to reduce cognitive overhead and instructional friction—while preserving the human, discussion-driven core of learning. Institute faculty maintain active engagement with national and international mathematics education communities through conference presentations, editorial service, and curriculum development.

"I've witnessed the remarkable impact of Vinay's work... His dedication to hands-on, student-centered math education transforms classrooms... Students not only master essential concepts, but also develop critical thinking and problem-solving skills. I strongly endorse the Active Learning Institute for its unwavering commitment to elevating math education..."

— Dr. Sherry Herron
Director (Emeritus), CSME, Univ. of Southern Mississippi

Prof. Sharanya Narra brought to the institute her deep experience in K–8 mathematics, ABACUS, and early numeracy. Her background working closely with children in both India and the U.S.—including volunteer after-school instruction across Parkway and Rockwood schools—helped shape the institute's philosophy for younger learners: precision, patience, and joyful exploration.

As the initiative expanded, Prof. Vinay Kanth Rao Kodipelly broadened its academic scope with his university-level expertise in mathematics, statistics, data analytics, and math for computer graphics. Over the years, he has presented at several national and international mathematics education conferences, including ICTCM, ITLC, MAA meetings, UConn's Teaching & Learning with Technology Conference, and additional invited events across the U.S. His professional roles include serving as an editorial reviewer for AMTE and being selected as a Project NExT Fellow, along with recognition such as the 2025 FTTC Teaching Excellence Award nomination. These experiences shaped the institute's identity as a serious, research-aligned center that blends academic rigor with innovation.

Rather than offering traditional tutoring, Active Learning Institute operates as a true "math and STEM lab," where students build understanding through active learning, structured practice, problem labs, coding tasks, portfolio building, and competitive experiences. Younger learners encounter strong numeracy foundations and ABACUS-based fluency, while older students engage with advanced topics ranging from AP Calculus to computer graphics mathematics, STEM competitions, teacher-style note-taking, and personal academic portfolio development.

Rather than relying on worksheets or passive tutoring, our instruction emphasizes non-routine problems, visual and computational representations, structured math writing, and discussion-based refinement of ideas.

Whether through in-person coaching, STEM events, online simulations, or teacher professional development, the institute continues to follow the core principle that began in 2018: students learn best by exploring, constructing, discussing, and creating — not by memorizing. From Warangal to St. Louis, that commitment remains unchanged and continues to drive everything ActiveSTEM delivers.

Quality Commitment: Course materials and instructional approaches are regularly reviewed, refined, and piloted before reuse. Feedback from students, parents, and educators is incorporated into revisions, ensuring that programs remain rigorous, clear, and aligned with evolving educational standards.

Global Roots, Local Impact

ActiveSTEM India Chapter

Active Learning Institute's story began in 2018 in Warangal, India, where Prof. Vinay and Prof. Sharanya founded a community-centered math and STEM initiative supported by the Center for Science and Mathematics Education at the University of Southern Mississippi. What started as a local effort to bring high-quality mathematics instruction to students quickly evolved into a movement focused on active learning, hands-on exploration, and deep conceptual understanding.

The India chapter continues to serve students in Warangal and surrounding communities, offering foundational mathematics, ABACUS training, early STEM enrichment, and family engagement programs. The work in India laid the groundwork for the pedagogical approach, community partnerships, and student-centered philosophy that now defines Active Learning Institute LLC in the United States.

From Warangal to St. Louis, the commitment remains the same: students learn best when they explore, discuss, and create together.
